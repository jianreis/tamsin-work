# Glossary

Plain-language definitions of the SA pharmacy, regulatory, funding, dispensing,
digital, consumer, and service-design terms used across the vault. Each term
links to the wiki page where it's treated in depth. Alphabetical within sections.

> Maintained per [[CLAUDE|schema]]. When a new term recurs across ≥2 pages, add it
> here with a one-line definition and a wikilink.

## Regulatory & clinical
- **AES (Advanced Electronic Signature)** — the ECTA-defined signature a truly paperless e-script needs to be the legal equivalent of a wet signature. See [[e-prescription-telehealth-popia]].
- **Dispensing doctor** — a doctor licensed under §22C(1)(a) to dispense the medicines they prescribe. See [[pharmacy-act-sapc-gpp]].
- **ECTA** — Electronic Communications and Transactions Act 25 of 2002; governs electronic signatures/records (basis for e-prescriptions). See [[e-prescription-telehealth-popia]].
- **Generic substitution (§22F)** — a pharmacist substituting a less-expensive interchangeable generic, unless the prescriber/patient refuses or it's not permitted. See [[prescription-requirements-repeats]].
- **GPP (Good Pharmacy Practice)** — the SAPC's mandatory minimum standards for pharmacy practice (premises, dispensing, counselling). See [[pharmacy-act-sapc-gpp]].
- **HPCSA** — Health Professions Council of SA; regulates doctors and sets telehealth rules. See [[e-prescription-telehealth-popia]].
- **Medicines Act** — Medicines and Related Substances Act 101 of 1965; the master statute for medicine scheduling, registration and pricing. See [[medicines-act-scheduling]].
- **Pharmacy Act** — Pharmacy Act 53 of 1974; governs the SAPC, pharmacies and pharmacists. See [[pharmacy-act-sapc-gpp]].
- **PIMART** — Pharmacist-Initiated Management of ART; an expanded-scope pharmacist prescribing qualification (HIV) — legally cleared but operationally stalled as of 2026. See [[clinical-checks-and-mtm]].
- **POPIA** — Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013; treats prescription/health data as "special personal information." See [[e-prescription-telehealth-popia]].
- **Reg 33** — the General Regulations provision setting valid-prescription content requirements. See [[prescription-requirements-repeats]].
- **Repeat dispensing** — supplying further quantities against one prescription; rules vary by schedule (S6 = no repeats; S2–S5 ≤ 6 months). See [[prescription-requirements-repeats]].
- **SAHPRA** — South African Health Products Regulatory Authority; registers medicines, licenses the supply chain, sets schedules. See [[sahpra-overview]].
- **SAPC** — South African Pharmacy Council; registers pharmacies/pharmacists, sets GPP. See [[pharmacy-act-sapc-gpp]].
- **Schedule (S0–S6, +S7/S8)** — the risk classification of a medicine; the "master switch" determining who may sell/prescribe/dispense it and what records apply. See [[medicines-act-scheduling]].
- **Section 22A / 22C / 22F / 22G** — Medicines Act provisions: 22A (scheduling/prescription control), 22C (dispensing & manufacturing licences), 22F (generic substitution), 22G (Single Exit Price). See [[medicines-act-scheduling]], [[sep-dispensing-fee]].
- **SEP (Single Exit Price)** — the single, non-negotiable price at which a manufacturer sells a medicine to everyone; no discounting. See [[sep-dispensing-fee]].
- **SEPA** — the annual SEP Adjustment (max % uplift) gazetted each year (2026 ≈ 1.47%). See [[sep-dispensing-fee]].
- **Dispensing fee** — the regulated, banded professional fee a pharmacy may add on top of SEP. See [[sep-dispensing-fee]].

## Funding (medical schemes)
- **CMS** — Council for Medical Schemes; statutory regulator under the Medical Schemes Act 131 of 1998. See [[cms-medical-schemes-overview]].
- **PMB** — Prescribed Minimum Benefit; the legal floor of cover every scheme must fund (emergencies + ~270 DTPs + 26 CDL conditions). See [[pmb-cdl-chronic]].
- **DTP** — Diagnosis Treatment Pair; a defined PMB condition paired with its defined treatment (~270/271). See [[pmb-cdl-chronic]].
- **CDL** — Chronic Disease List; 26 chronic conditions schemes must fund (meds + consults + tests); requires member registration. See [[pmb-cdl-chronic]].
- **DSP** — Designated Service Provider; scheme's contracted preferred provider; using a non-DSP can trigger a co-payment. See [[dsp-formularies-copayments]].
- **MMAP** — Maximum Medical Aid Price; reference-price model capping reimbursement for chronic & acute medicine. See [[dsp-formularies-copayments]].
- **Formulary** — scheme's list of reimbursed medicines; off-formulary = co-payment or rejection. See [[dsp-formularies-copayments]].
- **Co-payment** — portion the member pays (off-formulary, above-MMAP, or non-DSP). See [[dsp-formularies-copayments]].
- **Switching house / switch** — clearing house (SwitchOn/MediSwitch, MediKredit, Healthbridge) routing claims for real-time adjudication. See [[realtime-claims-switching]].
- **NAPPI** — National Pharmaceutical Product Index; 9-digit unique medicine/product code used to claim. See [[realtime-claims-switching]].
- **ICD-10** — diagnosis coding standard linking medicine to a fundable condition on a claim. See [[realtime-claims-switching]].
- **Open vs restricted scheme** — open = anyone may join; restricted = employer/sector-bound (e.g. GEMS). See [[cms-medical-schemes-overview]].
- **CCMDD** — Central Chronic Medicine Dispensing & Distribution; public-sector pre-pack + pick-up-point chronic-meds programme. See [[courier-chronic-pharmacy-models]].
- **Pelebox** — public-sector smart-locker network for chronic-medicine collection (SMS + PIN, no app). See [[last-mile-addressing-logistics]].
- **Courier pharmacy** — meds-by-mail / chronic delivery model (Pharmacy Direct, Clicks Direct, Medipost, etc.). See [[courier-chronic-pharmacy-models]].
- **NHI** — National Health Insurance; planned single-purchaser fund (Act 2023, paused/in court 2026). See [[nhi-horizon]].
- **Health insurance (vs scheme)** — cheaper primary-care insurance product (e.g. GetSavvi); NOT a medical scheme, no PMBs. See [[cash-uninsured-pathway]].
- **MSA** — Medical Savings Account; member's self-funded day-to-day pot on many scheme options. See [[cash-uninsured-pathway]].

## Dispensing & clinical practice
- **Clinical / therapeutic review** — the pharmacist's check of a prescription against the patient's whole medication record (dose, interactions, contraindications) before dispensing. See [[clinical-checks-and-mtm]].
- **Cold chain** — temperature-controlled handling of fridge-line medicines & vaccines from store to patient. See [[inventory-coldchain-controlled]].
- **DAA (Dose Administration Aid) / adherence packaging** — blister/multi-dose packs organising medicines by date/time to support adherence. See [[adherence-packaging]].
- **DDI (Drug–Drug Interaction)** — a clinically significant interaction screened for during the clinical review. See [[clinical-checks-and-mtm]].
- **FEFO** — First-Expiry-First-Out stock rotation. See [[inventory-coldchain-controlled]].
- **High-alert medicine** — a drug carrying heightened risk of significant harm if used in error (extra checks). See [[error-prevention-and-safety]].
- **LASA / Tall Man lettering** — Look-Alike/Sound-Alike drug names; mixed-case ("predniSONE") used to reduce mix-ups. See [[error-prevention-and-safety]].
- **MTM (Medication Therapy Management)** — structured pharmacist service optimising a patient's medication regimen. See [[clinical-checks-and-mtm]].
- **Near-miss reporting** — non-punitive logging of caught errors to improve safety. See [[error-prevention-and-safety]].
- **Serialisation (GS1)** — unique-identifier coding of medicine packs for traceability. See [[inventory-coldchain-controlled]].
- **Teach-back** — counselling technique where the patient repeats instructions to confirm understanding. See [[counselling-and-health-literacy]].
- **The "rights" of dispensing** — right patient, drug, dose, route, time, documentation (+ counselling). See [[dispensing-process-steps]].

## Digital & technology
- **ADC (Automated Dispensing Cabinet)** — secured automated medicine storage/dispensing unit. See [[pharmacy-automation-and-robotics]].
- **Central fill** — centralised high-volume dispensing site feeding multiple stores/patients. See [[pharmacy-automation-and-robotics]].
- **EHR / EMR** — Electronic Health/Medical Record. See [[interoperability-identity-records]].
- **EMPI** — Enterprise Master Patient Index; matches a patient across systems. See [[interoperability-identity-records]].
- **e-prescribing** — electronic creation/transmission of prescriptions. See [[e-prescribing-systems-and-standards]].
- **FHIR** — HL7 Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources; the modern health-data exchange standard (`MedicationRequest`/`MedicationDispense`). See [[e-prescribing-systems-and-standards]].
- **HNSF / HPRS** — SA's Health Normative Standards Framework / Health Patient Registration System (national digital-health building blocks). See [[interoperability-identity-records]].
- **NCPDP SCRIPT** — the dominant US e-prescribing messaging standard. See [[e-prescribing-systems-and-standards]].
- **Telepharmacy** — remote pharmacist verification/counselling. See [[telepharmacy-and-omnichannel]].
- **Digital therapeutics (DTx)** — software-based therapeutic interventions. See [[adherence-tech-and-digital-therapeutics]].

## Dis-Chem & retail
- **Better Rewards** — Dis-Chem's loyalty programme (relaunched Oct 2025 as instant at-till discounts). See [[dischem-company-overview]].
- **Click-and-collect** — order online, collect in-store (Dis-Chem ~2-hour). See [[dischem-dispensary-services]].
- **DeliverD** — Dis-Chem on-demand delivery (~60-min, selected stores). See [[dischem-dispensary-services]].
- **Dis-Chem Direct** — Dis-Chem's courier/chronic-medicine pharmacy channel. See [[dischem-dispensary-services]].
- **PackMyMeds** — Dis-Chem repeat/chronic pre-pack-and-reminder service. See [[dischem-dispensary-services]].
- **ClubCard** — Clicks' loyalty programme (competitive benchmark). See [[competitive-landscape-sa-pharmacy]].

## Consumer & SA context
- **Spaza** — informal township/neighbourhood retail shop. See [[two-economy-affordability]].
- **Stokvel** — informal rotating savings/credit group. See [[two-economy-affordability]].
- **USSD** — menu-based mobile protocol that works on any phone without data/app. See [[digital-access-and-whatsapp]].
- **Zero-rating** — data-cost-free access to designated services (mostly public-benefit/.gov.za). See [[digital-access-and-whatsapp]].
- **Two-economy** — SA's insured-minority (~16%) vs uninsured-majority (~84%) health split. See [[two-economy-affordability]].
- **Sixty60** — Checkers' rapid (~60-min) grocery delivery app; a key expectation benchmark. See [[adjacent-digital-expectations]].
- **Pargo / PUDO / Paxi** — pickup-point & locker last-mile networks. See [[last-mile-addressing-logistics]].

## Service design
- **Service blueprint** — a map of a service across time AND depth: customer actions, frontstage, backstage and support processes, separated by lines of interaction/visibility/internal interaction. See [[service-blueprinting-method]]; template [[service-blueprint-template]].
- **Customer journey map** — a persona's experience over time (actions, thoughts, emotions, pains, opportunities). See [[journey-mapping-method]]; template [[journey-map-template]].
- **Frontstage / Backstage** — what the customer sees vs the work done out of sight. See [[service-blueprinting-method]].
- **Line of visibility** — the blueprint boundary between frontstage and backstage. See [[service-blueprinting-method]].
- **Line of interaction** — the boundary where the customer touches the service. See [[service-blueprinting-method]].
- **JTBD (Jobs To Be Done)** — framing needs as the "job" a customer hires the service to do. See [[jtbd-and-behavioural-design]]; library [[jtbd-library]].
- **Moment of truth** — a make-or-break interaction that disproportionately shapes perception. See [[moment-of-truth]].
- **COM-B** — Capability–Opportunity–Motivation behaviour model (adherence design). See [[jtbd-and-behavioural-design]].
- **EAST** — Easy, Attractive, Social, Timely behavioural-design framework. See [[jtbd-and-behavioural-design]].
- **Sludge** — friction that obstructs good outcomes (the opposite of a helpful nudge). See [[jtbd-and-behavioural-design]].
- **Persona** — a research-grounded archetype of a user segment. See [[design-kit/personas/_personas-index|personas]].
- **MOC (Map of Content)** — a hand-curated index note (e.g. [[Home]]).
