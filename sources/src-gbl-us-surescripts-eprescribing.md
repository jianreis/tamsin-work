# Summary

How the US transmits prescriptions electronically: a privately-run network
(Surescripts) rather than a government rail, with a regulated 2FA subset (EPCS)
for controlled substances. Contrasts with the UK/Australia state-run models —
intense private feature innovation but a fragmented, no-single-patient-token
system. Network operator (self-reported) + ONC regulator → B/A.

# Key claims
- **Surescripts** runs the dominant US e-prescribing/health-information network
  connecting prescribers, pharmacies, PBMs and EHRs; prescribers transmit scripts
  electronically over it. ~**2.5 billion** electronic prescriptions processed in
  2023 (Surescripts self-reported).
- **EPCS** (Electronic Prescribing of Controlled Substances) is the DEA-regulated
  subset requiring **identity-proofing and two-factor authentication** (anti-fraud/
  diversion, an opioid-crisis response); many states now mandate it. End-2023:
  ~1.29m prescribers e-prescribing, **83.2% also enabled for EPCS**.
- No single government-run patient-facing e-script token in the US — fulfilment UX
  is owned by private players, hence fragmentation alongside rapid feature
  innovation.
