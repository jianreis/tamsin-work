## Summary

CCMDD is the NDoH's public-sector programme that pre-packs chronic medicine
centrally and distributes it to external pick-up points (PuPs) — retail
pharmacies, churches, community sites — and clinic-based points, for clinically
stable patients (notably ARV/HIV and chronic NCD patients). It decongests
clinics and improves access for the uninsured majority. The public-sector mirror
of private courier pharmacy. Documented implementation pain points are directly
relevant UX evidence.

## Key claims

- CCMDD provides **community-based chronic medicine access** for clinically
  stable public-sector patients (HIV/ARV and chronic NCDs); collection at
  **external community pick-up points OR clinic-based points**.
- Purpose: **improve medicine access AND decongest public clinics**.
- **Over 4.9 million patients enrolled** (per programme reporting).
- Medicine is **pre-packed at a central pharmacy** and delivered to **pick-up
  points selected by the DoH** (incl. retail pharmacies and churches).
- Documented implementation challenges: **late delivery, lack of parcel
  tracking, late collection-notification messages, incorrect medication issued,
  too few pick-up points in rural areas, patient-data availability gaps.**

## Verbatim excerpts
