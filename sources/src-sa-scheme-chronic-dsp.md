# Summary

The funding rules that shape how a Dis-Chem prescription is paid and where it
must be dispensed for scheme members: **PMBs**, **Chronic Disease List (CDL)**,
**Designated Service Providers (DSPs)** and **co-payments**. CMS is the Tier-A
regulator; scheme guidance corroborates. Anchors the Fund/Claim stage of the
as-is journey. (Detailed synthesis belongs in wiki/medical-schemes — this note
captures the journey-relevant essentials.)

# Key claims
- A **DSP** is a scheme's designated provider (pharmacy/doctor/hospital) for PMB
  conditions; using a non-DSP voluntarily can trigger a **co-payment** (the
  difference vs DSP cost, or a registered % co-pay).
- For chronic medicine, members are typically steered to a **courier pharmacy**
  and/or a **network pharmacy** to avoid co-payments.
- A member can usually choose a courier pharmacy **or** a network pharmacy within
  **~10km** of home/work, and must **stay with that pharmacy for ~6 months**.
- Chronic cover usually requires **registration of the condition** with the
  scheme before claims are paid from the chronic benefit.
- Real-time claim adjudication ("switching", e.g. via Mediswitch/healthbridge) at
  the dispensary determines on-the-spot what the scheme pays vs the member's
  co-payment — central to the in-store Pay stage.

# Verbatim excerpts
> "A Designated Service Provider (DSP) is a healthcare provider … that is a
> medical scheme's first choice when its members need … a Prescribed Minimum
> Benefit."
