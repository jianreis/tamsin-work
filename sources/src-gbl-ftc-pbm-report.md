# Summary

US regulator's findings on pharmacy benefit managers (PBMs). Documents the
extreme concentration and vertical integration of the US drug-payment middle
layer — the structural feature that most distorts US pharmacy pricing and that
South Africa does **not** share. Useful as the canonical "what NOT to import"
reference. FTC = regulator → reliability A.

# Key claims
- The **top three PBMs** — Caremark (CVS Health), Express Scripts (Cigna),
  OptumRx (UnitedHealth) — processed **~80% of the ~6.6 billion prescriptions**
  dispensed by US pharmacies in 2023; the top six processed **>90%**.
- The largest PBMs are **vertically integrated** with the largest health
  insurers and with specialty/retail pharmacies (e.g. CVS Health = Caremark +
  Aetna + CVS pharmacies), letting them set formularies, steer patients to their
  own pharmacies, and retain rebate spread.
- FTC's second interim report (Jan 2025) found the Big 3 marked up many
  **specialty generic drugs by hundreds-to-thousands of percent**, generating
  **>$7.3 billion** above estimated acquisition cost, 2017–2022.
- FTC filed an administrative **complaint (2024-09-20)** alleging the three PBMs
  "artificially inflated" insulin list prices via rebate practices.
