# Summary

Two government design canons: the **GOV.UK 10 design principles** (and the
14-point Service Standard) and the **NHS design principles / NHS service
standard** (the GOV.UK standard plus extra health-specific points). The most
authoritative public-sector guidance on inclusive, needs-led, plain-language
service design — directly transferable to a public-facing pharmacy service.

# Key claims
- **GOV.UK 10 design principles:** (1) Start with user needs; (2) Do less;
  (3) Design with data; (4) Do the hard work to make it simple; (5) Iterate.
  Then iterate again; (6) **This is for everyone** — accessible, inclusive
  design; (7) Understand context; (8) Build digital services, not websites;
  (9) Be consistent, not uniform; (10) Make things open: it makes things better.
- **GOV.UK Service Standard** = 14 points in three groups (understand users &
  their needs; provide a joined-up, accessible service; use the right
  technology), incl. "make sure everyone can use the service" and "test from
  beginning to end".
- **NHS design principles** include: design things that **understand and respect
  people's needs** (the *whole person* — emotional, physical, and technical
  needs); **NHS services are for everyone** (people with different physical,
  mental-health, social, cultural or learning needs must be able to use them);
  put **plain language** and accessibility first; design for the context of use
  (stress, low confidence, shared/borrowed devices).
- **NHS service standard** = the 14 GOV.UK points plus 3 extra health/social-care
  points (e.g. make the service safe and high quality; consider impact on the
  whole health/care pathway).
- "**Do the hard work to make it simple**" — complexity is moved away from the
  user and absorbed by the service/organisation.
- Accessibility is a baseline (UK public-sector regs target **WCAG 2.x AA**), not
  an add-on.
