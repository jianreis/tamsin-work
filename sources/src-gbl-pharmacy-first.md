# Summary

England's January 2024 service letting community pharmacists assess and supply
prescription-only medicines for seven common conditions under Patient Group
Directions — without a GP appointment. A model for using the pharmacy as a
clinical front door and easing GP load. NHS/DHSC official + academic eval → A
(milestone counts via reputable press/preprint).

# Key claims
- Launched **31 January 2024**, funded within a **£645m** primary-care access
  plan. Pharmacists follow a **defined clinical pathway** (inclusion/exclusion
  criteria, safety-netting) and may supply restricted POMs under **Patient Group
  Directions (PGDs)** for: **sinusitis (12+), sore throat (5+), acute otitis
  media/earache (1–17, in person), infected insect bites (1+), impetigo (1+),
  shingles (18+), uncomplicated UTI in women (16–64)**.
- Access by **walk-in / direct contact** (no referral) or referral from GP/NHS
  111. **Remote consultation allowed for six** of seven conditions; **otitis
  media requires in-person** examination.
- Outcomes: OpenSAFELY first-year study (Jan 2024–Jan 2025) recorded **402,165
  consultations for 340,710 patients**; most common acute pharyngitis (28.9%) and
  UTI (28%). Press reported **~5.4 million** total Pharmacy First consultations by
  end-Feb 2025 (~8% of the population), though **uptake ran below NHS targets**.
