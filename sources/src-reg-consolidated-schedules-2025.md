# Consolidated Schedules (01 August 2025)

## Summary
SAHPRA's consolidated, periodically-updated schedule list (Gazette) that assigns
each substance to Schedule 0–8. Substances move between schedules by amendment
(call-up notices). This is the operative reference for "what schedule is X."

> corroborated across the SAHPRA Scheduling Guideline (SAHPGL-CEM-NS-02) and
> multiple secondary explainers.

## Key claims
- S0: may be sold in any outlet (supermarket/general dealer), may be advertised to
  the public (e.g. low-dose paracetamol, some antacids/antiseptics).
- S1 & S2: pharmacist-initiated — sold only from a pharmacy, must be handed over
  by a pharmacist (or under supervision); no doctor's prescription needed. S2 is
  recorded (name + medicine + quantity). Only S1 may be advertised to public.
- S3–S6: prescription-only medicines.
- S3 & S4: chronic/serious conditions (e.g. hypertension, diabetes, infections);
  repeats restricted to six months.
- S5: stronger control (e.g. some sedatives, certain controlled analgesics).
- S6: moderately-to-highly addictive (e.g. opioid analgesics); no repeats, 30-day
  maximum supply, strict register.
- S7: no recognised medicinal use except scientific study; use prohibited (permit only).
- S8: very high dependence risk but some proven value (in SA: amphetamine,
  dexamphetamine, nabilone); available only via Director-General special permission
  to a named medical practitioner.

## Verbatim excerpts
- "Schedule 7 substances ... aren't recognised for medicinal use apart from
  scientific study ... their use is prohibited."
- S8 substances are "available by the Director-General to medical practitioners who
  have obtained special permission."
