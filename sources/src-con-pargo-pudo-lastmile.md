## Summary

Pickup-point and locker networks (click-and-collect) exist precisely because
home delivery is hard where addresses are incomplete or informal. Establishes the
scale of the alternative-collection infrastructure SA consumers already use, and
quantifies the addressing problem. Company/press sources → C; figures are
self-reported network scale.

## Key claims

- **>21% of South Africans live in informal settlements** and **>35.7% in rural
  areas** — "inconclusive addresses and subpar infrastructure make home delivery
  impossible" for a large share (Pargo).
- **Pargo**: >2,500 pickup points covering **>87% of SA postal codes**; explicitly
  targets informal settlements (e.g. Silulo Ulutho partnership).
- **PUDO** (The Courier Guy): launched 2020; **~1,500 smart lockers** nationwide;
  send/collect parcels up to 20kg.
- Other networks: **Paxi** (Pep stores, ~2,000+ points), **PostNet**, retailer
  click-and-collect. Pickup-at-a-known-store is the dominant work-around to the
  addressing gap.
