## Summary

FinScope is the standard SA financial-inclusion survey. Establishes that formal
banking is now near-universal by access but that affordability stress, cash
preference, and reliance on informal mechanisms persist — central to payment
design for a low-income majority.

## Key claims

- **Financial exclusion fell from 51% (2014) to ~12% (2023)**, largely via mobile
  money and low-cost digital banks; but ~**19% of residents are unbanked** by some
  measures and **~10% see a bank account as too expensive** (FinScope).
- **40% of adults borrow money for food**; **~20 million adults** experienced
  electricity cuts they couldn't afford in the past year — deep affordability
  stress despite "inclusion".
- ~**12 million adults struggle with debt** and rely on credit to cope.
- High formal-access numbers mask heavy **cash use** for daily spend and continued
  reliance on **informal savings (stokvels)**.
