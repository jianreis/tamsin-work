# Summary

The UK's distance-selling (online) pharmacy model built on EPS nomination, and a
cautionary trust lesson. Pharmacy2U/LloydsDirect set themselves as the patient's
nominated pharmacy, auto-request repeats from the GP, dispense centrally, and
post for free. Includes the 2015 ICO fine for selling patient data — a direct
trust/POPIA warning. Reputable trade press → B (company-stated volumes flagged).

# Key claims
- **Mechanism:** patient registers and sets the online pharmacy as **EPS-
  nominated**; the app reminds and **requests the repeat from the GP** (approved
  via EPS); dispensed centrally; **free Royal Mail delivery** home.
- Pharmacy2U is the UK's largest distance-selling pharmacy; dispenses ~**1.6
  million NHS items/month to >750,000 patients** (company-stated, ~2023–24).
- **LloydsDirect** began as **Echo (2015)**, acquired by McKesson (2019),
  rebranded LloydsDirect (2021). **Pharmacy2U acquired LloydsDirect on 9 October
  2023**; combined business runs under the Pharmacy2U brand. (Lloyds closed its
  retail community pharmacies in 2023.)
- **Trust lesson:** in **October 2015 the ICO fined Pharmacy2U £130,000** — its
  first such penalty — for selling names/addresses of **>20,000 customers**
  without consent (data segmented by condition; buyers included a lottery scam).
