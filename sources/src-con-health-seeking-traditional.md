## Summary

National-household-survey evidence on where South Africans first seek care. Debunks
the popular "80% use traditional healers" myth: the large majority use **public
health facilities first**, traditional/faith healers are a minority and often
complementary, not substitutive. Important for grounding trust and first-contact
design without overstating the traditional-healer channel.

## Key claims

- **~70–81% of (black) SA households consult public-sector facilities first**;
  ~17% private sector first; only **~1.5% "other" (traditional/spiritual) first**
  (2011 national data).
- Earlier surveys: only **1.2% reported using a traditional healer** (2008 national
  survey); **5.2% traditional / 6% faith healer** in the 2003 SADHS.
- Africa Check: the claim that **80% of South Africans regularly consult
  traditional healers is unproven/false**.
- Traditional healers retain cultural salience (continuity of care, belief in
  efficacy) and are used **alongside** biomedical care, especially for chronic /
  HIV / mental-health concerns — relevant to disclosure and adherence.
