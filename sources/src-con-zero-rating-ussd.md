## Summary

Reporting on ICASA's zero-rating obligation (effective 15 Jan 2024) requiring
operators to zero-rate approved public-benefit content, plus context on USSD as
the low-end access channel and the limits of price-only interventions. C-tier
reporting of an A-tier regulation — the underlying ICASA rule is authoritative.

## Key claims

- From **15 Jan 2024**, SA mobile operators have **36 months** to zero-rate
  content from **approved public-benefit organisations** (NPOs, trusts, `.gov.za`
  government departments). Once ICASA-approved, content must be zero-rated within
  **14 business days**. Primarily benefits less-affluent users.
- **USSD** ("dial-string" menus, e.g. `*120#`) works on **any phone including
  feature phones**, needs **no data or app**, and accommodates low digital
  literacy — the backbone of basic mobile banking/airtime for the low end.
- Price cuts alone don't fix digital inequality: **device cost and digital
  literacy** remain barriers once connected (The Conversation / Research ICT
  Africa).

## Verbatim excerpts

> "South African mobile network operators had 36 months starting 15 January 2024
> to fulfil zero-rating obligations … content from approved public benefit

> "USSD banking provides essential services through any mobile phone for those

> "Reducing data prices without attending to the cost of devices and other
