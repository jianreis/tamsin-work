## Summary

Load-shedding (rolling blackouts) is the SA resilience constraint that breaks
both digital access and cold chain. Even with 2024's reprieve, it remains an
ever-present planning assumption: payments, connectivity, refrigerated stock, and
locker/clinic power can all fail simultaneously on a schedule. Improved markedly
in 2024 but not eliminated; risk recurs.

## Key claims

- **2023: ~335 days with load-shedding** (the worst year); **2024 moderated to
  ~83 days**. Stage 4 most common in 2023 (~80 days); Stage 6 = **4–6 hours
  without power per outage**.
- **Mobile networks**: outages force operators to divert capex; **tower batteries
  lose efficacy beyond ~Stage 4**, so connectivity drops during heavy stages —
  exactly when people are home and trying to transact.
- **Cold chain**: blackouts spoil temperature-sensitive stock — Pick n Pay cited a
  **~R200m loss (2023)** to spoilage; generators cost a major retailer **>R500m**.
  Direct relevance to cold-chain medicines (insulin, vaccines, biologics).
- Recurrence risk means digital health/pharmacy services need **offline-tolerant**
  flows and **cold-chain-resilient** fulfilment.
