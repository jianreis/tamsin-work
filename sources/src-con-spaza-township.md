## Summary

The township/informal-retail economy is large, cash-based, and physically close
to low-income consumers. Spaza shops are the everyday-purchase default for
millions — a benchmark for proximity, trust, and cash convenience that a formal
pharmacy network does not match in townships. Market-size figures vary widely by
source/method (C-tier reporting of Trade Intelligence estimates).

## Key claims

- Trade Intelligence estimates the **spaza market ~R197 billion (2023)**, with
  **~11.1 million people regularly shopping** in **~150,000 stores**.
- The broader **township economy is estimated ~R900 billion**, informal retail the
  largest component (some headlines cite "R900bn spaza market" loosely).
- Spaza = isiZulu "hidden/improvised"; community hubs born under apartheid-era
  exclusion; **cash-dominant**, walking-distance, extend informal credit.
- **Nov 2024**: Ramaphosa mandated spaza/food-handling registration with
  municipalities (regulatory formalisation push).
