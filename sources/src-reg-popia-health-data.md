# POPIA and health data

## Summary
POPIA governs processing of personal information in SA. Health information is
"special personal information" (s26–s27): processing is prohibited unless an
exemption applies — including processing by/for health professionals for treatment,
or with the data subject's consent. Enforced by the Information Regulator;
full commencement 1 July 2021. New health-data regulations under discussion (2026).

> Note: corroborated across POPIA text + Webber Wentzel / Fasken / Werksmans
> commentary (Tier B). Effective-date and "special information" classification are
> Tier-A statutory facts.

## Key claims
- Health information is "special personal information"; its processing is prohibited
  (s26) unless a s27 ground applies — notably processing by medical professionals/
  institutions for the provision of healthcare, or data-subject consent.
- The 8 conditions for lawful processing apply: accountability, processing
  limitation, purpose specification, further-processing limitation, information
  quality, openness, security safeguards, data-subject participation.
- Security safeguards (s19) and breach notification (s22) are mandatory; a
  responsible party must secure integrity/confidentiality of health records.
- Consent must be voluntary, specific and informed; broad/blanket consent is
  insufficient.
- Fasken (Apr 2026): new POPIA regulations on processing of health information are
  in progress — tighter rules expected. [In flux.]

## Verbatim excerpts
- "POPIA establishes a prohibition on processing health and sex life information
  unless a justification exists ... consent ... must be an informed, voluntary
  expression of will that is specific and cannot be overly generalised."
- "POPIA permits processing of health information by medical professionals,
  healthcare institutions or facilities ... where such role players are providing
  healthcare services."
