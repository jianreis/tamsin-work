## Summary

Peer-reviewed evidence on what drives and blocks chronic-medicine adherence in
SA, and on the mental-health stigma/treatment gap. Adherence barriers are
overwhelmingly **system- and stigma-related** (queues, travel cost, disclosure
fear, stock-outs), not patient indifference — directly informing the design of a
respectful, low-friction, private scripting experience.

## Key claims

- ART-adherence barriers: **long clinic waiting times**, lack of confidentiality,
  travel cost/distance, stock-outs/poor follow-up; socially, **fear of unintended
  HIV-status disclosure and stigma**.
- Decentralised collection (CCMDD-type) **reduces waiting times, improves
  convenience and refill hours, and reduces stigma** — patients value it.
- **SMS reminders** can help adherence but real-world gains are modest and
  implementation is fragile (wrong refill dates, poor clinic↔PUP communication,
  date inflexibility).
- **Mental health**: ~**75% treatment gap** for common mental disorders; depression
  & anxiety each ~**4–5%** of adults; lifetime any-disorder ~**30%**. Stigma + cost
  + under-resourced public services keep most untreated.
