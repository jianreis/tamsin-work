# Summary

China's health super-app model: AI triage → online consultation in a licensed
"internet hospital" → legally valid e-prescription (electronic signature +
pharmacist review) → fast medicine delivery, increasingly with medical-insurance
settlement online. The structural driver is "medicine separated from prescription"
reform pushing prescriptions out of hospitals ("prescription outflow"). Company
filings/releases + reputable press + academic → B (user counts are company-reported).

# Key claims
- **Internet-hospital flow (regulated):** rules issued **14 Sept 2018** (NHC) require
  an internet hospital to be **based on a licensed physical hospital**; online
  prescriptions are valid only with **real-name doctor authentication, the
  doctor's electronic signature, and pharmacist review**, after which a third party
  may deliver the medicine. **No first visit online** — online diagnosis/prescribing
  is restricted to **follow-up/revisit care for common and chronic diseases** with a
  prior offline diagnosis.
- **Ping An Health:** in-app **AI triage / "AI Doctor"** → consultation (in-house +
  contracted external doctors) → prescription → one-hour drug delivery from partner
  pharmacies; "Healthcare + Medicine + Insurance" / "HMO + family doctor + O2O"
  model linked to Ping An's insurance. ~400m registered users; ~20m family-doctor
  members; first full-year profit FY2024 (revenue RMB 4.81bn, adj. net profit RMB
  158m; announced 12 Mar 2025).
- **Alibaba Health (AliHealth):** Tmall/Alipay marketplace + self-operated pharmacy
  + "cloud hospital" e-prescription; **O2O same-day/30-minute delivery via Ele.me +
  local pharmacies**; drug-traceability lineage. FYE 31 Mar 2025 revenue RMB 30.6bn
  (+13.2%), net profit RMB 1.43bn (+62.2%).
- **JD Health:** largest online healthcare platform in China by revenue; key
  differentiator is **self-operated (1P) inventory + JD logistics** (~19 pharma
  warehouses, 490+ cities, 150,000+ cooperative pharmacies); internet hospital with
  e-prescription + **online medical-insurance payment**. ~183m annual active users
  (end-2024); >480,000 daily online consultations (mid-2024); FY2024 revenue RMB
  58.16bn (+8.6%).
- **System reforms:** "medicine separated from prescription" (zero-markup, 2017) →
  prescription outflow to retail/online; **Dual-Channel policy (2021)** extended
  insurance to designated outpatient pharmacies; by **July 2023, 26 provinces** ran
  medical-insurance e-prescription circulation centres (~1.02m institutions, 66,300
  pharmacies). **2022 tightening** banned **AI from auto-generating prescriptions**
  and enforced "prescription first, drug second".
