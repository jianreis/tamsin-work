## Summary

SA-context evidence on automation uptake in pharmacy. Automated dispensing units
(e.g. **Mach4 Pharmacy Dispensing Units**) have been adopted "progressively" in
the SA pharmaceutical industry to augment/substitute unit-dose distribution,
improve medication availability and productivity, and reduce dispensing errors.
Larger retail and hospital operators have introduced automated dispensing in
high-volume sites to lift throughput and cut wait times. A LMIC tertiary-hospital
case study (cited alongside) reports robotic central-pharmacy automation improving
management with few dispensing errors and tighter stock control/anti-diversion.
Signals that the technology is operable in SA, but adoption is uneven and
concentrated in higher-volume/well-capitalised settings. ⚠️ Specific named SA
retail-chain robot deployments (Dis-Chem/Clicks) are asserted in secondary
coverage and need primary confirmation.

## Key claims
- Automated dispensing units (e.g. **Mach4 PDUs**) adopted progressively in SA to
  improve availability, productivity and reduce dispensing errors.
- High-volume SA pharmacies have introduced automated dispensing to boost
  throughput and reduce wait times.
- Robotic central-pharmacy automation (LMIC hospital case) improved management with
  few errors and better anti-diversion/stock tracking.
- ⚠️ UNVERIFIED: specific named Dis-Chem/Clicks robotic deployments — secondary
  claims, confirm against primary disclosures.
