# SAPC Rules: Good Pharmacy Practice (GPP)

## Summary
SAPC's binding GPP rules/manual setting minimum professional standards for
premises, services, human resources and management of a pharmacy — including the
dispensing process, patient counselling/information, record-keeping, and cold-chain
storage. Updated periodically by Board Notice (e.g. BN70/2021, BN480/2023).

> Note: PDF returned 403; content triangulated from SAPC/UCT GPP extracts.

## Key claims
- GPP applies to every registered pharmacist, support person, and licensed pharmacy
  owner; standards cover premises, services, human resources and management.
- Dispensing must be performed by or under the personal supervision of a pharmacist;
  counselling on safe/effective use is a GPP requirement.
- Thermolabile products must be identified on receipt, stored per written
  instructions, with temperatures monitored and recorded twice daily; controlled
  temperature storage areas must have temperature recorders maintaining the whole
  area within range (cold-chain rule).
- RP and pharmacy owner are jointly responsible for meeting GPP standards;
  non-compliance can lead to enforcement and loss of recording/licence.

## Verbatim excerpts
- "All practising pharmacists are obliged to ensure that the service they provide
  is of high quality and complies with Good Pharmacy Practice Standards as
  published by Council in rules."
- "Thermolabile pharmaceutical products require controlled temperature storage and
  must be identified on receipt and be stored in accordance with written
  instructions, with temperatures monitored and recorded twice daily."
