# Summary

The foundational patient-experience framework: Picker's **eight principles of
person-centred care** (from *Through the Patient's Eyes*, 1993) and the IOM's
definition of patient-centred care. Defines what a "good" health experience must
deliver from the patient's point of view — the lens against which a pharmacy
journey should be evaluated.

# Key claims
- **IOM definition:** patient-centred care is "providing care that is respectful
  of, and responsive to, individual patient preferences, needs and values, and
  ensuring that patient values guide all clinical decisions." (Crossing the
  Quality Chasm, 2001 — one of six aims for quality care.)
- **Picker's eight principles of person-centred care:**
  1. **Respect for patient preferences** (values, autonomy, dignity).
  2. **Coordination and integration of care** (clinical, ancillary, support).
  3. **Information and education** (about condition, prognosis, process).
  4. **Physical comfort** (incl. pain management, environment).
  5. **Emotional support** (relieving fear and anxiety).
  6. **Involvement of family and friends** (caregivers/proxies).
  7. **Continuity and transition** (between settings; clear what-happens-next).
  8. **Access to care** (ability to get the care/medicine when needed).
- These dimensions are derived directly from patient and caregiver focus-group
  research, not provider assumptions.
- Beryl Institute's Experience Framework similarly frames experience as eight
  integrated strategic areas (culture/leadership, infrastructure, patient/family
  partnership, etc.) — complementary, organisation-facing view.
