## Summary

The Council for Medical Schemes (CMS) is the statutory regulator of medical
schemes in South Africa, established under the Medical Schemes Act 131 of 1998.
Its annual Industry Report is the authoritative dataset on scheme numbers,
beneficiaries, administrators and finances. The 2024 figures (published 2024/25)
describe a stable but slow-growing, ageing industry of ~9.1m beneficiaries.

Note: the CMS site and PMG PDF mirror returned HTTP 403 to automated fetches on

## Key claims

- In 2024/25 there were **71 medical schemes** — **16 open** and **55 restricted**
  — serving **more than 9.1 million beneficiaries**, via **33 administrators**,
  43 managed care organisations and 10,000+ brokers/brokerages.
- The industry covered **9.17 million beneficiaries in 2024**, a marginal
  **+0.45%** increase on 2023.
- Average beneficiary age rose from **32 (2023) to 34.2 (2024)**; open schemes
  skew older (36.4) than restricted (31.8) — a gradually ageing risk pool.
- By 2024, **open schemes = 56.1% of principal members**; restricted = 43.9%
  (restricted share rising).
- The industry is becoming bigger, older and more concentrated.
