# SAHPRA mandate, registration and licensing

## Summary
SAHPRA (established under the Medicines Act, operational from 2018, replacing the
Medicines Control Council) is the regulator that registers all health products and
licenses manufacturers, wholesalers, distributors and importers, and the
establishments/persons who dispense. No health product may be sold in SA without
SAHPRA registration; §22C licences govern dispensing and the supply chain.

## Key claims
- Mandate: regulate health products (medicines, medical devices, IVDs,
  complementary/biological medicines) for quality, safety and efficacy; register
  medicines (§14); license establishments (§22C(1)(b)).
- "No health product ... can be legally marketed, distributed, or sold in South
  Africa without prior authorisation (registration) from SAHPRA."
- The Pharma Licensing sub-unit issues licences to manufacturers/wholesalers/
  distributors/importers on GMP/GWDP conditions; §22C(1)(a) dispensing licences
  (incl. dispensing doctors) are processed via the DoH Director-General with SAPC
  course accreditation.
- Online selling: SAHPRA's position is that only medicines registered with SAHPRA
  may be sold, and only via websites linked to a DoH-licensed, SAPC-recorded
  pharmacy. Unregistered online "pharmacies" selling S3+ without a prescription are
  illegal; SAHPRA/SAPC have taken enforcement action (e.g. compounded GLP-1, 2026).

## Verbatim excerpts
- "No health product – whether a medicine, medical device, IVD, or complementary
  medicine – can be legally marketed, distributed, or sold in South Africa without
  prior authorisation (registration) from SAHPRA."
- 2024 study: "None of these [unregistered] websites requested that a prescription
  be provided upon ordering, even if a medicine ... is registered as a Schedule 3
  or above."
