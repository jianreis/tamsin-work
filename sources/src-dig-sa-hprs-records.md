## Summary

SA-context evidence on records, identity and NHI digital ambitions. The **HPRS**
(from 2014) is the national patient registration system / master patient index,
keyed on the SA ID number (and passports), intended to track patients across
facilities and underpin the NHI EHR. NHI is positioned to drive digital-health
investment. But progress is partial: EHR diagnostic/treatment/billing modules
remain undeveloped; non-compliant and paper-based record-keeping persists (with
judicial criticism), and systems integration/fragmentation across disease programmes
and provinces is a known weakness. Unique patient identification and case-based
surveillance are still maturing across the SADC region. Implication: a unified
patient profile and reliable e-Rx exchange in SA face identity-matching,
fragmentation and capacity constraints, not just technology gaps.

## Key claims
- **HPRS** (2014–) = national patient registry / MPI on SA ID number (+ passports),
  to track patients across facilities; central to NHI EHR.
- NHI positioned as a driver of SA digital-health investment.
- EHR diagnostic/treatment/billing modules for NHI **not yet developed**.
- Non-compliant / paper record-keeping persists in SA, drawing judicial responses;
  digital remedy still in progress.
- Systems integration & fragmentation across programmes/provinces is a known
  weakness; unique patient ID and case-based surveillance still maturing (SADC).
