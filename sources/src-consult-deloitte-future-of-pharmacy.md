# Summary

Deloitte's "Future of Pharmacy" / "Future of pharmacists" pieces forecast retail
pharmacies becoming **consolidated health destinations**, with automation/AI
absorbing dispensing mechanics so **pharmacists become front-line clinical
providers** (managing chronic disease, limited prescribing). A clear, citable
"pharmacy of the future" POV. Reliability **B**. Futures claims ⚠️ UNVERIFIED (403).

# Key claims
- Retail pharmacies "could become **consolidated health destinations**" with
  distribution reshaped by 3D printing, kiosks, telehealth and same-day delivery
  (drones/bots/driverless) — a futures vision.
- **Automation + AI** enhance (not replace) pharmacists, letting them become
  recognised **care providers** who "treat patients with acute illnesses and manage
  chronic conditions like diabetes, hypertension, and asthma."
- Community pharmacies are **losing share** to online/digital disruptors;
  **reorienting toward clinical services** is the defensible revenue + footfall play.
- Pharmacists today spend disproportionate time "**counting pills and addressing
  clinical edits**" rather than operating at the top of their licence — the time
  reallocation thesis (shared with McKinsey/Oliver Wyman).
