# SAPC — electronic transmission of prescriptions

## Summary
SAPC's own guidance to pharmacists on handling electronically transmitted and
electronic prescriptions, and the pharmacist's duty to verify authenticity. The
regulator's view confirms e-prescriptions are permissible under Reg 33 (2017).

> Note: page returned 403 to fetch; claims from search extraction of SAPC/Pharmaciae.

## Key claims
- Reg 33 (2017 General Regulations) allows electronic signatures and electronic
  transmission of prescriptions.
- The pharmacist must verify the authenticity of the prescription regardless of
  format (paper, electronic, faxed, verbal).
- For non-electronic-agent transmissions (fax, scanned, verbal), a permanent record
  copy must be retained; verbal scripts require a signed prescription within 7 days.
- The S5/S6 "dilemma": SAPC/SAPJ have flagged that faxed/emailed scripts for S5 and
  S6 raise authenticity/control concerns — these schedules attract the strictest
  handling.

## Verbatim excerpts
- "Regulation 33 of the General Regulations (2017) ... allows for electronic
  signatures and the electronic transmission of prescriptions ... the pharmacist
  must verify the authenticity of the prescription, irrespective of what format the
  prescription is in."
