## Summary

NCPDP SCRIPT is the de-facto transactional standard for transmitting prescriptions
electronically between prescribers and pharmacies in the United States (new Rx,
refill request/response, change, cancel, RxFill, medication history, electronic
prior authorisation, controlled-substance e-prescribing/EPCS). CMS named SCRIPT
in its Medicare Part D e-prescribing rules; the 17 June 2024 final rule
(CMS-4205-F2) adopts **SCRIPT version 2023011** as the required standard from
**1 January 2028**, with a transition period during which both old and new
versions may be used. NCPDP also stewards complementary standards (Telecom for
claims billing, RxNorm-aligned drug identifiers).

## Key claims
- **NCPDP SCRIPT** is the named US standard for electronically transmitting
  prescriptions, prescription-related information, medication history and
  electronic prior authorisation (ePA).
- CMS final rule **CMS-4205-F2** (17 Jun 2024) requires SCRIPT **v2023011** from
  **1 Jan 2028**; transition period allows both versions until then.
- v2023011 adds extensibility, REMS observation elements, dental procedure codes,
  patient gender/pronouns, patient conditions, and pharmacy-initiated transfer of
  electronic controlled-substance prescriptions.
- SCRIPT supports the full prescription lifecycle: NewRx, RefillRequest/Response,
  RxChangeRequest/Response, CancelRx, RxFill, medication history, EPCS.
