## Summary

Research ICT Africa's RAMP index tracks prepaid mobile-data prices across Africa.
The authoritative independent SA data-cost tracker. Establishes that data is a
material, rationed household cost for low-income users and that prices, though
declined ~88% over a decade, may be entering an upward cycle. Triangulated with
StatRanker/Statista price points.

## Key claims

- Research ICT Africa tracks **seven prepaid bundles (100MB–10GB)**; by Dec 2023
  the **median price/GB had declined ~88%** over the tracked period.
- Prices may be **entering a fourth cycle of increases** in the short-to-medium
  term, bucking the long decline.
- SA pays **more for data than 27 other African countries** (RAMP, reported 2026).
- StatRanker/Statista price points: avg **1GB ≈ R20.50 (2025)**; for prepaid
  customers **1GB fell from ~R100 (2020) to ~R79 (2025)** — i.e. small bundles
  remain expensive per-GB; the poor pay the highest unit rates ("poverty premium").
- MTN advertises entry data plans "for as little as R20" — small bundles dominate
  prepaid purchasing.
