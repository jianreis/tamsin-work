# Pharmacy Act 53 of 1974 (as amended)

## Summary
Establishes the South African Pharmacy Council (SAPC) and governs registration of
pharmacists, pharmacist's assistants, pharmacy support personnel, and the
registration/recording and ownership/licensing of pharmacies. §35A empowers SAPC
to make rules, including the obligatory Good Pharmacy Practice (GPP) standards.

## Key claims
- SAPC regulates the practice of pharmacy in terms of the Act; it registers
  pharmacists, interns, pharmacist's assistants and pharmacy support personnel.
- A pharmacy must be recorded with SAPC and licensed by the National Department of
  Health (Director-General) under the Regulations Relating to the Ownership and
  Licensing of Pharmacies (and §22 / §22A of the Pharmacy Act).
- Every pharmacy must have a Responsible Pharmacist (RP); a pharmacy operating
  without a pharmacist risks losing its SAPC recording and DoH licence.
- §35A makes GPP obligatory; SAPC publishes GPP rules as binding standards.
- Pharmacist's assistant categories: learner basic, basic, learner post-basic,
  post-basic. Learners and basics act under the DIRECT personal supervision of a
  pharmacist; post-basic may act under indirect supervision (regulation 12).
- A pharmacist may not have more than three pharmacist's assistants under direct
  personal supervision, nor more than five post-basic assistants under indirect
  supervision, at one time.

## Verbatim excerpts
- "Good Pharmacy Practice is obligatory in terms of section 35A of the Pharmacy Act
  53 of 1974, regulation 20(1) of the Regulations Relating to the Practice of
  Pharmacy, and regulation 7(a) of the Regulations Relating to the Ownership and
  Licensing of Pharmacies."
- "A pharmacist's assistant (learner basic) ... may provide the services or perform
  acts prescribed in regulation 9 under the direct personal supervision of a
  pharmacist in a pharmacy."
- "[A pharmacist may not] have under his or her direct personal supervision more
  than three pharmacists' assistants in a pharmacy, or ... more than five
  pharmacists' assistants (post-basic) under his or her indirect personal supervision."
