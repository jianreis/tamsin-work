# Summary

Evidence for the South-Africa-specific constraints that shape inclusive design:
**expensive mobile data**, **prepaid (pay-as-you-go) dominance**, and the world's
widest **urban–rural connectivity gap**. The factual basis for low-data /
zero-rated / feature-phone-friendly design choices in the scripting service.

> Note: corroborated by ICASA's State of the ICT Sector Report (2024) and
> Cable.co.uk price data cited in the Freedom House report.

# Key claims
- Mobile data is **costly relative to income**: ~**R34.69 (~US$1.81) per GB**
  (2023, Cable data); **59%** of individuals earning **< R2,500/month** cite data
  cost as their main barrier to internet access.
- Within BRICS, SA is the **3rd most expensive** for data (500 MB ≈ US$5.72).
- **Prepaid dominates:** ~**76%** of users are on prepaid plans vs ~13% postpaid
  — so users actively watch and ration every megabyte.
- **Widest urban–rural gap in the world:** ~**57% of city residents online vs
  ~23% rural** (2024).
- Implication: design must be **low-data by default** (light pages, no autoplay,
  cached/offline-capable, SMS/USSD/WhatsApp fallbacks), tolerate **intermittent
  connectivity**, and never assume an always-on, big-data smartphone.

# Verbatim excerpts
> "59 percent of individuals earning less than 2,500 rand ($150) per month
> reported the costs of data as their main limitation to accessing the internet."

> "Internet use is much higher in cities, where 57% of residents were online in
> 2024, compared to just 23% in rural areas. This is the widest urban-rural gap
> anywhere in the world."
