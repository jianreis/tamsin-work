# Summary
Scale and business-model anchors. The strongest *hard* fact is Amazon's **absence**
from the Top-15 US pharmacies; revenue/loss figures are from a single leaked-document
story (⚠️ UNVERIFIED). All hosts 403'd; claims from search extracts.

## Key claims
- **Amazon Pharmacy is NOT in Drug Channels' Top-15 US pharmacies (2024 data)** — led by CVS (~$100.7B), Walgreens (~$91.1B), ESI (~$65.7B); top-15 ≈ $683B / ~75% of dispensing. ⇒ Amazon is **well under 1% of US dispensing revenue**. (Tier A/B, data 2024)
- **~$1.81B 2024 revenue (+45%)** and **~$420M 2024 loss (narrowing)** — from **leaked internal Amazon docs, Business Insider (Aug 2024)**; **⚠️ UNVERIFIED**, not confirmed by Amazon (no pharmacy segment disclosure). (Tier C leak / B amplification)
- Amazon Pharmacy **bills existing PBMs (ESI, CVS Caremark, OptumRx)** — it is **not** its own PBM. (Do not conflate with Amazon's employee benefit plan / "RxAdvance".)
- Strategy framed as a **Prime flywheel / long-horizon loss-leader** (~150M Prime members), not a current profit centre.
- Why PillPack struggled pre-Amazon: per-state licensing + capital-intensive central-fill + PBM gatekeeping = low margin, slow growth.
