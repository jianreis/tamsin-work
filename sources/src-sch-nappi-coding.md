## Summary

A NAPPI (National Pharmaceutical Product Index) code uniquely identifies a
medicine/surgical/consumable product and is the standard code used to bill and
claim it from a scheme. MediKredit owns and maintains the NAPPI file. NAPPI (the
product), ICD-10 (the diagnosis) and a valid prescription are the data triad a
dispensing claim is adjudicated against. CMS CMScript 10 of 2024 addresses
coding and funding of claims.

## Key claims

- A **NAPPI code** is a unique identifier for a pharmaceutical/medicinal/surgical/
  appliance/consumable product; it is the standard for **electronic claiming of
  medicines** (analogous to tariff codes for procedures).
- NAPPI codes are **9 digits**: first 6 identify the product/medicine, last 3 the
  pack size.
- **MediKredit owns and manages the NAPPI file** and is the only body that can
  assign NAPPI codes; the file is available in the public domain.
- A valid NAPPI is required to claim a rebate/refund; claims with missing/invalid
  NAPPI can be **rejected** (precedent: hearing-aid claims from 2017).
- ICD-10 diagnosis coding is required alongside NAPPI for adjudication (CMS
  CMScript 10 of 2024: coding and funding of claims).
