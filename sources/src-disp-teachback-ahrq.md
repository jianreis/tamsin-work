## Summary

The authoritative description of teach-back — an evidence-based health-literacy
technique to confirm patient understanding by asking them to restate
instructions in their own words — plus pharmacy-specific evidence of its impact
at medicine handover. Core method for counselling at the dispensing point.
Direct fetch returned HTTP 403 on 2026-06-13; claims drawn from AHRQ and PMC
search extracts.

## Key claims

- **Teach-back** is an **evidence-based health-literacy intervention**: the
  clinician asks the patient (or carer) to **explain the information back in
  their own words** to confirm understanding.
- Teach-back **confirms the clarity of the explanation, not the intelligence of
  the patient** — if the patient cannot restate it, the **clinician re-explains
  differently**; responsibility for clear communication sits with the
  professional.
- Best-practice counselling uses **plain language (target ~6th-grade reading
  level)**, **open-ended questions** ("tell me in your own words how you'll
  take this"), active listening and empathy.
- In a study of **pharmacist-provided teach-back at hospital discharge, 93.7% of
  patients reported increased confidence** in their medication knowledge and
  satisfaction with counselling.
- Patient-education materials should be designed for a **maximum ~6th-grade
  reading level**.
