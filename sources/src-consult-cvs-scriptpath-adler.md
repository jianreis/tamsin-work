# Summary

CVS's **ScriptPath** is the clearest example of **design applied to the script
artefact itself** — a redesigned pill-bottle label + consolidated medication
schedule by designer **Deborah Adler** (who created Target's acclaimed ClearRx
system). It turns a confusing pile of separate prescriptions into **one clear,
icon-driven schedule** showing when to take everything. A concrete, transferable
information-design pattern for the SA dispense/adhere moment. Reliability **B**
(company + trade-press corroborated). ⚠️ scale UNVERIFIED.

# Key claims
- **ScriptPath** = a system of tools: a redesigned **bottle label**, a consolidated
  **Prescription Schedule**, and a **Prescription Overview**, giving a complete
  picture of a patient's current CVS prescriptions in one place.
- Designed by **Deborah Adler** — designer of Target's **ClearRx** — explicitly to
  help patients (and caregivers) understand **the best time of day** to take each
  medicine, improving efficacy, safety, and **adherence**.
- Uses **easy-to-understand icons** to simplify dosing/timing for patients and
  caregivers, especially those on **multiple chronic prescriptions** (polypharmacy).
- Lesson: the **label and schedule are a design surface**, not just a regulatory
  printout — high-leverage for low-literacy / multilingual SA contexts.
