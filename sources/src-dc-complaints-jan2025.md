# Pharmacy & Scripting Complaints Analysis — January 2025 (C4C Resolved Complaints)

## Summary
Dis-Chem internal analysis of its **January 2025 C4C (Care-for-Customer)
"resolved complaints" dataset**, scoped to **360 pharmacy-related complaints**
across **Dispensary, OTC and Clinic** operations. The headline finding: the
overwhelming majority of complaints are **not about medication availability**,
but about the **end-to-end medication fulfilment journey**. The report groups
the complaints into **five themes**, draws **strategic findings**, and proposes
**five recommended focus areas** (ranked, with chronic medication as Priority 1).
A recurring meta-finding is that **root causes often sit in digital ordering,
script management and fulfilment, but surface as a pharmacy/service complaint** —
the pharmacy is "the visible face of the service".

**Reliability — why B (not A):** This is **first-party operational data** from
Dis-Chem's own complaints system, which is strong primary evidence of *what
customers complain about*. It is downgraded from A because: (1) it covers a
**single month** (January 2025) and may carry seasonal effects (post-festive,
January chronic re-orders); (2) it is limited to **resolved** complaints, so it
under-counts unresolved/abandoned cases and is a **selected** subset; (3) it
reports **no denominators** (complaints as a share of scripts/orders, or vs
total throughput), so frequencies are **directional, not rates**; (4) it is an
internal narrative analysis, not an audited or peer-reviewed study. Treat theme
**rankings and verbatim quotes as reliable signals of pain**, but treat any
implied prevalence cautiously.

## Key claims
- The dataset is **January 2025**, **n = 360** pharmacy-related complaints,
  spanning **Dispensary, OTC and Clinic** operations.
- Complaints are **predominantly about the fulfilment journey, not stock
  availability** — frustration is generated "at the intersection of Digital
  Ordering, Script Management, Pharmacy Operations and Last-Mile Delivery,
  rather than within a single functional area".
- **Theme 1 — Chronic medication & repeat-script management (the dominant
  cluster).** Customers struggle to re-order chronic medication, manage repeat
  prescriptions, use the Dis-Chem App, use WhatsApp ordering, and get help when
  digital journeys fail. The repeat-script journey generates **high customer
  effort and repeated contact**; customers get "trapped between digital
  channels, pharmacy staff and support centres without clear ownership".
- **Theme 2 — Medication delivery & fulfilment failures.** Medication is
  processed but not successfully delivered: approved on medical aid, delivery
  notification received, order marked dispatched — **yet medication never
  arrives**. One case: a chronic delivery **returned over a disputed co-payment**
  that a quick verification call could have resolved. Breakdown chain identified:
  **Script Processing → Dispensing → Delivery Scheduling → Customer Handover**.
- **Theme 3 — Dispensing accuracy & medication safety.** Customers receive
  **incorrect quantities** or experience dispensing errors (e.g. one week's
  supply instead of two; **blood-pressure tablets missing**, discovered only
  after leaving the store). Flagged as **higher-risk** because it directly
  affects **adherence and patient trust**.
- **Theme 4 — Pharmacy service experience.** Long waiting times, poor
  communication, **lack of ownership**, difficulty getting assistance, and
  **escalations that fail to resolve**. Customers associate process failures
  with **individual staff** because the pharmacy is the visible face — "even
  where root causes may sit in digital systems or fulfilment processes, customer
  dissatisfaction ultimately manifests as a pharmacy service complaint".
- **Theme 5 — Medical aid & billing administration.** Uncertainty around
  co-payments, medical-aid claims, clinic billing, and **scheduled-medication
  pricing**. Customers expect **greater consistency and transparency**; notably,
  scheduled-medication pricing perceived to **differ dramatically between
  Dis-Chem pharmacies**.
- **Strategic finding:** pharmacy complaints should **not** be viewed solely as
  Pharmacy Operations issues; they point to weaknesses across the broader
  fulfilment ecosystem where these functions intersect: **Digital Ordering
  Platforms · Script Management · Chronic Medication Services · Pharmacy
  Operations · Medical Aid Processing · Delivery Fulfilment**.
- **The strongest complaint cluster is the chronic-medication journey** — flagged
  for prioritised, deeper investigation.
- **Recommended Focus Area — Priority 1: Chronic Medication Journey Review** —
  end-to-end review of App ordering, WhatsApp ordering, repeat-script management,
  and customer-support escalation paths.
- **Priority 2: Medication Delivery Reliability** — order-tracking visibility,
  delivery exception handling, pharmacy-to-delivery handover processes.
- **Priority 3: Dispensing Quality Controls** — quantity-verification controls,
  final medication checks, chronic-medication packaging procedures.
- **Priority 4: Pharmacy Service Recovery** — escalation ownership, pharmacist
  issue-resolution capability, customer-communication standards.
- **Priority 5: Billing & Medical Aid Transparency** — co-payment communication,
  medical-aid claim visibility, pricing consistency for scheduled medication.
- **Conclusion:** dissatisfaction is driven primarily by **failures in the
  chronic medication fulfilment journey**, not availability; the biggest
  opportunity is improving the **end-to-end chronic experience**, where
  complaints highlight **process breakdowns, customer effort and a lack of clear
  ownership**.
