# Summary

Bain's long-running **Front Line of Healthcare** survey programme (US and
Asia-Pacific editions) plus patient-experience pieces. The most scripting-relevant
finding: **pharmacies are now a primary point of care** for a majority of
consumers, and patients-as-consumers expect retail-grade convenience and
responsiveness. Reliability **B** (survey-backed insight). Figures ⚠️ UNVERIFIED
beyond extracts (403).

# Key claims
- **Asia-Pacific Front Line of Healthcare (2024)**: ≥**65% of consumers** (≈74% of
  Gen Z) use **pharmacies as a primary point of care** — accelerated by COVID
  testing/vaccination experiences. ⚠️ UNVERIFIED (extract).
- Bain's patient-experience survey: **~65%** of healthcare consumers expect a **more
  convenient** experience and **~70%** expect **more responsiveness** than three
  years ago. ⚠️ UNVERIFIED (extract).
- Patient experience today is "marred by long wait times, difficulty scheduling,
  the need to chase down answers, confusion over billing" — i.e. **service-design
  failures**, the same failure modes a scripting journey must remove.
- Healthcare is shifting to delivery models that are **more convenient for consumers
  and cheaper to deliver** — pharmacy is a winner of this shift.
