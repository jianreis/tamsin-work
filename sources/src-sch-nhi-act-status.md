## Summary

The NHI Act (2023) was signed into law on 15 May 2024 to establish a single
NHI Fund as the dominant purchaser of healthcare, to be phased in 2024–2028.
Its most contested provision, **Section 33**, would restrict medical schemes to
"complementary cover" only once the Minister declares NHI "fully implemented"
(undefined). As of mid-2026 implementation is **paused** under court agreement;
the Constitutional Court heard public-participation challenges (BHF, Western
Cape Premier) on 5–7 May 2026 with judgment pending. Timelines are highly
uncertain — treat as scenarios.

## Key claims

- **NHI Act signed into law 15 May 2024**; establishes an NHI Fund as a single
  (dominant) purchaser; planned **phased implementation 2024–2028**, "fully
  functional by 2028" (aspirational).
- **Section 33**: "Once National Health Insurance has been fully implemented as
  determined by the Minister… through regulations in the Gazette, medical schemes
  may only offer complementary cover to services not reimbursable by the [NHI]
  Fund." The Minister decides when "fully implemented"; the term is **undefined**.
- Challengers (incl. **BHF, SAMA, HASA, Discovery Health, Solidarity**, Western
  Cape) argue **constitutional defects**: inadequate public participation,
  provincial powers, the signing process, and the schemes limitation.
- **Implementation paused**: High Court (Pretoria) ordered a halt; government
  agreed not to advance key parts pending resolution. **ConCourt heard
  challenges 5–7 May 2026**; **judgment pending** as of June 2026.
- Realistic near-term outcome: **status quo persists** — schemes, PMBs, DSPs and
  courier pharmacy continue largely unchanged for the medium term.
