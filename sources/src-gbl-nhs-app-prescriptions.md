# Summary

The consumer-facing layer over EPS: patients order repeat prescriptions in the
NHS App, route them to a nominated pharmacy, and track status with "Amazon-style"
real-time updates. Strong evidence that a single, free, government-run app can
reach mass adoption — directly relevant to a future SA scripting app. NHS
official → A. Some figures are NHS-reported.

# Key claims
- **Flow:** in-app → "View and manage prescriptions" → order repeat items →
  request routes to GP for approval → if the patient has a **nominated pharmacy**
  the approved script flows via **EPS** straight there (no paper/barcode); app
  shows real-time status (ordered → ready → collected/delivered).
- **>2.7 million** people used the new NHS App prescription feature in its first
  two months (NHS England, March 2024).
- Repeat prescriptions ordered via the app rose **40%, from 3.9m (April 2024) to
  5.5m (April 2025)**.
- **"Amazon-style" tracking** went live May 2025 at **~1,500 pharmacies including
  every Boots in England**, reaching ~7.5m patients.
- December 2025: **>39 million registered users**; **67.8 million repeat
  prescriptions** ordered via the app in the 12 months to 30 Nov 2025.
- From **July 2025** a redesign made nominating a pharmacy in-app simpler.
