## Summary

CCMDD is the public-sector programme that lets clinically stable chronic patients
collect repeat medicine away from clinics, at external pick-up points (PUPs). It
is the largest decentralised chronic-medicine collection system in SA and the key
public-sector precedent for "scripting at distance" — proof that out-of-facility
collection works at scale and reduces queues/stigma. NDoH page returned 403 on
direct fetch; figures from NDoH/Project Last Mile/peer-reviewed reporting.

## Key claims

- CCMDD lets **clinically stable** public-sector chronic patients collect ARVs
  and/or other chronic meds at a **certified community pick-up point of choice**
  (private pharmacies, retail, schools, churches, workplaces).
- **>4.9 million patients enrolled** (some reporting 5.3m registered); **~64%
  collect at external pick-up points** rather than at the clinic.
- Network of **>3,000 external pick-up points** nationwide.
- Originally for stable HIV/ART patients; **expanded to all chronic conditions**
  (hypertension, diabetes, epilepsy, asthma, mental health, etc.).
- Patients collect a **pre-packed medicine parcel every 2–3 months**.
