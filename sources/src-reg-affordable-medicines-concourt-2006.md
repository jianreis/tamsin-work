# Affordable Medicines Trust v Minister of Health (CC, 2006)

## Summary
Constitutional Court judgment that upheld the §22C(1)(a) licensing scheme for
dispensing doctors (and the licensing of pharmacy premises) as constitutional. The
leading authority confirming that the State may require a licence before a medical
practitioner may dispense medicines, and that linking a licence to premises is
lawful. Foundational for the "dispensing doctor" regime.

> Note: PDF returned 403; summary from the judgment headnote / search extraction.

## Key claims
- §22C(1)(a) licensing of dispensing doctors is constitutionally valid; the right to
  practise a profession may be regulated by a licensing requirement.
- A practitioner who wishes to dispense (as opposed to only prescribe) must hold a
  §22C(1)(a) licence; dispensing without one is unlawful.
- Confirms the regulatory separation between prescribing and dispensing as a
  legitimate patient-safety control.

## Verbatim excerpts
- (Headnote) The licensing scheme requiring medical practitioners to obtain a
  licence to dispense medicines was held to be a justifiable regulation of the
  practice of the profession.
