## Summary

Evidence on robotic/automated dispensing. Central Fill Pharmacy Systems (CFPS)
use robotic dispensing systems (RDS) to store, count and dispense at high volume;
camera, scale, barcode and RFID checks underpin accuracy. A peer-reviewed Brazilian
retail-pharmacy case study (MDPI Pharmacy, 2024) found daily staff time on stock
intake/storage/order selection fell **59.3%** after RDS implementation. Vendor and
review sources cite ~**99.9%** dispensing accuracy for automated filling, with
machine cycle times of ~3s input and 8–12s output per pack and simultaneous
multi-pack dispensing. Separately, barcode-assisted dispensing is associated with
large error reductions (one study: target dispensing errors −85%, potential ADEs
−74%). A "Critical Evaluation … Call to Action" review cautions that automation
evidence quality is uneven and benefits depend on workflow redesign, not the robot
alone. Distinguishes inpatient ADCs from community/central-fill use cases.

## Key claims
- CFPS + RDS fulfil **high-volume** prescriptions; accuracy underpinned by cameras,
  scales, barcodes, RFID.
- Vendor/review figure: ~**99.9%** accuracy for robotic automated filling.
- Brazilian retail case study: **−59.3%** daily staff time on stock/order tasks
  post-RDS.
- Throughput: ~3s stock input, 8–12s output per pack; up to nine packs
  simultaneously; dual picking heads.
- Barcode technology study: **−85%** target dispensing errors, **−74%** target
  potential adverse drug events.
- Critical review: automation evidence is uneven; gains require workflow redesign
  and are not guaranteed by hardware.
