## Summary

SA's National Digital Health Strategy (NDHS) 2019–2024 frames digital health as
foundational to National Health Insurance (NHI). It sets a goal of building, by
end-2024, the integrated information architecture — anchored on the **Health
Patient Registration System (HPRS)** as a national patient registry / master
patient index (MPI) — needed for a national electronic health record (EHR). The
**Health Normative Standards Framework (HNSF)** ("National Health Normative
Standards Framework for Interoperability in eHealth in South Africa") prescribes
the standards SA systems should use, derived from review of international eHealth
standards against SA business use cases. HNSF references content standards (HL7
CDA, Continuity of Care Document), identifier standards (ISO 22220, ISO/TS 27527),
messaging (HL7 v2.x, DICOM), terminology (ICD-10) and medicine codes. Notably the
named messaging/content standards skew to **older HL7 v2/CDA** rather than FHIR,
and e-prescribing maturity is low: a national e-prescribing standard is not yet
operationalised, and EHR diagnostic/treatment/billing modules for NHI remain
undeveloped.

## Key claims
- NDHS 2019–2024 developed amid healthcare reorganisation for **NHI**; digital
  health positioned as an NHI enabler.
- Goal: by **end-2024**, establish integrated information architecture via the
  **HPRS** for a robust national EHR system.
- **HPRS** (started 2014) = national patient registration / MPI, keyed on the SA
  ID number (and passports), to track patients across facilities.
- **HNSF** sets normative interoperability standards; reviewed international
  standards against SA use cases for compliance assessment of patient/hospital
  information systems.
- HNSF-named standards: HL7 CDA + CCD (content), ISO 22220:2011 / ISO/TS
  27527:2010 (identifiers), HL7 v2.x, DICOM, SDMX-DM (messaging), ICD-10 +
  medicine codes (terminology). FHIR not centrally mandated.
- EHR diagnostic/treatment/billing modules needed for NHI "are yet to be
  developed"; SA e-prescribing maturity is low.
