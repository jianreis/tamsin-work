# Identifying legitimate websites selling medicines in SA (2024)

## Summary
Peer-reviewed study setting out the legal requirements for legitimate online
medicine sale in SA and auditing actual online "pharmacy" websites against them.
Useful as the clearest synthesis of the courier/online/distance-selling rules.

> Note: PMC/HSAG/SciELO all returned 403 to fetch; claims from search extraction.

## Key claims
- Legitimate online medicine sale in SA requires: (1) the medicine is registered
  with SAHPRA; (2) the selling website is linked to a physical community or
  institutional pharmacy that is licensed by the NDoH and recorded with SAPC;
  (3) scheduled medicines are sold only by/under the supervision of a pharmacist;
  (4) compliance with Medicines Act + Pharmacy Act + GPP.
- Prescription-only (S3+) medicines require a valid prescription on ordering.
- Findings: many audited sites were non-compliant — none required a prescription on
  ordering even for S3+ equivalents; some product names/APIs were not verifiable on
  the SAHPRA register (i.e. unregistered/illegal supply).

## Verbatim excerpts
- "consumers should only use websites associated with physical community and
  hospital pharmacies registered by the NDoH and recorded by the SAPC, as well as
  can only purchase medication that is registered with SAHPRA."
- "Scheduled medicines may only be sold to the public by a pharmacist and/or
  pharmacy support personnel ... under the direct supervision of a pharmacist, from
  a community or institutional pharmacy with a license issued by the Department of
  Health and recorded with the SAPC."
