## Summary

Describes the telepharmacy operating model and its evidence. A pharmacist at one
location provides oversight/care at another via secure audio-video + software: an
on-site trained technician (or automation) receives, prepares and processes the
prescription; the **remote pharmacist performs final verification**, conducts
required counselling by video, answers questions and documents. Remote order entry
and verification let a central pharmacist check work across multiple sites. Key
evidence: a study of rural-Midwest telepharmacy found **no difference in medication-
use quality** versus traditional pharmacies; the model is described as proven and
scalable for expanding access and letting pharmacists practise "at the top of their
license". Directly relevant to extending pharmacist verification across a branch
network or to underserved areas without a resident pharmacist.

## Key claims
- Telepharmacy: remote pharmacist oversees care at another site via audio-video +
  secure platform; on-site technician/automation handles prep.
- **Remote pharmacist performs final verification** + video counselling +
  documentation.
- Remote order entry/verification lets one pharmacist cover multiple sites.
- Rural-Midwest study: **no difference** in medication-use quality vs traditional
  pharmacies.
- Model described as proven/scalable for access expansion and top-of-licence
  practice.
