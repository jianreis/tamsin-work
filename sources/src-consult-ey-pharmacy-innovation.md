# Summary

EY UK's pharmacy-innovation article argues automation of dispensing + a
physical/digital blend will reshape community pharmacy, freeing pharmacists for
clinical roles; it flags **low digital uptake of repeat prescriptions** as the key
gap, with international exemplars. Plus EY's **Consumer Health Survey** on
friction/"digital front door." Reliability **B**. Figures ⚠️ UNVERIFIED (403).

# Key claims
- Only **~1 in 10 patients in England** manage repeat prescriptions **digitally** —
  a large untapped digital-adoption gap (the same opportunity SA faces). ⚠️ UNVERIFIED.
- As **dispensing automates**, more pharmacies may close, and the survivors blend
  **physical + digital** channels; pharmacists shift to **clinical** work.
- Workforce fragility: in 2022, **81%** of UK community pharmacists considered leaving;
  ~**8%** vacancy — a warning that the clinical-pharmacist vision needs workforce
  investment to be real.
- International exemplars: **~70% of Danish community pharmacies use dispensing
  robots**; European pharmacies increasingly access **patient medical records** to
  advise better (interoperability as an enabler).
- EY **Consumer Health Survey 2023** frames the "**digital front door**" — intuitive
  digital tools that reduce friction finding/accessing care; "Digital Delighters"
  simplify the pharmacy consumer journey with omnichannel access.
