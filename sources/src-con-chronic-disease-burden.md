## Summary

Establishes the scale and mix of SA's chronic-disease burden — the demand driver
for repeat scripting. SA carries the world's largest HIV treatment programme plus
a rising non-communicable-disease (NCD) load (hypertension, diabetes), and a high
TB burden — increasingly as multi-morbidity in single patients. Mix of
peer-reviewed and official surveillance → B (specific prevalence figures vary by
survey/year).

## Key claims

- SA has the **largest HIV population in the world (~7.9 million people living
  with HIV)**; ARV rollout since 2004 has driven the world's largest ART
  programme and rising life expectancy.
- **Hypertension** is the leading NCD: ~**14.3% (2017)** among adults ≥25 living
  with HIV; population-wide hypertension is substantially higher in older adults.
- **Diabetes ~4.4%** (national survey among the studied cohort); rising with
  ageing/obesity.
- **Multi-morbidity** (HIV + hypertension/diabetes; HIV + TB) is increasingly
  common — patients on **several concurrent chronic scripts**.
- Implication: repeat-scripting volume is dominated by **stable chronic patients**,
  often on multiple medicines, the exact CCMDD-eligible cohort.
