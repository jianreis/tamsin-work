# Summary

McKinsey's flagship "retail pharmacy of the future" article, grounded in a
consumer survey of 1,000+ US adults, segmenting how different pharmacy types
(retail chains, regional/grocer, independents, mail-order/online) must respond to
shifting consumer preferences toward **convenience, personalisation, and a wider
clinical-services offer**. Consultancy thought-leadership → reliability **B**
(survey methods plausible, framing promotional; ⚠️ UNVERIFIED on exact figures
since WebFetch is 403-blocked — triangulated from McKinsey result extracts).

# Key claims
- McKinsey surveyed **1,000+ US consumers** to understand changing preferences and
  segment likely pharmacy responses. ⚠️ UNVERIFIED (extract).
- The retail-pharmacy sector is framed as **four types**: retail chains; regional
  (mass retail / grocers); independents; and mail-order/online.
- **Convenience and personalisation** are the two dominant growth trends.
- **~40% of surveyed consumers** said they were interested in accessing
  common-illness treatment, "whole-health fusion," and other services (primary
  care, dental, labs, X-rays) **via their pharmacy** — i.e. the pharmacy as a
  broader care destination, not just a dispensary. ⚠️ UNVERIFIED (extract).
- Implication: pharmacies that win will combine **frictionless fulfilment** (the
  transactional script journey) with **higher-value clinical/health services**.
