## Summary

What a member/pharmacist experiences when a real-time claim adjudicates: the
medicine is dispensed immediately if funds/benefits are available; if the
medicine is off-formulary a co-payment may be requested or the claim rejected;
the pharmacist relays this at the counter and the member may get an SMS.
Rejections often stem from data mismatches, missing benefit, off-formulary
medicine, or coding errors. Appeals typically take ~30 days.

## Key claims

- Pharmacies send medicine claims **electronically at point of sale**; the scheme
  applies its rules so the member knows **immediately** whether it will pay.
- The member **gets the medicine immediately if funds/benefits are available**.
- If the medicine is **not on the scheme list**: scheme may require a
  **co-payment** or **reject** the claim; the pharmacist informs the member, who
  may also receive an **SMS** (if contact details are current).
- Common rejection cause: **submitted info not matching the database** (name, ID,
  policy/membership number, date of birth).
- Other causes: missing documentation, off-formulary, failed medical-necessity,
  benefit exhausted, coding errors.
- Appeals process typically ~**30 days** to a decision.
