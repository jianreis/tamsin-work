# NHI Act 2023 — status and pricing implications

## Summary
The NHI Act was signed 15 May 2024 but is not yet in force (no proclaimed effective
date as of mid-2026). It is the dominant in-flux factor for future SA pharmacy
design. It amends the Medicines Act only lightly for now (Pricing Committee
membership) but signals deeper future change to procurement and reimbursement.

## Key claims
- Signed into law by the President on 15 May 2024; gazetted 16 May 2024; effective
  date not yet proclaimed (in whole or in part) as of 2026.
- Amends the Medicines Act to give the NHI Fund a role in appointing Pricing
  Committee members; otherwise proposes no §22G amendments yet.
- Long-term: NHI Fund intended as a single (strategic) purchaser; implications for
  private retail pharmacy supply chains, SEP and dispensing fees are uncertain.

## Verbatim excerpts
- "The National Health Insurance Act 20 of 2023 ... was signed into law by President
  Ramaphosa on 15 May 2024 ... However, the President has not yet proclaimed the
  effective date of the NHI Act, either as a whole or in part."
- "The NHI Act amends the Medicines and Related Substances Act to introduce the NHI
  Fund's influence into the appointment of members of the pricing committee, but
  otherwise proposes no amendments to that Act."
