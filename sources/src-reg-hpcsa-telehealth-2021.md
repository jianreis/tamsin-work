# HPCSA Telehealth Guidelines (Booklet 10, Dec 2021)

## Summary
HPCSA ethical guidance for registered health practitioners practising telehealth
(renamed from "Telemedicine" in 2021). Governs the practitioner-patient
relationship, informed consent, and telehealth prescribing. Materially relaxed
during COVID-19 (April 2020) and the relaxation was carried into the Dec 2021
revision.

> Note: PDF returned 403; content triangulated across the guideline extraction,
> ITWeb, Cliffe Dekker Hofmeyr and the published HPCSA-review literature.

## Key claims
- Pre-COVID rule: telehealth permissible ONLY where an established
  practitioner-patient relationship existed.
- April 2020 amendment (COVID): relaxed to "should preferably be practised" where a
  relationship exists; "where such a relationship does not exist, practitioners may
  still consult using telehealth provided that such consultations are done in the
  best clinical interest of patients."
- Dec 2021 revision retained the relaxed position; distinguishes routine,
  specialist and emergency telehealth.
- Telehealth prescribing: informed consent must be taken to the same standard as
  face-to-face prescribing; the clinical assessment must support the prescription.
- Guidelines remain in force (2024+) and are amended by HPCSA from time to time.

## Verbatim excerpts
- (April 2020 amendment) "Telehealth should preferably be practised in circumstances
  where there is an already established practitioner-patient relationship. Where
  such a relationship does not exist, practitioners may still consult using
  telehealth provided that such consultations are done in the best clinical
  interest of patients."
- "When prescribing care using telemedicine, consulting practitioners should ensure
  that informed consent is taken in accordance with the standards practice used in
  face-to-face issuing of prescriptions."
