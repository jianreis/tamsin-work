# Summary

The standard practitioner textbook for service design (the "TiSDD" canon,
successor to *This Is Service Design Thinking*). Establishes the shared
vocabulary and process the field uses: research → ideation → prototyping →
implementation, with **journey maps** and **service blueprints** as the two core
mapping tools. Confirms the swim-lane anatomy of blueprints and the
persona+scenario framing of journey maps, and frames service design as
iterative, evidence-based, and co-created. Used to anchor method conventions in
the vault's UX pages.

> Note: claims drawn from the book's official site, the Service Design Network
> (SDN) summaries, and widely indexed chapter material, retrieved 2026-06-13.

# Key claims
- Service design is human-centred, collaborative, iterative, sequential
  (visualising the service as a sequence of interrelated actions), real
  (grounded in real evidence/prototypes), and holistic (front- and backstage
  together). These are the field's working principles.
- A **service blueprint** "visualizes the relationships between different
  service components — people, props (physical or digital evidence), and
  processes — that are directly tied to touchpoints in a specific customer
  journey." Blueprints "often act as the next step after customer-journey maps."
- Blueprint **swim lanes** typically include: customer actions, frontstage
  (line-of-visibility) actions, backstage actions, and support processes;
  columns align to the **phases of the customer's journey**; arrows link
  interactions across lanes.
- A **journey map** visualises the experience of a specific persona pursuing a
  specific goal/scenario over a sequence of steps; it is the primary tool for
  building shared empathy and locating pain points and opportunities.
- Stickdorn's later "Journey Management / JM-Ops" work argues maps and
  blueprints must be **living, owned, operational artefacts** — continuously
  updated against evidence — not one-off deliverables.
