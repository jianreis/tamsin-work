# Summary

The Behaviour Change Wheel (BCW) and its central **COM-B** model (Michie, van
Stralen & West, 2011) — the dominant evidence-based framework for diagnosing
behaviour and selecting interventions. COM-B holds that any behaviour (B)
requires **Capability**, **Opportunity**, and **Motivation**. Widely applied to
medication adherence to identify *why* a patient is non-adherent and which
intervention type fits. Used to structure adherence design in the vault.

# Key claims
- **COM-B:** Behaviour occurs when **Capability** + **Opportunity** + **Motivation**
  are all sufficient. The three interact (e.g. opportunity and capability can
  shape motivation).
- **Capability** = psychological (knowledge, skills, comprehension) and physical
  (dexterity, e.g. opening packaging, using an app) ability to perform the
  behaviour.
- **Opportunity** = physical (time, resources, access, cost, location) and
  social (norms, cues, support, culture) factors external to the person that
  make the behaviour possible or prompt it.
- **Motivation** = reflective (conscious plans, beliefs, intentions) and
  automatic (habits, emotions, impulses) brain processes that energise the
  behaviour.
- The BCW maps COM-B deficits to **nine intervention functions** (education,
  persuasion, incentivisation, coercion, training, restriction, environmental
  restructuring, modelling, enablement) and to policy categories. Diagnose
  first, then choose the function that addresses the actual deficit.
- Applied to **adherence**: non-adherence is often *not* a motivation problem —
  it is frequently a capability (forgetting, not understanding the regimen) or
  opportunity (cost, access, supply) problem. Design the intervention to the
  diagnosed cause (e.g. reminders for automatic-motivation/forgetting;
  affordability/auto-refill for opportunity; teach-back for capability).
- Adherence interventions evaluated with the BCW (e.g. two-way mHealth) target
  multiple COM-B components simultaneously.

# Verbatim excerpts
> "The 'behaviour system' involves three essential conditions: capability,
> opportunity, and motivation… interacting to generate behaviour that in turn
> influences these components." (Michie, van Stralen & West, 2011)

> COM-B "guides the identification and organization of multilevel determinants
> of medication adherence along three categories: psychological and physical
> capability, automatic and reflective motivation, and physical and social
> opportunity."
