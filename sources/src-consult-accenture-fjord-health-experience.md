# Summary

Accenture's health-experience offer is delivered partly through **Fjord (now part of
Accenture Song)**, its global design/innovation arm, plus the **Patient Experience
Lab** for life sciences. The work is human-centred service/experience design for
patients and caregivers; Fjord Trends explicitly calls for **equitable and
empathetic healthcare**. Marketing-adjacent → reliability **C** (use as lead/colour;
the concrete CCS adherence engagement is captured separately as B-ish).

# Key claims
- **Fjord** = Accenture Song's design/innovation consultancy (founded 2001;
  2,000+ designers, ~36 studios) doing human-centred design across health.
- **Fjord Trends** push "**equitable and empathetic healthcare**" — designing for
  access, dignity, and the caregiver, not just the patient.
- Accenture's **Patient Experience Lab** combines "experience, evidence and
  economics" end-to-end for life sciences patient services.
- Accenture frames the **caregiver** as a first-class user alongside the patient —
  relevant to SA proxy/collection journeys.
