# Dr Andy Gray on SA medicine pricing (Feb 2026)

## Summary
Authoritative explainer by a WHO-collaborating-centre pharmaceutical-policy expert
on how SEP, logistics fee, dispensing fee and SEPA work, and how NHI may change
the pricing framework. Strong Tier-B corroboration of the §22G regime.

## Key claims
- The Pricing Committee annually solicits input on two things: the dispensing fees
  (pharmacists + dispensing doctors) and the SEPA (max manufacturer price increase).
- SEPA 2026 = max 1.47% (2025 was 5.25%); the mechanism has protected SA from large
  international-style price increases.
- Wholesalers earn a logistics fee paid by the manufacturer, not a mark-up on SEP.
- NHI Act amends the Medicines Act to bring the NHI Fund into the appointment of
  Pricing Committee members, but otherwise leaves §22G largely intact for now.

## Verbatim excerpts
- "The SEPA allowed for 2026 was set at a maximum of 1.47%, compared with 5.25% in 2025."
- "Wholesalers do not add a mark-up to the SEP charged by the manufacturer, but are
  paid a logistics fee by the manufacturer, as a portion of the exit price."
