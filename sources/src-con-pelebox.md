## Summary

Pelebox (by Technovera, founder Neo Hutiri) is an SA-built smart-locker system —
a "medicine ATM" — for self-service collection of pre-packed chronic medicine,
used in conjunction with CCMDD. The standout local precedent for an SMS+PIN,
no-app, low-literacy collection UX that collapses 3.5-hour clinic queues to under
a minute. Reliability B (multiple consistent reports of a deployed, awarded pilot).

## Key claims

- Patient's medicine is **pre-packed and loaded into a locker**; system sends an
  **SMS with a one-time PIN**; patient enters **cellphone number + PIN** at the
  locker to open their compartment. **No app, no data required.**
- Cuts collection from **~3.5 hours of queueing to under ~30 seconds** (reported
  average 36s; "under 22 seconds" cited).
- Stores **chronic medication only** (HIV, TB, hypertension, etc.); parcels are the
  CCMDD brown-box parcels.
- Deployable at **non-medical sites**; piloted with City of Tshwane, NDoH, City of
  Johannesburg; thousands of patients served.
