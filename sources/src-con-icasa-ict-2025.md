## Summary

ICASA's annual regulatory market report. The authoritative SA source for mobile
subscriptions, smartphone penetration, network coverage, broadband, and the
prepaid/postpaid split. Figures here are the 2024 reporting year as published in
the March 2025 report. ICASA's official figures (Tier A) are preferred over
secondary aggregators for connectivity stats. Direct PDF fetch was blocked on
retrieval; figures below are taken from ICASA-sourced reporting (ITWeb /
Engineering News summarising the report) pending direct verification.

## Key claims

- **Smartphone subscriptions rose 10.36% to 82.74 million in 2024** (ICASA, 2024
  reporting year). Subscriptions exceed population; many users hold multiple SIMs.
- **Mobile cellular data users: 42.31 million in 2024** (+1.52%).
- **3G population coverage 99.79%; 4G/LTE 99.07%; 5G 46.64%** in 2024 (5G up from
  35.42% in 2023, 0.70% in 2020). Coverage is near-universal; the gap is
  affordability/devices, not signal.
- **Fixed broadband surged from 1.4m (2023) to 2.7m (2024)**, driven by fibre;
  fibre-to-the-home/building 2.47m in 2024 (from 1.49m in 2023). Still a small
  share of households — most access is mobile.
- SA mobile market is **overwhelmingly prepaid** (the large majority of
  subscribers are prepaid; postpaid is a minority — long-standing structural
  feature of the SA market).
