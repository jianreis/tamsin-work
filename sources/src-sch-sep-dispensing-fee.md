## Summary

How medicine is priced in SA's private sector: the Single Exit Price (SEP) is
the single, transparent, regulated maximum manufacturer-to-everyone price (no
discounts/bonusing), on top of which a **regulated dispensing fee** applies.
The fee is a banded formula (rand + % of SEP) capped at the top. Cash and
insured patients alike pay SEP + the regulated dispensing fee. Annual SEP
adjustments (SEPA) are capped (1.47% for 2026, down from 5.25% in 2025).

## Key claims

- **SEP** = the single maximum price a medicine can be sold for in the private
  sector; the same to all buyers (no discounts/rebates).
- A **regulated dispensing fee** is added on top of SEP, by banded formula (2025
  Regs / Sept 2025 version cited):
  - SEP < R109.56 → fee max **R14.50 + 46.0% of SEP**
  - SEP < R292.25 → fee max **R27.75 + 33.0% of SEP**
  - SEP < R1022.94 → fee max **R79.00 + 15.0% of SEP**
  - otherwise → fee max **R182.00 + 5.0% of SEP**
  - (Sept 2025 update: where SEP > R1,530.73, an additional/alternate fee cap of
    R2 applies in certain provisions — re-confirm against the Gazette.)
- **SEPA** (annual SEP adjustment ceiling): **1.47% for 2026** vs **5.25% for
  2025**.
- Nov 2025: the DG of Health raised concern about state bid prices exceeding
  private SEP.
