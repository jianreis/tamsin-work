# Summary

India's e-pharmacy players scaled to tens of millions of users in an emerging
market with low formal-insurance penetration and a fragmented independent-chemist
landscape — close to SA's structure. The model that won was **omnichannel /
conglomerate-backed** (Apollo, Tata), not venture-subsidised pure-play
(PharmEasy's collapse). All run a teleconsult → e-prescription → order → deliver
loop with prescription-photo upload + pharmacist verification. Mix of reputable
press and listed-company disclosure → B; user counts are company claims.

# Key claims
- **Prescription-upload mechanism (PharmEasy):** user uploads a **photo of a paper
  script**; **licensed pharmacists review/verify** it (doctor name + registration
  number + patient name + date); phone-call confirmation before dispatch; the
  delivery agent also gets it validated at the dispensing store. Integrated
  teleconsult → digital prescription → order → diagnostics.
- **Apollo 24|7 omnichannel:** teleconsult → digital prescription → fulfilment
  **from the Apollo Pharmacy physical store network** (largest chain in Asia;
  reached **7,000 stores, Nov 2025**) used as last-mile dark stores; **~19-minute
  delivery** in metros; "Apollo Circle" membership ~₹299/year. Apollo 24|7 daily
  orders ~**75,000 in Q3 FY25** (up from 61,000 a year earlier); Apollo HealthCo
  revenue ~₹9,093 Cr FY25 (listed-disclosure = strongest numbers).
- **Tata 1mg:** drug-info + interaction checker (its differentiator), upload +
  order, home lab tests, e-consult; Tata Digital control (2021). Became market
  leader at **~31% share (Sept 2023)** as PharmEasy fell to ~15%. FY25 revenue
  ~₹2,392 Cr (+22%), losses narrowing — conglomerate balance sheet beat cash-burn.
- **Netmeds (Reliance, acquired 2020):** subscription/scheduled chronic refills;
  fulfilment via Reliance Retail network + JioMart integration; true scale opaque
  (spread across multiple Reliance entities).
- **PharmEasy cautionary tale:** peaked ~$5.6bn (2021), debt-funded M&A
  (Thyrocare), **withdrew its IPO (2022–23)**, recapitalised in a ~90%-down rights
  round (2023). Pure-play venture-subsidised e-pharmacy economics did not hold.
- **Regulatory grey-zone:** no dedicated notified e-pharmacy law; governed by the
  **Drugs & Cosmetics Act 1940/Rules 1945** (valid Rx required for Schedule H/H1/X;
  pharmacist dispensing). Draft e-pharmacy rules pending since the **28 Aug 2018**
  gazette, never finalised; **Feb 2023 DCGI show-cause notices** to ~20 platforms;
  strong offline-chemist lobby (AIOCD, ~1.25m members); Delhi HC pressure (2023).
  **Telemedicine Practice Guidelines 2020** legitimised teleconsult → e-prescription
  (excluding high-risk Schedule classes) — the legal hinge for the consult→order loop.
