## Summary

The SA legal framework for scheduling (Schedules 0–6) and the record-keeping /
supply controls for scheduled substances — the non-negotiable regulatory floor
for controlled-substance handling in any Dis-Chem dispensing workflow. Direct
fetch returned HTTP 403 on 2026-06-13; claims drawn from SAHPRA/Act search
extracts and the Act's well-documented provisions.

## Key claims

- Medicines are classified into **Schedules 0–6** by abuse potential and risk;
  scheduling determines **where and how a medicine may be sold and what records
  must be kept**.
- **Schedule 2–6 substances** may only be sold/dispensed by a **pharmacist (or
  intern / pharmacist's assistant under personal supervision)**; **Schedule 0**
  is general sale and **Schedule 1** requires pharmacist oversight; **Schedule
  2** may be sold by a pharmacist without a prescription within limits.
- **Schedule 3–6** require a **valid prescription** (Schedule 5 has a default
  6-month / limited-repeat regime; Schedule 6 requires the strictest control).
- **Schedule 5 and 6** substances have **moderate-to-high abuse/dependence
  potential**, requiring **close management and strict control over supply**;
  **Schedule 5 may not be advertised to the public**.
- **All prescribed particulars of every sale of a scheduled substance must be
  recorded** in a prescription book or other permanent record kept in the
  prescribed manner (the scheduled-substances register).

## Verbatim excerpts

> "Any Schedule 2, Schedule 3, Schedule 4, Schedule 5 or Schedule 6 substance
> shall not be sold by any person except by a pharmacist, pharmacist intern or a
> pharmacist's assistant acting under the personal supervision of a pharmacist."

> "All the prescribed particulars of every sale shall be recorded in the
> prescribed manner in a prescription book or other permanent record."

> "Schedule 6 substances may have a moderate to high potential for abuse or for
> producing dependence, which necessitates close medical management and
> supervision and strict control over supply."
