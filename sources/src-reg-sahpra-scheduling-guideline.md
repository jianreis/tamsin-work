# SAHPRA Scheduling Guideline (SAHPGL-CEM-NS-02 v3, May 2022)

## Summary
SAHPRA's official guideline explaining the principles by which substances are
classified into schedules and the access/sale conditions that follow from each
schedule. Authoritative interpretive companion to §22A and the Schedules.

> Note: PDF returned 403 to fetch; content triangulated from search extraction.

## Key claims
- Defines who may prescribe within scope: practitioners, nurses and other
  Health-Professions-Act registrants may prescribe S4 (and certain S6) only within
  their scope of practice, subject to the indication and conditions set by SAHPRA,
  and must be registered as prescribers by their own professional council.
- Confirms the prescription thresholds (S3+ require a prescription) and the
  emergency-supply and S6 30-day rules.
- Scheduling status changes via call-up / amendment notices in the Gazette.

## Verbatim excerpts
- "A practitioner, nurse or a person registered under the Health Professions Act,
  1974, other than a medical practitioner or dentist, may prescribe and supply,
  only within his/her scope of practice and subject to the indication for use of
  such substances and medicines and to the conditions determined by the Authority,
  to patients under his/her care."
