## Key claims

- **MTM has five core elements**: (1) **medication therapy review**; (2)
  **personal medication record**; (3) **medication-related action plan**; (4)
  **intervention and/or referral**; (5) **documentation and follow-up**.
- Pharmacists can identify **clinically significant drug–drug interactions**
  because they have **insight into the patient's entire therapy** — across
  multiple prescribers and OTC products — that individual prescribers lack.
- Beyond detection, pharmacists **recommend how to resolve or monitor** potential
  interactions; DDI assessment should occur **at each MTM visit and during the
  dispensing process**.
- Advanced medication reviews address **interactions, side effects, unusual
  dosages, adherence issues, drug–food interactions, effectiveness and dosage
  problems**.
- A **standardised DDI-management protocol** increases efficiency, enables
  standardisation and improves quality of care.
