# Summary

NHS England's articulation of the **"digital front door"** — a single, coherent,
patient-facing entry point (typically an app/web/messaging layer) that lets
people access health services, navigate to the right care, and self-serve, while
keeping non-digital routes open. The reference pattern for the unified entry to a
future-state scripting service, including its **care-navigation** function.

# Key claims
- A **digital front door** is a unified, easy front-end through which people
  access and navigate services (book, order, message, check status, find the
  right next step) — a "front door," not the whole building behind it.
- It must **route/triage** users to the appropriate service or channel (**care
  navigation**), not just present a menu — reducing wrong turns and unnecessary
  contact.
- Must be **co-created with the people who face the biggest barriers** to digital
  access, to surface problems early and avoid excluding them.
- **Channel choice and assisted digital:** a digital front door should not be the
  *only* door; offline/assisted routes (phone, in-person, proxy) must remain for
  those who cannot or will not use digital.
- Value: consolidation reduces fragmentation across multiple apps/portals;
  consistency builds trust and lowers cognitive load.

# Verbatim excerpts
> The digital front door means "making it easier for people to access NHS
> services … a consistent and joined-up way in."

> "Working closely with people who face the biggest barriers to using digital
> health services to uncover problems early and design solutions together."
