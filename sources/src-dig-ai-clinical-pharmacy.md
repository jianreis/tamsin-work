## Summary

Peer-reviewed and professional-body synthesis on AI in pharmacy. AI strengthens
clinical decision support — screening drug-drug interactions, flagging medication
errors, generating allergy alerts, optimising dosing from patient data — and
supports demand sensing, shortage-risk identification and inventory optimisation
(including automated suggestion of therapeutic alternatives during shortages). The
JACCP forecast (Wong, 2025) frames AI as an emerging partner that augments, not
replaces, the pharmacist, contingent on data quality and governance. Reviews stress
that much current evidence is early-stage; benefits are real but unevenly proven,
and require "solid data foundations". Useful for separating proven CDS/forecasting
gains from hype.

## Key claims
- AI CDS can screen drug-drug interactions, flag medication errors, generate
  allergy alerts and optimise dosing from patient-specific data.
- AI demand sensing / shortage-risk identification improves inventory optimisation;
  can suggest therapeutic alternatives during shortages.
- Effective supply-chain AI requires "solid data foundations"; gains are uneven
  without them.
- AI positioned as augmenting the pharmacist (CDS, documentation, communications),
  not replacing clinical judgement.
- Current evidence skews early-stage; rigour/standardisation still maturing.
