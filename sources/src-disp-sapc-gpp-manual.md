## Summary

The binding South African standard for dispensing. SAPC's GPP rules (made under
s35A of the Pharmacy Act 53 of 1974) set minimum standards for premises,
personnel and the **dispensing process** that all licensed pharmacies and
pharmacists must meet. It localises the FIP/WHO GPP standard for SA and is the
applicable legal floor for any Dis-Chem dispensing workflow. pharmcouncil.co.za

## Key claims

- The **dispensing process** under GPP Rule 2.7 is structured into phases:
  **(1) interpretation and evaluation of the prescription; (2) preparation and
  labelling of the prescribed medicine; (3) provision of information and
  instructions to the patient** (supply with counselling).
- Dispensing of a **Schedule 2–6 substance** may only be performed **by a
  pharmacist, or a pharmacist intern / pharmacist's assistant acting under the
  personal supervision of a pharmacist**.
- Pharmacists must adhere to SAPC's GPP standards as a condition of licensure;
  the **responsible pharmacist** and pharmacy owner are accountable for meeting
  GPP standards (enforced via inspections).
- Amendments include **BN172 of 2019 (Unit Dose Dispensing)** and **BN84 of 2018
  (minimum standards for premises, facilities and equipment)** — i.e. SA GPP
  explicitly contemplates unit-dose/adherence dispensing.
- GPP covers records, labelling content, premises, cold storage and security of
  scheduled substances.

## Verbatim excerpts

> "The dispensing process consists of … interpretation and evaluation of the
> prescription; the preparation and labelling of the prescribed medicine; and
> the provision of information and instructions to the patient."

> "Any Schedule 2, Schedule 3, Schedule 4, Schedule 5 or Schedule 6 substance
> shall not be sold by any person except by a pharmacist, pharmacist intern or a
> pharmacist's assistant acting under the personal supervision of a pharmacist."
