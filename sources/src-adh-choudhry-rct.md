# Summary
The honest evidence base for multi-dose/reminder packaging and synchronisation:
real but modest gains, highly heterogeneous, and weakest as standalone tech.
Complements [[src-disp-conn-packaging-meta]]. PubMed/JAMA 403'd; from search extracts.

## Key claims
- **Cochrane (Mahtani, CD005025, 2011):** reminder packaging ↑ ~**11%** of pills taken (95% CI 6–17) but **I²≈96%** (huge heterogeneity); "adherent patient" proportion **not significant** (OR 0.89).
- **Medication synchronisation:** small PDC gains (~**+3pp**, Health Affairs 2017), **concentrated in low-baseline-adherence patients** (~3× larger); MA study +1.6/4.8/2.9% by class (JAPhA 2019).
- **Choudhry et al., JAMA Intern Med 2017:** pillbox/digital-timer-cap reminder devices showed **NO** adherence improvement over 12 months — the key disconfirming RCT.
- **Healthy-adherer bias:** Coronary Drug Project — good adherers to *placebo* had 15% vs 28% 5-yr mortality ⇒ adherence correlates with unmeasured healthy behaviour, inflating observational outcome claims.
- Net: packaging/sync ≈ +8pp adherence / +3pp PDC, best for low-baseline polypharmacy + pharmacy-delivered + contact-rich; **not** a standalone fix.
