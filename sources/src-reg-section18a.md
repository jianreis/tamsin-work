# Summary
The legal basis that a flat-fee / discounted-medicine subscription (RxPass-style)
**cannot** lawfully exist in SA. Mondaq 403'd; §18A wording from search extract +
corroborated by the vault's SEP page (Tier A).

## Key claims
- **§18A, Medicines Act 101/1965:** prohibits supplying medicine "according to a **bonus system, rebate system or any other incentive scheme**." A $5-unlimited-generics subscription is an incentive scheme over the medicine.
- **§22G + Pricing Regulations (Reg 6):** a **Single Exit Price** applies; **selling below SEP / offering discounts, rebates or bonuses to non-State purchasers is prohibited**. See [[sep-dispensing-fee]] (Tier A).
- The **dispensing fee is regulated (banded), not free margin** — a subscription cannot bundle below the gazetted fee.
- ⇒ **RxPass does not transfer to SA** without reform (`🔮 requires-reform`). A *lawful* "subscription" could only charge for a **service** (packing/delivery/reminders), leaving the medicine at SEP + capped fee — **untested**, see [[open-questions]].

## Verbatim excerpts
> §18A: medicines may not be supplied "according to a bonus system, rebate system or any other incentive scheme." (as relayed via legal commentary)
