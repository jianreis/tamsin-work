## Summary

Covers point-of-care and pickup automation. **Automated dispensing cabinets (ADCs)**
are computerised cabinets that store and dispense medication near the point of care
while controlling and tracking distribution (mainly inpatient). **Smart lockers**
provide secure, contactless prescription pickup and reduce hand-off errors.
**Computer-vision verification** checks medication appearance pre-dispense, with
barcode scanning confirming right-drug/right-patient; one figure cites up to ~73%
of hospitals using verification tooling that enables remote oversight and low-risk
auto-approval while routing high-alert meds for manual approval. Market context:
global pharmacy automation valued ~**USD 6.31bn (2024)**, projected ~**USD 19.35bn
by 2035** (~10.75% CAGR). Reliability mixed (peer-reviewed usability study A/B;
market sizing C-grade vendor/analyst) — treat market numbers as directional.

## Key claims
- **ADCs** = computerised cabinets storing/dispensing meds near point of care with
  tracking/control (primarily hospital/inpatient).
- **Smart lockers** enable secure, contactless pickup; reduce pickup/hand-off errors.
- **Computer-vision verification** confirms medication appearance pre-dispense;
  barcode confirms right-drug/right-patient.
- ~**73%** of hospitals reported using verification tools enabling remote oversight,
  low-risk auto-approval, manual routing for high-alert meds. *(directional)*
- Global pharmacy automation market ~**USD 6.31bn (2024)** → ~**USD 19.35bn by
  2035**, ~10.75% CAGR. *(market estimate — Tier C, directional)*
