# Summary

McKinsey article arguing pharmacy is shifting **into the home** via delivery and
medication synchronisation, and that the value is not only fulfilment but
**clinical support** — medication review/reconciliation, adherence counselling,
limited prescribing, and disease management — delivered remotely. Reliability
**B** (insight piece). ⚠️ figures unverified (WebFetch 403).

# Key claims
- Home delivery + **medication synchronisation** (pharmacist coordination of
  refills onto one schedule) can **improve adherence** in defined use cases.
- A core consumer segment "want[s] to spend as little time and energy as possible
  interacting with their pharmacy" — young professionals, busy parents, active
  retirees — i.e. a **convenience-first** segment.
- Many patients still **rely on pharmacist clinical support**: medication review &
  reconciliation, adherence counselling, limited prescribing, disease management —
  so home models must carry the clinical layer, not just the box.
- Strategic read: the "home" channel is an adherence and relationship play, not
  merely logistics.
