# Summary

Cluster of McKinsey insight pieces on pharmacy-economics reinvention: the shift to
**cost-based / cost-plus reimbursement**, automation freeing pharmacists for
clinical work, and untapped clinical-service revenue for health-system pharmacies.
Less about the patient scripting journey, more about the **operating/economic
model** behind it. Reliability **B**; figures ⚠️ UNVERIFIED (403).

# Key claims
- "The revival of pharmacy: the rise of cost-based reimbursement" — US pharmacy
  reimbursement is moving toward **cost-plus** transparency (echoing Mark Cuban
  Cost Plus / chain cost-plus launches), pressuring the old spread-pricing model.
- Pharmacists spend disproportionate time on **dispensing mechanics** vs operating
  "at the top of their licence"; **automation** is the lever to reallocate time to
  clinical services (counselling, point-of-care testing, chronic management).
- "Untapped opportunities for health-system pharmacies" — significant unrealised
  value in specialty, ambulatory, and adherence services.
