## Summary

PMBs are the legal floor of cover every registered scheme must fund regardless
of plan/option, defined under the Medical Schemes Act 131 of 1998 and its
regulations. They fall into three buckets: emergency conditions, ~270 Diagnosis
Treatment Pairs (DTPs), and the 26-condition Chronic Disease List (CDL).
Chronic conditions must be registered by the member (usually via doctor or
pharmacy) on a chronic-management programme to be funded. CMS site returned 403
to automated fetch on 2026-06-13; claims drawn from CMS-sourced search extract
and corroborating scheme PMB guides.

## Key claims

- PMBs are a set of defined benefits **all medical schemes must cover**,
  regardless of the option/plan a member is on, per the **Medical Schemes Act
  131 of 1998**.
- Three categories: (1) **emergency medical conditions**; (2) a defined list of
  **~270/271 conditions** specified as **Diagnosis Treatment Pairs (DTPs)**;
  (3) the **26 chronic conditions** on the **Chronic Disease List (CDL)**.
- CDL conditions include asthma, diabetes mellitus type 1 & 2, epilepsy,
  hypertension, chronic renal disease, coronary artery disease, glaucoma,
  hypothyroidism, HIV, etc.
- For CDL conditions, schemes must cover **medication + doctor consultations +
  certain related tests**.
- **Every scheme member automatically qualifies for PMB cover**, but each
  chronic condition must be **registered separately** (once) — the **treating
  doctor or pharmacy contacts the scheme** to register the member on the chronic
  management programme.
- **Treatment algorithms** for each CDL condition are published in the
  Government Gazette; DTP-section chronic diseases follow public-sector protocols.
- To benefit, the condition must be a defined PMB condition AND the treatment
  must match the defined/approved benefit (formulary + algorithm).
