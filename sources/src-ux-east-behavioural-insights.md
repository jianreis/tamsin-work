# Summary

BIT's "EAST" framework — the most widely used practitioner checklist for applying
behavioural science, distilled from hundreds of randomised controlled trials.
To encourage a behaviour, make it **Easy, Attractive, Social, Timely**. Designed
as a simpler successor to MINDSPACE (2010); revised 2024. Used as the practical
"how to nudge" layer in the vault, complementing COM-B's diagnostic layer.

# Key claims
- **Easy** — (1) harness the power of **defaults** (people tend to go with
  pre-set options); (2) **reduce the "hassle factor" / friction** of taking up a
  service (remove steps, pre-fill, reduce effort); (3) **simplify messages** —
  make the message and required action clear and short. Effort is a powerful
  deterrent; small frictions cause large drop-off.
- **Attractive** — (1) **attract attention** (personalisation, salience, images,
  colour); (2) **design rewards/incentives** effectively (framing, lotteries).
- **Social** — (1) show that **most people perform the desired behaviour**
  (social norms); (2) use the power of **networks**; (3) encourage **commitments**
  to others (commitment devices increase follow-through).
- **Timely** — (1) prompt people **when they are most receptive**; (2) consider
  **immediate vs delayed** costs/benefits (present bias); (3) help people plan
  their response to events — i.e. **implementation intentions** ("if-then"
  plans), which reliably raise follow-through.
- EAST is a checklist, not a theory: pair it with a **behavioural diagnosis**
  (e.g. COM-B) so the chosen tactic addresses the real barrier.
- The "**friction with bad intent**" inverse — **sludge** — should be removed;
  good design strips needless steps from valuable actions.
