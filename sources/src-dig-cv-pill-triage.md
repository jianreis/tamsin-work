## Summary

Performance evidence for two AI capabilities relevant to scripting. (1)
**Computer-vision pill recognition / verification**: deep-learning models report
high lab accuracy (e.g. precision 0.998 / recall 1.000 / combined 0.991 for 20
pill types; ~97.8% detection in another framework; ~98.7% precision in a real-time
inspection model) and can flag look-alike blister/pack errors, with multicenter
real-world studies showing code-free DL transferring across platforms. (2)
**Conversational AI symptom triage**: a UCSD protocol-grounded tool selected the
correct medical flowchart ~**84%** of the time and followed decision steps with
>99% accuracy across varied phrasings. Caveat: high in-distribution accuracy does
not guarantee safety on edge cases / unseen drugs or symptoms; clinical deployment
needs human verification and monitoring.

## Key claims
- DL pill-recognition lab metrics: precision 0.998 / recall 1.000 / combined 0.991
  (20 types); ~97.8% detection (another framework); ~98.7% precision (real-time).
- CV can flag look-alike blister/pack dispensing errors; integrable into
  prescription/dispensing systems.
- Multicenter real-world study: code-free DL pill recognition transfers across
  platforms.
- Conversational AI triage: correct flowchart selection ~**84%**; decision-step
  adherence **>99%** across varied phrasings.
- High in-distribution accuracy ≠ safety on unseen items; needs human verification.
