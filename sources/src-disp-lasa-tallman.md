## Summary

Synthesis of LASA error prevalence and the evidence-based countermeasures —
Tall Man lettering, physical separation, barcode verification and indication
prompts. Corroborated by an NCBI peer-reviewed review of LASA prevention
(PMC6988319) and ISMP/FDA recommendations. Direct fetch returned HTTP 403 on
2026-06-13; claims drawn from search extracts of these sources.

## Key claims

- LASA medicines are confused because of **visually similar packaging/dosage
  forms and phonetically similar names**.
- LASA confusions account for an estimated **6.2%–14.7% of all medication
  errors** (some estimates up to ~25%).
- **Tall Man lettering** uses selective capitalisation to emphasise the
  difference between similar names (e.g. **hydrALAZINE vs hydrOXYzine**,
  **predniSONE vs prednisoLONE**); recommended by both **ISMP and FDA**.
- Effective prevention is **system-level**: Tall Man lettering, dual
  brand/generic display, **storing LASA pairs in separate locations / labelling
  bins**, **barcode verification from stocking through dispensing**, and
  **decision support with indication checks and confirmation prompts**.
- Including the **reason for use / indication** on the prescription significantly
  reduces the risk of a drug name being misheard or misread.
