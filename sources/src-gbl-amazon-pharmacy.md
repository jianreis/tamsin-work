# Summary

Amazon Pharmacy's prescription model: insurance/price-comparison at checkout, the
RxPass flat-fee generics subscription, PillPack pre-sorted dose packaging, and an
aggressive same-day delivery build-out. Company-published → reliability B
(mechanics solid; growth framing is promotional). Scale numbers are
Amazon-reported.

# Key claims
- **RxPass** (launched Jan 2023): flat **US$5/month for Amazon Prime members**
  giving **unlimited fills of ~60 eligible generic medications** (covering 80+
  common conditions), free delivery, 24/7 pharmacist access. **Does not use
  insurance.** Expanded to **Prime members on Medicare** in 2024 (**50M+**
  beneficiaries eligible).
- Amazon Pharmacy can run a script through insurance at checkout and **display
  the price**, auto-comparing cash/coupon options for transparency.
- **Same-day delivery** uses mode-appropriate logistics (e-bikes in dense cities,
  EVs in suburbs, a drone pilot from Oct 2023 in College Station TX). Announced
  (2026-02-11) expansion to **nearly 4,500 US cities/towns by end of 2026**.
- **PillPack** (acquired 2018): medications **pre-sorted into individual dose
  packets labelled by date and time**, delivered by mail — built for patients on
  multiple daily medications.
