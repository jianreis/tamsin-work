## Summary

Definitions and mechanics of the steering tools schemes use: Designated Service
Providers (DSPs), medicine formularies, reference pricing (MMAP — Maximum
Medical Aid Price), and co-payments/levies. These determine where a member can
get medicine dispensed without penalty and which products are reimbursed in
full vs subject to a co-payment.

## Key claims

- **DSP** = a provider (pharmacy, doctor, hospital) the scheme selects/contracts
  to deliver services to members; using a non-DSP can trigger a **co-payment**.
- **Formulary** = the scheme's list of reimbursed medicines (generics + some
  originals); a **preferred list**. Medicine **not on the formulary is subject to
  a co-payment**.
- **MMAP (Maximum Medical Aid Price)** = a reference-price model setting the max
  the scheme reimburses for a medicine; applies to **chronic and acute**.
- **Reference pricing** groups generically/therapeutically equivalent medicines
  and sets a maximum reimbursed price for the group; members pay the difference
  if they choose a more expensive product.
- Co-payment can be a **percentage** or the **difference between the DSP tariff
  and the provider's charge**.

## Verbatim excerpts

> "A DSP is a healthcare provider selected by the medical scheme to provide
> healthcare services to its members, which may apply to pharmacies, doctors and
> hospitals."

> "MMAP is a reference price model that serves as a guide to determine the
> maximum medical aid price that medical schemes will reimburse for medication.
> MMAP applies to chronic and acute medication."

> "if you choose not to use the DSP… you may have to pay a portion of the bill as
> a co-payment, which could either be a percentage co-payment or the difference
> between the DSP's tariff and that charged by the provider you went to."
