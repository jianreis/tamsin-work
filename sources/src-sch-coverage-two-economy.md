## Summary

Triangulated statistics on the insured/uninsured "two-economy" split in South
African healthcare. Roughly one in six South Africans is on a medical scheme;
the rest depend on the public sector or pay out of pocket. Private spend serves
a minority while the public sector carries the majority disease burden — the
central structural tension behind NHI and any retail-pharmacy strategy.

Coverage percentage and absolute beneficiary count come from different bases
(Stats SA GHS survey self-report vs CMS registered beneficiaries vs total
population) so figures vary; cross-check with [[src-sch-cms-industry-report-2024]].

## Key claims

- Approximately **16% of the population** is covered by medical schemes; as of
  **2022, 15.8%** of individuals were medical-aid members (Stats SA / Statista).
- The **majority (~84%)** rely on **public** health facilities.
- Of a population of 54m+, only ~17.4% have a scheme → **~9.5m insured, 44m+
  not** (figures vary by source/year).
- South Africa spends **~8.5% of GDP** on health, **roughly half in the private
  sector** serving the wealthier minority; the public sector serves the majority.
- An estimated **~28%** access private primary care, with low-income uninsured
  often **paying out of pocket** for GP/pharmacy visits.
