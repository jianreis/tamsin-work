# Summary

Industry signal that **WhatsApp is the de-facto conversational-commerce channel
in South Africa** — used for discovery, selection, payment, and service — and
that solutions are explicitly engineered for **local languages** and
**load-shedding resilience**. Supports a WhatsApp-first scripting channel
strategy. Treated as Tier-C/vendor colour; scale claims corroborate the Tier-B
MomConnect/Freedom House sources.

# Key claims
- Conversational commerce (buying/selling/servicing through chat) is growing fast
  in SA, with WhatsApp the dominant channel; Juniper projected global
  conversational-commerce spend **>US$290bn by 2025**.
- Real SA deployments complete **end-to-end transactions in chat** — e.g.
  Prepaid24 + Chat Inc + Ozow let users buy prepaid electricity and essentials
  and **pay inside WhatsApp**.
- **Language:** effective SA chatbots greet and serve customers in **local
  languages** (isiZulu, Afrikaans, etc.); WhatsApp Business API supports **all 11
  official languages**.
- **Load-shedding:** vendors market WhatsApp/mobile-optimised architectures that
  keep working **during power outages**, framed around Eskom load-shedding — a
  recognised, designed-for SA constraint.
- Implication: a WhatsApp-based scripting flow (capture → confirm → pay → track)
  is **culturally and technically native** to the SA market and resilient to the
  local connectivity/power reality.
