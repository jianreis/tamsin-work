# Summary

Dis-Chem's audited results for FY2026 (year ended 28 February 2026), released
via SENS on 29 May 2026. The primary disclosure for current company scale,
revenue mix, store/pharmacy footprint and dividend. Headline earnings fell
year-on-year because of heavy investment in the "healthcare ecosystem"
(insurance, loyalty revamp), but underlying retail performance and market share
improved. Figures corroborated by FY2026 trade-press coverage
([[src-dc-fy2026-supermarket]], [[src-dc-tlc-500-franchise]]).

# Key claims
- Group revenue +9.3% to **R42.8 billion** for the 12 months to 28 Feb 2026.
- Basic EPS 114.2c and HEPS 113.7c — down 17.1% / 17.4% YoY (driven by
  ecosystem investment + non-recurring items).
- Excluding ecosystem investment and non-recurring items, Group profit before
  tax was up ~20.1%.
- Footprint at year-end: **316 retail pharmacy stores** and 42 retail baby
  (Baby City) stores. Net change: 31 retail pharmacy stores opened/acquired;
  3 Baby City stores closed.
- External wholesale revenue +11.3%, supported by 16.4% growth from the
  expanding TLC (The Local Choice) franchise base.
- Total dividend declared 45.34c/share (vs 54.83c prior; -17.3%). Final gross
  cash dividend 15.91872c/share. Dividend policy: 40% of headline earnings.
- Strategy framed around a long-term integrated "healthcare ecosystem"
  (pharmacy + clinics + insurance + loyalty).
