## Summary

Stokvels are rotating savings/credit clubs — a deeply embedded SA savings and
mutual-insurance behaviour, often used for groceries, funerals, school fees, and
lump-sum purchases. Relevant to how low-income households save for, and pool
money toward, predictable expenses (incl. health/medicine costs).

## Key claims

- **~11.5 million South Africans** belong to **~800,000 stokvels** (~half of
  adults are in at least one).
- Average stokvel has **~34 members**; market estimated **~R45 billion**;
  South Africans save **~R50 billion/year** via stokvels.
- Used for life essentials (school fees, **funeral cover**, groceries), home
  deposits, emergency credit, and small-business capital — a **trusted,
  community-governed** alternative/complement to formal finance.
