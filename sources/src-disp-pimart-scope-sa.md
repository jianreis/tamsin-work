## Summary

Tracks South Africa's most significant recent expansion of pharmacist scope:
PIMART (Pharmacist-Initiated Management of Antiretroviral Therapy). Combines
regulatory primary material (Board Notice 101 of 2021; SCA judgment ZASCA
148/2025) with current-status reporting. Shows both the direction of travel
(pharmacists prescribing) and the SA legal friction around it. Court ruling and
academic journal are Tier A within; this composite note is B because status is
news-reported. Direct fetch returned HTTP 403 on 2026-06-13; claims drawn from
search extracts of these sources.

## Key claims

- **PIMART** allows trained, permitted pharmacists to **assess patients, conduct
  HIV testing, and prescribe + dispense ART** for **PrEP, PEP and (where
  appropriate) first-line ARV therapy** per NDoH guidelines — a form of
  **task-shifting**.
- Established by SAPC **Board Notice 101 of 2021**, which set the scope,
  competency standards and PIMART-course accreditation criteria.
- A specialised **supplementary qualification (PCDT — Primary Care Drug Therapy,
  s22A(15) permit)** lets pharmacists diagnose and prescribe from an approved
  list and offer an advanced service package: **minor ailments, immunisation,
  family planning and wellness screening**.
- On **9 October 2025 the Supreme Court of Appeal** (IPA Foundation v SAPC,
  ZASCA 148/2025) **dismissed the appeal by a private-doctor group** seeking to
  block PIMART.
- As of **June 2026, no pharmacists were yet permitted to provide PIMART
  services** in practice — implementation lagged the legal green light.
- SA community pharmacies already provide PHC services for those who can pay:
  **screening tests, family planning, emergency care for minor ailments**.
