## Summary

The reference list of high-alert medications for community/ambulatory pharmacy —
drugs that carry a heightened risk of significant patient harm when used in
error and that warrant extra safeguards (independent double-checks, auto-
verification limits, enhanced counselling). Directly relevant to a retail
pharmacy. Direct fetch returned HTTP 403 on 2026-06-13; claims drawn from ISMP
and Pharmacy Times search extracts.

## Key claims

- **High-alert medications** are those bearing a **heightened risk of causing
  significant harm when used in error**; the consequences of error are
  disproportionately severe even if errors are not more frequent.
- In community/ambulatory settings the most frequently cited high-alert classes
  include **insulin and oral hypoglycaemics, anticoagulants (e.g. warfarin,
  DOACs), opioids, chemotherapy/immunosuppressants, and paediatric liquid
  medications**.
- Insulin, oral hypoglycaemics and opioids are considered **high-alert in all
  environments**.
- The IHI 5 Million Lives Campaign specifically targets error reduction for
  **anticoagulants, insulin and opioids**.
- The list is used to determine which medicines **require special safeguards**
  (e.g. independent double-check, standardised processes, patient education).
