## Summary

Sets out the SA legal basis for electronic prescriptions. Primary blog/SciELO
pages returned HTTP 403 on 2026-06-13; claims drawn from corroborated search
extracts across EMGuidance, SAPC Pharmaciae, the SA Pharmaceutical Journal and the
SciELO SAPJ article on e-script implementation. **Regulation 33** of the General
Regulations (2017) under the **Medicines and Related Substances Act 101 of 1965**
permits electronic signatures and electronic transmission of prescriptions. A
valid e-prescription is generated by an "electronic agent" (per ECTA) and signed
with an **Advanced Electronic Signature (AES)** as defined in the Electronic
Communications and Transactions Act 25 of 2002 (s13); an AES has the same legal
standing as a wet-ink signature. Critically, a photographed/emailed paper script
is **not** an electronic prescription. Authentication responsibility rests on the
**dispensing pharmacist**; SAPC advises accepting e-scripts sent directly from the
authorised prescriber to the pharmacy. ⚠️ Whether higher-schedule (S5/S6)
controlled substances may be e-prescribed in routine community practice is
contested and needs confirmation against the current Gazette/SAPC rules.

## Key claims
- **Regulation 33** (General Regulations, 2017) under the **Medicines Act 101 of
  1965** allows electronic signatures and electronic transmission of prescriptions.
- Two valid formats: paper (wet-ink signature) and **electronic** (generated by an
  ECTA "electronic agent", signed with an **AES** per ECTA 2002 s13).
- An **AES** has the same legal standing as a handwritten signature in SA law.
- A scanned/photographed/emailed copy of a paper script is **not** an e-prescription
  — it is a transmitted image of a paper script (different evidentiary status).
- Authentication of the prescription rests on the **pharmacist**; SAPC advises
  pharmacists, to avoid "dispensing without a valid prescription", to accept
  e-scripts sent directly by authorised prescribers to the pharmacy.
- ⚠️ UNVERIFIED: routine e-prescribing of S5/S6 controlled substances in community
  practice — scope/limits to be confirmed against current SAPC/Gazette rules.
