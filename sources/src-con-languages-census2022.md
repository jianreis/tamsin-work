## Summary

Census 2022 home-language distribution. Establishes the multilingual reality:
no single language is spoken at home by even a quarter of households, and English
— the usual default for apps and labels — is a home language for under 1 in 10.
Note: SA now has **12 official languages** (South African Sign Language added in
2023), commonly referred to historically as "11 official languages".

## Key claims

- SA recognises **12 official languages** (was 11; SA Sign Language added 2023).
- Home-language shares (Census 2022): **isiZulu 24.4%**, **isiXhosa 16.3%**,
  **Afrikaans 10.6%**, **English 8.7%**, Sesotho 7.8%, Setswana, Sepedi, Xitsonga
  4.7%, siSwati 2.8%, Tshivenda 2.5%, isiNdebele 1.7%.
- **English is a home language for only ~8.7%** yet dominates digital/written
  interfaces — a literacy/comprehension gap for health information.
- isiZulu has been the most-spoken home language continuously 1996–2022.

## Verbatim excerpts
