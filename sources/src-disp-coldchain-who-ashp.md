## Summary

Defines cold-chain requirements for vaccines and refrigerated medicines: the
2–8°C window, continuous monitoring, equipment and procedures, plus the
consequences of excursions. Relevant to pharmacy fridge lines, vaccines and
biologics (e.g. insulin, GLP-1 agonists). Direct fetch returned HTTP 403 on
2026-06-13; claims drawn from WHO/ASHP/CDC search extracts.

## Key claims

- WHO guidance requires vaccines to be stored at **+2°C to +8°C throughout the
  supply chain**; this preserves potency from manufacture to administration.
- **Temperatures below 0°C freeze and irreversibly damage** many vaccines;
  **temperatures above 8°C accelerate degradation** — both directions matter.
- **Continuous temperature monitoring** is required at every level (national,
  regional, facility, transport); temperatures should be **recorded near the
  product several times per day** (with data-logging recommended).
- Core elements: **trained handlers, validated storage equipment, defined
  procedures, backup power, insulated packaging and temperature monitors**.
- Cold-chain failure is a **patient-safety and stock-loss risk**; excursion
  management requires documented procedures and quarantine of affected stock.
