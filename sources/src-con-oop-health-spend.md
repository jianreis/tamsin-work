## Summary

Evidence on what uninsured/low-income households actually spend out of pocket on
health, and what drives "distressed" or catastrophic spending. Medicines and
health products are the single most-used paid health service — so price
transparency and the avoided cost of travel matter directly to the scripting UX.

## Key claims

- **Medicines and health products are the most-used paid health service**;
  households spent **~R11.2 billion on medicines** (Stats SA IES); of that **~R5.0bn
  on prescribed** and **~R4.5bn on non-prescription** products.
- Total OOP **~R31.5 billion**, averaging **~R1,481/household** (~1% of total hh
  expenditure on average — but highly skewed toward poorer/sicker households).
- OOP includes **travel cost and distance to facilities, registration, and
  consultation** — these stack into distressed/catastrophic financing for the poor.
- Implication: removing **trips, queues, and surprise costs** is a direct
  financial benefit to the uninsured, not just convenience.
