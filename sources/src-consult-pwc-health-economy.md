# Summary

PwC's pharmacy-relevant work is **macro/economic**, not a scripting-journey design:
the **Health Research Institute** publishes the annual **Medical Cost Trend**, the
**"New Health Economy" / Future of Health** series (pharma evolving from "medicine
makers to lifespan partners"), and a **Health Consumer Survey**. Honest note: the
widely-cited consumer "**Pharmacy Next**" survey is **Wolters Kluwer**, not PwC.
Reliability **B**. Figures ⚠️ UNVERIFIED (403).

# Key claims
- PwC HRI **Medical Cost Trend** (annual) tracks the cost drivers behind rising US
  health/pharmacy spend — context, not journey design.
- PwC **Future of Health / New Health Economy**: pharma firms evolve "from medicine
  makers to **lifespan partners**, using AI, genomics, and real-world evidence to
  predict, prevent, and personalise care."
- PwC notes **all major PBMs and pharmacy chains launched cost-plus models** — the
  transparency/cost-plus shift (corroborates McKinsey/Oliver Wyman).
- PwC **Health Consumer Survey (2025)** covers consumer expectations of convenience,
  transparency, and digital tools.
- **Honest gap:** no PwC report found on the **prescription/scripting journey**
  itself; PwC's strength is health economics and pharma strategy, not pharmacy CX.
