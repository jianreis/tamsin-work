# Transparent Pricing Regulations (Single Exit Price)

## Summary
Regulations under §22G establishing the Single Exit Price (SEP) system, the
logistics fee, and the regulated dispensing fee for pharmacists and dispensing
doctors. The Pricing Committee advises the Minister; SEP is adjusted annually
(SEPA). Dispensing fees are banded by SEP value.

> Note: PDF returned 403; figures triangulated across the Pricing Regulations and
> the 2026 Daily Maverick/Spotlight analysis by Dr Andy Gray (Tier B).

## Key claims
- SEP is the single, only price at which manufacturers/importers may sell to anyone
  other than the State; it must be published and may rise only via the annual SEPA.
- Logistics fee (incl. VAT) is paid by the manufacturer to the wholesaler for
  logistical services; wholesalers may not add a separate mark-up to SEP.
- Dispensing fee = flat amount + % of SEP, in four price bands (% falls as price
  rises). Indicative maxima (ex-VAT) from current regulations:
  - SEP < R118.80: R15.80 + 46.0% of SEP
  - SEP < R315.53: R30.24 + 33.0% of SEP
  - SEP < R1,104.40: R86.11 + 15.0% of SEP
  - otherwise: R198.36 + 5.0% of SEP
- SEPA 2026 set at a maximum 1.47% (vs 5.25% in 2025) — annual mechanism that has
  shielded SA from large price increases.

## Verbatim excerpts
- §22G: a SEP "shall be the only price at which manufacturers shall sell medicines
  and Scheduled substances to any person other than the State."
- "Where the SEP is less than R118.80, the maximum dispensing fee is R15.80 + 46.0%
  of the SEP."
- "The SEPA allowed for 2026 was set at a maximum of 1.47%, compared with 5.25% in 2025."
