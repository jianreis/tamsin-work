## Summary

Triangulated evidence that South Africa is the world's most unequal major economy
by income. Establishes the structural backdrop for the "two-economy" framing:
extreme dispersion of household income, a small affluent (largely insured) tier
and a large low-income (uninsured, cash-constrained) majority. Stats SA figures
are Tier A; the synthesis around them is B.

## Key claims

- SA reports one of the **highest Gini coefficients in the world** (~0.63–0.67
  depending on measure/year; Stats SA per-capita expenditure Gini 0.67 in 2006,
  0.65 in 2015; forecast ~0.63 for 2025). World Bank routinely ranks SA the most
  unequal of measured countries.
- The **labour market is the largest driver (~74%)** of income inequality.
- Income is highly racialised: white-headed households had the **highest average
  annual income** (Stats SA Income & Expenditure Survey 2022/23, ~20,000 hh).
- Implication: pricing, payment, and delivery design must serve **two very
  different wallets** simultaneously.
