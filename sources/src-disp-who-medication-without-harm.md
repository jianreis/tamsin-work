## Summary

WHO's Third Global Patient Safety Challenge, launched in 2017, set the goal of
reducing severe avoidable medication-related harm by 50% globally over five
years. It frames medication safety around priority areas and domains of action
and is the authoritative global anchor for dispensing-error prevention. WHO
domains returned HTTP 403 to automated fetch on 2026-06-13; claims drawn from
WHO initiative-page and IRIS search extracts, the Lancet launch article, and
peer-reviewed commentary retrieved the same day.

## Key claims

- Launched **2017**; goal to reduce **severe avoidable medication-related harm
  by 50%** globally within five years.
- Unsafe medication practices and medication errors are **a leading cause of
  avoidable harm** in health care worldwide; the **global cost of medication
  errors is estimated at US$42 billion annually**.
- Medication harm accounts for **around 50% of the overall preventable harm** in
  medical care.
- Three **flagship priority areas** ("early priorities"): **high-risk
  situations**, **polypharmacy**, and **transitions of care**.
- The challenge addresses four domains: **patients and the public; health-care
  professionals; medicines; and systems and practices of medication**.
- **Look-alike, sound-alike (LASA)** medicines are named among the key
  contributors to unsafe medication practice.
- Follow-up resource "Medication safety in high-risk situations" details
  **high-alert medications**, provider/patient factors and system factors.
