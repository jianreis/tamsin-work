# Summary

Europe's two largest online/mail-order pharmacies and the **CardLink** mechanism
that lets them redeem German e-prescriptions remotely (NFC health card + phone,
no PIN). Shows how a national e-prescription rail unlocks scaled mail-order Rx —
and the strategic prize for whoever owns the digital redemption flow. Company
investor releases → B (scale figures company-reported).

# Key claims
- **Redcare Pharmacy** = renamed **Shop Apotheke Europe** (2023); Europe's largest
  online pharmacy by revenue, ~7 countries. **2024 revenue €2.37bn (+32%)**;
  active customers grew to **12.5m in 2024**, **>13.7m by Q3 2025**. 2025 guidance:
  Rx expected to roughly **double**.
- **DocMorris** (formerly Zur Rose Group; NL-based, serves Germany cross-border).
  Active customers **9.1m (end 2023) → 10.3m (end 2024)**; 2024 revenue
  **CHF 1,085m**; reported a **fivefold rise in new Rx customers** since CardLink.
- **CardLink mechanism (live in apps from April/May 2024):** patient holds their
  **NFC electronic health card (eGK) to the smartphone** within the pharmacy's
  app; the app reads the card (Card Access Number + SMS verification, **no PIN**)
  and pulls the e-prescription from the Telematics Infrastructure for remote
  mail-order fulfilment. gematik extended both firms' CardLink approval **to 31
  January 2027** (bridge to next-gen "PoPP").
- EU context: cross-border mail-order built on EU free-movement law; German
  fixed-price/bonus rules repeatedly litigated (ECJ 2016; VOASG 2020; ECJ Feb
  2025 upheld the ban on Rx-drug discount promotions).
