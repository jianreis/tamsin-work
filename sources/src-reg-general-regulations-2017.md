# General Regulations to Act 101 of 1965 — Regulation 33 (prescriptions)

## Summary
The General Regulations operationalise the Act. Regulation 33 (renumbered from the
older Reg 28; current form effective 2017) sets out what a valid prescription must
contain, validity periods, repeat rules per schedule, and — importantly —
recognises electronic prescriptions signed with an advanced electronic signature.

## Key claims
- Reg 33 defines two valid prescription formats: (a) paper — handwritten/printed
  and signed with a wet-ink signature; (b) electronic — generated by an "electronic
  agent" (as defined in ECTA) and signed with an Advanced Electronic Signature (AES).
- Required content: prescriber name, qualification, registration number and
  address; patient name, identification and address; date; medicine name, dose,
  strength, dosage form, quantity, directions; and number of repeats.
- Validity / repeats: a repeat prescription may not be repeated for longer than
  six months for S2, S3, S4 or S5. For S4 the prescriber must indicate the number
  of times it may be dispensed. S6 may not be repeated and is limited to 30 days.
- Verbal/telephonic prescription: a signed prescription must be obtained within
  7 days; for any prescription transmitted by means other than an electronic agent
  (fax) or communicated verbally, a permanent copy must be made for records.
- The pharmacist must verify the authenticity of the prescription regardless of
  format.

## Verbatim excerpts
- "A practitioner may provide a patient with a repeat prescription, which
  prescription shall not be repeated for longer than six months, in the case of
  Schedule 2, Schedule 3, Schedule 4 or Schedule 5 medicines."
- "In the case of a verbal prescription, a signed prescription must be obtained
  within 7 days."
- For S4: "the prescription may be repeated if the person who issued the
  prescription has indicated thereon the number of times it may be dispensed."
