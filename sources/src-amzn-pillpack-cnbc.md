# Summary
Triangulated account of PillPack's model, the 2018 Amazon acquisition, and the
2019 Surescripts data-access fight. Direct fetch returned HTTP 403; claims drawn
from search extracts of CNBC + corroborating press.

## Key claims
- PillPack founded Feb 2013 (TJ Parker, pharmacist; Elliot Cohen, MIT); Techstars + IDEO prototype.
- Amazon acquired PillPack **June 2018**; **US$753M "net of cash acquired" per Amazon's FY2018 10-K** — corrects earlier "~$1B" reports. Walmart reportedly bid ~$700M (⚠️ single-source).
- Model: mail pharmacy sorting meds into **per-dose sachets labelled by date/time**, auto-refill, 24/7 pharmacist; **PharmacyOS** automates refills, PBM copay adjudication, interaction checks, fill-cycle sync.
- Licensed **all 50 states + DC**, NABP-accredited, robotic central-fill (NH/AZ/FL/TX/NY circa 2019–20).
- **2016:** Express Scripts threatened to drop PillPack. **2019:** Surescripts (routes ~80% of US e-Rx) cut off med-history access via vendor ReMyHealth, alleged misuse, referred to FBI; Amazon threatened suit.
- Founders left Amazon Sept 2022; launched General Medicine (2025).
