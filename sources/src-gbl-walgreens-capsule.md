# Summary

Two cautionary tales bounding the optimism elsewhere. Walgreens' med-sync
("Save a Trip Refills") and barcode-scan refill are good UX, but the company's
financial collapse and 2025 take-private show scale + discounting do not
guarantee a durable pharmacy business. Capsule's same-day free-courier model is
excellent UX but proved economically fragile. Reputable press + SEC → B.

# Key claims
- **Walgreens UX (borrowable):** app **barcode scan to refill**; **"Save a Trip
  Refills"** medication synchronisation aligns all eligible maintenance meds to one
  pickup/delivery date; Rx by mail and same-day delivery offered.
- **Walgreens decline:** announced ~**1,200 store closures** over three years (Oct
  2024); large FY2024 loss driven by a VillageMD goodwill impairment; **went private
  via Sycamore Partners** in a ~**US$10bn** equity deal (announced 6 Mar 2025,
  closed ~Aug 2025), shareholders paid **$11.45/share + a contingent value right**,
  splitting the retailer into separate businesses — ending ~a century as a public
  company.
- **Capsule (NYC, 2016):** phone-first pharmacy — e-prescribed → filled →
  **same-day free hand-delivery by courier**, **text-based pharmacist chat**,
  insurance handled in background. **Cut ~13% of staff (Oct 2023)** amid retreat
  from aggressive multi-city expansion — the unit economics of free dedicated
  courier delivery are hard.
