# SA retail-pharmacy competitive landscape (scripting & delivery)

The South African pharmacy market splits into four overlapping groups: the
**big-two corporate retail chains** (Clicks, Dis-Chem); **supermarket-embedded
dispensaries** (Medirite, Pick n Pay); **independents and franchise/buying
banners** (Mopani, Alpha Pharm, TLC); and **courier/distance pharmacies**
(Clicks Direct, Pharmacy Direct, Medipost, etc.) that dominate **scheme-funded
chronic** delivery. This page benchmarks each on **scripting and delivery** - the
capabilities most relevant to a future Dis-Chem scripting experience.

## Clicks (the primary competitor)
**Clicks is larger than Dis-Chem** on the metrics that matter for scripting reach:
**900+ stores, 730+ in-store pharmacies**, and a **~12.9m-member ClubCard** that
drives ~84% of sales [[src-clicks-group-results]]. It owns wholesaler **UPD**.
- **Scripting/delivery strengths:**
  - **Clicks Direct Medicines** - **free** nationwide chronic-medicine delivery to
    home or nearest Clicks pharmacy, supplied via UPD [[src-clicks-direct-medicines]].
    A mature, scheme-aligned chronic courier and a direct rival to Dis-Chem Direct.
  - **On-demand OTC delivery** launched **April 2026** [[src-clicks-direct-medicines]]
    - Clicks catching up to Dis-Chem's DeliverD.
  - Dense pharmacy network → strong **collect** convenience and DSP coverage.
- **Implication:** Clicks matches/exceeds Dis-Chem on **chronic courier** and
  **network density**; Dis-Chem's edge is **per-store range, wellness/clinics,
  loyalty value, and earlier on-demand delivery**.

## Dis-Chem (anchor - for comparison)
**316 retail pharmacy stores**, **540+ clinics**, **DeliverD** (~60-min),
**PackMyMeds** (chronic repeats), **Dis-Chem Direct** (courier pharmacy), and
**280 TLC franchise pharmacies** extending reach to independents
[[src-dc-fy2026-results]] [[src-dc-tlc-500-franchise]]. See
[[wiki/dischem/dischem-dispensary-services]].

## Supermarket-embedded dispensaries
- **Medirite (Shoprite/Checkers):** **140+ in-store dispensaries** inside Shoprite
  and Checkers; first opened 1999; newer **Medirite Plus** standalone health &
  wellness format [[src-retail-pharmacy-competitors]]. Strength: **footfall +
  grocery convenience + price**; couples meds with the weekly shop. Backed by
  Shoprite's logistics muscle. Likely the fastest-growing share-taker at the
  value end.
- **Pick n Pay pharmacy:** in-supermarket dispensaries and a scheme pharmacy
  network [[src-retail-pharmacy-competitors]]; smaller pharmacy presence than
  Medirite; PnP's own financial pressures constrain expansion (⚠️ pharmacy count
  not disclosed in sources).

## Independents, franchise & buying groups
- **Mopani Pharmacy:** prominent independent/regional chain (Limpopo-rooted),
  large-format destination stores - a Dis-Chem-style experience regionally
  [[src-retail-pharmacy-competitors]] (⚠️ national count unconfirmed).
- **Alpha Pharm:** a **buying/marketing banner** for independent community
  pharmacies - relevant as the organised face of independents
  [[src-retail-pharmacy-competitors]] (⚠️ membership count unconfirmed).
- **The Local Choice (TLC):** **Dis-Chem's own** franchise banner for
  independents - 280 franchisees, room for ~500 [[src-dc-tlc-500-franchise]].
  Lets Dis-Chem extend scripting reach (and wholesale supply) into small-town SA
  without owning the store.

## Courier / distance pharmacies (the chronic-delivery specialists)
These set the **volume benchmark** for "scripting at distance" and largely run on
**scheme funding** under DSP/network rules [[src-sa-scheme-chronic-dsp]]:
- **Pharmacy Direct** (AfroCentric; since 2004): **>1.2m prescriptions/month**,
  >800 staff; serves private, scheme **and public-sector** patients
  [[src-courier-pharmacies-sa]].
- **Medipost:** described as SA's **largest national courier pharmacy** -
  ~**730k prescriptions/month**, **>595k patients**, free courier, **WhatsApp**
  ordering, specialised oncology/renal/HIV management [[src-courier-pharmacies-sa]].
- **Clicks Direct Medicines** (above) - the retail-chain entrant.
- Others in the field: **MedXpress**, **Scriptpharm**, plus scheme-owned/aligned
  couriers (⚠️ not yet sourced in this vault - see Open questions).
- Market context: courier pharmacy is ~**10% of all SA chronic dispensing**
  [[src-clicks-direct-medicines]].

**What they do well:** zero-friction, **free**, reliable **recurring** delivery of
scheme-funded chronic meds, with proactive refill cycles - the very loop where
Dis-Chem's PackMyMeds is still opt-in/manual
([[wiki/dischem/dischem-rx-current-journey]]).

## Competitive read for scripting design
| Player | Scripting/delivery strength | Gap Dis-Chem can exploit |
|---|---|---|
| Clicks | Network density, free chronic courier | Range, on-demand speed, clinics |
| Medirite | Grocery footfall, price | Pharmacy depth, wellness, digital |
| PnP pharmacy | Footfall | Scale, investment |
| Mopani/independents | Local trust | Digital, supply chain |
| Courier pharmacies | Scheme-funded, proactive chronic loop | In-store + same-day + ecosystem |

**Strategic takeaway:** the *distance/chronic* battle is largely won on **scheme
funding + a reliable proactive refill loop** (courier pharmacies excel, Dis-Chem
lags on proactivity). The *acute/convenience* battle is won on **speed and
reliability** (Dis-Chem leads on DeliverD but is undermined by fulfilment
reliability issues). A future Dis-Chem scripting experience should aim to **own
both** by closing the digital↔physical reliability gap and making chronic refills
genuinely managed, using the **clinics + telehealth + insurance ecosystem**
that pure couriers and supermarkets lack.
