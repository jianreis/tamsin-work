# Dis-Chem — company overview

Dis-Chem Pharmacies Ltd is one of South Africa's two dominant listed retail
pharmacy groups (the other being [[src-clicks-group-results|Clicks]]). It pairs
large-format "pharmacy + health, beauty, wellness, baby and convenience" stores
with a wholesale/distribution arm and a fast-growing "healthcare ecosystem"
(clinics, telehealth, insurance, loyalty). For a service designer, the key facts
are: Dis-Chem is **pharmacist-founded and dispensary-centric**, runs at **national
scale**, is **smaller than Clicks** by store and loyalty footprint, and is
**actively investing** in adjacent health services - which is the strategic context
for reinventing the scripting experience.

## History & founders
Dis-Chem was founded in **1978** by pharmacists **Ivan and Lynette Saltzman**,
who opened their first store in **Mondeor, Johannesburg** with roughly R10,000 in
capital [[src-dc-history-wikipedia]]. From a single store it grew into a national
chain, introduced private-label products around 1997, and **listed on the JSE in
2016** with 100+ stores and about R15.5bn in revenue [[src-dc-history-wikipedia]].
The Saltzman family has remained a major shareholder; in early 2026 the founders'
sons sold a large tranche of shares (~R642m) [[src-dc-history-wikipedia]]. From
inception the model combined **linked dispensaries, family clinics, wound-care
clinics and self-medication centres** - the dispensary has always been central to
the brand [[src-dc-dispensary-services]].

## Scale (FY2026, year ended 28 Feb 2026)
- **Retail footprint:** **316 retail pharmacy stores** plus **42 Baby City
  (baby) stores** at year-end; 31 retail pharmacy stores opened/acquired in the
  year [[src-dc-fy2026-results]].
- **Clinics:** **540+ clinics** staffed by ~**535 registered nursing
  practitioners**, delivering **1.2m+ consultations** (FY2025 basis)
  [[src-dc-tlc-500-franchise]].
- **People:** approximately **18,500–20,000 employees** (sources vary - see Open
  questions) [[src-dc-tlc-500-franchise]].
- **TLC franchise:** **280 The Local Choice franchise pharmacies** (up from 240),
  with management citing room for ~**500** [[src-dc-tlc-500-franchise]].

## Revenue & mix
Group revenue grew **9.3% to R42.8bn** in FY2026 [[src-dc-fy2026-results]] (from
**R39.2bn** in FY2025, when retail revenue was R33.6bn [[src-dc-fy2025-results]]).
The business has **two engines**:
1. **Retail** - the pharmacy + front-shop stores (the majority of revenue).
2. **Wholesale & distribution (CJ Distribution)** - supplying independents and
   the **TLC franchise** banner; external wholesale revenue +11.3% in FY2026,
   with 16.4% growth from TLC [[src-dc-fy2026-results]] [[src-dc-tlc-500-franchise]].

FY2026 **headline earnings fell ~17%** (HEPS 113.7c) and the dividend was cut
~17%, explicitly because of **investment in the healthcare ecosystem** plus
non-recurring items; *underlying* profit before tax (ex-ecosystem) was up ~20%
[[src-dc-fy2026-results]]. The group is deliberately trading current
earnings for a longer-term ecosystem position.

## Market position vs Clicks
Dis-Chem is the **#2 listed pharmacy group by footprint and loyalty base**.
Clicks runs **900+ stores and 730+ in-store pharmacies** and a **~12.9m-member
ClubCard** contributing ~84% of sales [[src-clicks-group-results]] - materially
larger than Dis-Chem's 316 pharmacy stores and ~9m loyalty members
[[src-dc-better-rewards]]. Dis-Chem competes on **range/scale per store**,
**dispensary depth**, **wellness/clinics**, and increasingly on **loyalty value**
and **digital convenience**. Both groups own a **wholesaler** (Dis-Chem: CJ
Distribution; Clicks: UPD) and a **chronic courier** channel.

## Loyalty: Benefit → Better Rewards
On **21 October 2025** Dis-Chem replaced its 23-year-old **Benefit** card with
**Better Rewards**, moving from retrospective points to **instant at-till
discounts** (~10% off across 140+ brands / 10,000+ products) [[src-dc-better-rewards]].
The ~**9 million** existing members were auto-migrated. In the first 17 weeks the
programme returned **R410m** in savings and pulled in **+550,000** new shoppers
(boosted by a Capitec partnership) [[src-dc-better-rewards]]. Loyalty is being
positioned as an on-ramp into the healthcare ecosystem, not just discounts.

## Healthcare ecosystem: Dis-Chem Health, Kena, insurance
Dis-Chem is building beyond dispensing into a **vertically integrated health
ecosystem**: **Dis-Chem Health** sells primary health-insurance products
(medical insurance, gap & accident cover, e.g. "MyHealth Plus"); and a
partnership with **Kena Health** offers **app-based telehealth** (text/voice/video
consults) aimed at SA's uninsured [[src-dc-health-kena]]. Combined with clinics
and the dispensary, this lets Dis-Chem own more of the **prescribe → fund →
dispense → adhere** chain - directly relevant to a future scripting experience,
because the "prescriber" and "funder" may increasingly be inside Dis-Chem's own
walls.

## Why this matters for scripting design
- Dispensary-centric DNA + clinics + telehealth + insurance = Dis-Chem can
  realistically design an **end-to-end Rx journey it largely controls**.
- It is the **challenger** to Clicks on scale, so **experience differentiation**
  (speed, convenience, adherence) is strategically attractive.
- The group is already **spending to win the ecosystem** - appetite for
  investment in a better scripting experience is plausibly high.
