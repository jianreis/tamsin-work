# Consultancy & design-firm POV on pharmacy, scripting & medication experience

What the tier-1 strategy consultancies and the leading design/innovation firms have
actually published about pharmacy, prescriptions, medication adherence, digital
pharmacy, and pharmacy experience design — and, honestly, **where they have not**.

**Headline judgement.** No tier-1 firm has published a body of work specifically on
the **"scripting"** (prescription submission → validate → dispense → pay → collect →
deliver → adhere) **journey as a service-design problem**. What exists splits into
two camps that the SA design must *combine*:

1. **Strategy firms** (McKinsey, Bain, Deloitte, Oliver Wyman, EY) own the
   **"pharmacy of the future"** thesis — pharmacy becomes a **clinical health
   destination / front door**, automation absorbs dispensing mechanics, pharmacists
   move "to the top of their licence", reimbursement turns cost-plus, and consumers
   demand **retail-grade convenience**. This is the *why* and the *operating model*.
   It is **macro, not journey-level**, and almost entirely US/UK-framed.
2. **Design firms** (IDEO, frog, Mad\*Pow, Fjord/Accenture Song, plus the CVS
   ScriptPath exemplar) own the **experience layer** — trust-based pharmacist
   relationships, adherence-as-experience, behaviour-change design, and the
   information design of the script artefact itself. This is the *how* at the
   touchpoint, but rarely the **end-to-end regulated transaction**.

The gap between them — a regulated, multi-funder, low-bandwidth, emerging-market
**scripting service blueprint** — is precisely the white space this vault occupies.
Treat all of the below as **Tier B/C** (consultancy thought-leadership is
marketing-adjacent), and note that **WebFetch was 403-blocked**, so load-bearing
figures are flagged `⚠️ UNVERIFIED` and triangulated from search extracts.

## Strategy / management consultancies

### McKinsey & Company (+ McKinsey Health Institute)
**Has it done pharmacy work? Yes — the deepest "pharmacy of the future" library, but
not scripting-journey design.** McKinsey's Healthcare Practice has several named
pieces: *Meeting changing consumer needs: The US retail pharmacy of the future*
(consumer-survey-backed, segments four pharmacy types, finds **convenience +
personalisation** dominant and ~40% of consumers wanting broader clinical services
*via* the pharmacy) [[src-consult-mckinsey-retail-pharmacy-future]]; *Pharmacy's new
era — in the home* (delivery + **medication synchronisation** as an adherence and
relationship play, not just logistics) [[src-consult-mckinsey-pharmacy-home]]; and a
reinvention cluster — *The revival of pharmacy: the rise of cost-based
reimbursement*, *A new dose for pharmacies*, *Untapped opportunities for
health-system pharmacies* — on cost-plus economics and reallocating pharmacist time
from "counting pills" to clinical services via automation
[[src-consult-mckinsey-pharmacy-reinvention]].
**The McKinsey Health Institute (MHI)** is *not* a pharmacy source: it publishes on
holistic/employee health, workforce, and the women's health gap — relevant only as a
health-equity and behaviour framing [[src-consult-mhi-employee-health]].

### Bain & Company
**Has it done pharmacy work? Adjacent, and useful.** Bain's *Beyond the Pill: How to
Improve the Customer Experience in Pharma* explicitly imports **consumer-grade CX
discipline** (journeys, episodes, NPS loops) into medicine, arguing the competitive
frontier is the **experience wrapped around the drug**, and links experience to
**adherence/persistence** [[src-consult-bain-beyond-the-pill]]. Bain's **Front Line
of Healthcare** survey series is the standout data point for SA: its 2024 Asia-Pacific
edition finds **≥65% of consumers (≈74% of Gen Z) already use pharmacies as a primary
point of care**, and its patient-experience work finds ~65% expect **more
convenience** and ~70% **more responsiveness** than three years ago — with today's
experience "marred by long wait times, difficulty scheduling … confusion over
billing" (i.e. service-design failures) [[src-consult-bain-front-line-healthcare]].

### Boston Consulting Group (BCG)
**Has it done pharmacy-scripting work? No — be honest.** Despite being named as a
priority, **no dedicated BCG retail-pharmacy or scripting publication surfaced.**
BCG's health depth is in **biopharma** (drug modalities, AI in R&D, business-model
strain) and in **consumer personalisation/AI**. The closest adjacent, borrowable
work: *How Digital & AI Will Reshape Health Care in 2025* (AI as an "autonomous
partner" but framed **alongside** humans); a global survey of **13,353 consumers**
finding people **welcome AI-assisted health support *alongside* human interaction**,
not instead of it; and the **BCG Personalization Index** (personalisation leaders
grow ~10% faster) with the line "patients expect to be **understood, not processed**"
[[src-consult-bcg-digital-ai-healthcare]]. Borrow the *personalisation + AI-with-
human-fallback* stance; do not expect a pharmacy journey from BCG.

### Deloitte
**Has it done pharmacy work? Yes — a clear, citable "pharmacy of the future" POV.**
*The Future of Pharmacy* and *The pharmacist of the future* forecast retail pharmacies
as **consolidated health destinations**, with automation/AI freeing pharmacists to
become **clinical care providers** managing chronic disease and limited prescribing,
while community pharmacies that *don't* reorient toward clinical services lose share
to digital disruptors [[src-consult-deloitte-future-of-pharmacy]]. Deloitte's earlier
*Increasing consumer engagement through retail pharmacy* ("moving beyond the pill")
is the origin of the "value is the **ongoing relationship**, not the single fill"
thesis [[src-consult-deloitte-beyond-the-pill]].

### Oliver Wyman
**Has it done pharmacy work? Yes — the strongest *retail-health-site* POV.** A
sustained body: *The Future of Brick-and-Mortar Pharmacy* (2022), *10 Ways Pharmacy
Services Will Change Market Dynamics* (Dec 2025), and retail-clinic-as-network-hub /
"**new front door to healthcare**" work (projecting ≥$200bn of US spend shifting to
alternative care sites). Core thesis: organisations that treat **pharmacy services as
a strategic asset, not a downstream function**, win; physical estates become
integrated health touchpoints (screening, care coordination, behaviour change)
[[src-consult-oliver-wyman-pharmacy]]. **Best strategic fit for Dis-Chem/Clicks**,
which already own dense store + clinic estates.

### EY
**Has it done pharmacy work? Yes — UK community-pharmacy lens.** *How pharmacy
innovation could change patient lives* (UK, 2025) makes the **automation → clinical
pharmacist** argument and flags the key digital gap: only **~1 in 10 English patients
manage repeat prescriptions digitally** — the same untapped-adoption opportunity SA
faces — while warning about **workforce fragility** (81% of UK community pharmacists
considered leaving in 2022). It cites exemplars (~70% of Danish pharmacies use
dispensing robots; European pharmacies accessing patient records)
[[src-consult-ey-pharmacy-innovation]]. EY's **Consumer Health Survey** uses the
"**digital front door**" frame for reducing friction in finding/accessing care.

### PwC
**Has it done pharmacy-scripting work? No — macro only.** PwC's Health Research
Institute owns **health economics** (annual *Medical Cost Trend*), the **New Health
Economy / Future of Health** series (pharma → "**lifespan partners**"), and a Health
Consumer Survey. It corroborates the **cost-plus** shift, but there is **no PwC report
on the scripting journey** itself. Note: the widely-cited consumer "**Pharmacy
Next**" survey is **Wolters Kluwer, not PwC** — a common misattribution
[[src-consult-pwc-health-economy]].

### Accenture (strategy + Accenture Song/Fjord — see design firms)
**Has it done pharmacy-adherence work? Yes — with a rare quantified result.** Accenture
helped **CCS** deploy an **AI predictive-analytics model** that reportedly improved
**adherence among targeted high-risk patients by up to ~50%** (vendor-reported), and
its **Life Trends 2025** stresses that consumers want digital health **and** still
"deeply value face-to-face connection" — digital-*and*-human
[[src-consult-accenture-ccs-adherence]]. (Accenture's design capability is via Fjord —
below.)

### KPMG
**Has it done pharmacy-scripting work? No — honest gap.** No KPMG-branded named report
on the prescription journey, pharmacy CX, or medication adherence surfaced. KPMG's
adjacent strengths are health-system transformation, operating-model/cost work,
life-sciences supply chain, and (UK) community-pharmacy funding/clinical-services
policy. **Not a primary source for this vault** [[src-consult-kpmg-retail-health]].

## Design / innovation firms

### IDEO
**Has it done pharmacy work? Yes — the marquee physical-service case.** *Bringing Back
the Neighborhood Pharmacy* redesigned the **Walgreens** experience to rebuild
**trust-based pharmacist–customer relationships**: it brought the **pharmacist out
from behind the high rear counter onto the floor**, added **private consultation
rooms** and a friendly "**health guide**", prototyped two full-size stores, and helped
launch the **Well Experience** format (IDEO reports ~750 locations)
[[src-consult-ideo-walgreens-pharmacy]]. Directly relevant to the **in-store scripting
moment** and the dignity/privacy of the dispense interaction.

### frog (part of Capgemini Invent)
**Has it done pharmacy work? Adjacent — and the sharpest single frame.** frog's *Why
Pharma Needs to Invest in Experience Design (From Efficacy to Effectiveness)* argues
**experience is the delta between a drug's efficacy and its real-world
effectiveness**, because effectiveness depends on **adherence**, which is an
experience problem — "pharma drops the baton in the home stretch." frog reports a
designed coaching app reaching ~70% adherence, and offers a reusable healthcare **CX
Playbook** [[src-consult-frog-pharma-experience]].

### Fjord / Accenture Song
**Has it done pharmacy work? Adjacent, via Accenture's health-experience offer.**
Fjord (Accenture Song's design arm) runs Accenture's human-centred **Health
Experience** and **Patient Experience Lab** work; **Fjord Trends** explicitly call for
"**equitable and empathetic healthcare**" and treat the **caregiver** as a
first-class user alongside the patient — relevant to SA proxy/collection journeys
[[src-consult-accenture-fjord-health-experience]]. No standalone Fjord *scripting*
case found; capability + POV, not a named pharmacy artefact.

### Mad\*Pow
**Has it done pharmacy work? Adjacent — behaviour-change method.** Mad\*Pow's signature
**Behavior Change Design** fuses behavioural/decision science, motivational
psychology, population health and HCD to shift health behaviours including
**treatment adherence**, working with health systems, insurers, and pharma. Its
method (target behaviour → modifiable determinants → matched techniques, co-designed)
is a practitioner translation of the vault's **COM-B/EAST** frame
[[src-consult-madpow-behavior-change]] [[jtbd-and-behavioural-design]].

### CVS ScriptPath / Deborah Adler (design exemplar, not a consultancy)
**The clearest example of design applied to the script artefact itself.** ScriptPath
turns a pile of separate prescriptions into **one icon-driven medication schedule**
plus a redesigned bottle label — by **Deborah Adler** (designer of Target's ClearRx) —
explicitly to make timing/dosing legible and improve **adherence** for polypharmacy
patients and caregivers [[src-consult-cvs-scriptpath-adler]]. The lesson: **the label
and schedule are a design surface**, high-leverage for low-literacy/multilingual SA.

### Designit (Wipro), ustwo, Smart Design
**Has it done pharmacy-scripting work? No firm-specific case found — honest gap.** All
three do credible health/service design, but **no firm-attributed pharmacy or
scripting case study surfaced**. Designit has healthcare work (e.g. telemedicine);
ustwo does digital-health companion apps; **Smart Design**'s **inclusive-design**
heritage (OXO Good Grips) is directly relevant to elderly/low-vision/low-literacy
users. **Borrow the methods, not a named engagement** [[src-consult-designit-ustwo-smartdesign]]
[[inclusive-low-bandwidth-design]].

## Frameworks & findings worth borrowing (the shortlist)

1. **Efficacy → Effectiveness = Experience (frog).** Reframe adherence as a *design*
   problem, not a compliance problem: effectiveness is lost in the experience after
   dispensing [[src-consult-frog-pharma-experience]]. The single most useful framing
   for the **adhere/refill** stages.
2. **Pharmacy as front door / health destination (Oliver Wyman, Deloitte, McKinsey).**
   Design the scripting journey as the *entry* to a broader care relationship, and
   treat pharmacy services as a **strategic asset** — Dis-Chem already owns the estate
   [[src-consult-oliver-wyman-pharmacy]] [[src-consult-deloitte-future-of-pharmacy]]
   [[wiki/dischem/_index]].
3. **Top-of-licence pharmacist + automation (Deloitte, McKinsey, EY).** Use automation
   to absorb dispensing mechanics so pharmacists do **counselling and clinical work** —
   the human moments that build trust [[src-consult-deloitte-future-of-pharmacy]]
   [[src-consult-mckinsey-pharmacy-reinvention]] [[wiki/digital-transformation/pharmacy-automation-and-robotics]].
4. **Bring the pharmacist forward; design for trust & privacy (IDEO).** Out from behind
   the counter; private consultation space; a "health guide" role — relationship as
   the design goal [[src-consult-ideo-walgreens-pharmacy]]
   [[healthcare-service-design-patterns]].
5. **Medication synchronisation + home delivery as an adherence play (McKinsey).** Sync
   refills onto one schedule; the home channel is a relationship lever, not just
   logistics [[src-consult-mckinsey-pharmacy-home]]
   [[wiki/global-benchmarks/patterns-to-borrow-and-cautions]].
6. **The script artefact is a design surface (CVS ScriptPath / Adler).** One
   icon-driven schedule + a legible label beats a pile of bottles — critical for SA
   low-literacy/multilingual contexts [[src-consult-cvs-scriptpath-adler]]
   [[pharmacy-ux-patterns]] [[inclusive-low-bandwidth-design]].
7. **Behavior Change Design (Mad\*Pow) over reminders alone.** Target behaviour →
   modifiable determinants → matched techniques; co-designed; evidence-based — the
   professional version of COM-B/EAST [[src-consult-madpow-behavior-change]]
   [[jtbd-and-behavioural-design]].
8. **AI alongside humans, never instead (BCG, Accenture).** Consumers welcome AI
   support *with* a human fallback; "understood, not processed"; AI can lift adherence
   for targeted high-risk cohorts [[src-consult-bcg-digital-ai-healthcare]]
   [[src-consult-accenture-ccs-adherence]] [[wiki/digital-transformation/ai-in-pharmacy]].
9. **Patients-as-consumers expect retail-grade convenience (Bain).** Convenience,
   responsiveness, transparent billing — the bar is Sixty60/Takealot, not "the old
   pharmacy" [[src-consult-bain-front-line-healthcare]]
   [[src-consult-bain-beyond-the-pill]] [[wiki/consumer-context/_index]].
10. **Digital repeat-prescription adoption is the untapped lever (EY).** Only ~1 in 10
    UK patients manage repeats digitally — reducing **enrolment friction** is itself a
    growth and adherence lever [[src-consult-ey-pharmacy-innovation]].

## Relevance to the SA future-state scripting design

- **Combine the two camps the firms keep separate.** The strategy firms give the
  *destination/front-door* vision; the design firms give the *experience/adherence*
  craft. SA's scripting blueprint must fuse them **and** add the layer none of them
  carry: a **regulated, multi-funder, low-bandwidth, emerging-market transaction**
  (SEP pricing, scheme adjudication, scheduling law, POPIA, USSD/WhatsApp fallback)
  [[wiki/sa-regulatory/_index]] [[wiki/medical-schemes/_index]]
  [[wiki/consumer-context/_index]].
- **Most published work is US/UK and assumes single-payer or PBM plumbing.** Use it for
  *experience patterns and the clinical-services vision*, but reject the funding
  architecture — exactly the caution in
  [[wiki/global-benchmarks/patterns-to-borrow-and-cautions]]. The emerging-market and
  Australia models there transfer better than anything in the consultancy canon.
- **Borrow frog's reframing as the spine of the adhere/refill stages**: design for
  *effectiveness*, measured as adherence, not for transaction completion alone
  [[src-consult-frog-pharma-experience]].
- **Borrow IDEO's "pharmacist forward" + private consultation** for the in-store
  dispense moment, honouring dignity and the discreet-notification rules already in
  [[healthcare-service-design-patterns]] — but adapt for SA queue volumes and the
  proxy/caregiver collection norm (CCMDD, stokvel/household buyers)
  [[wiki/consumer-context/_index]].
- **Borrow ScriptPath's information design** for the dispense/adhere artefact: one
  icon-driven, multilingual, low-literacy-safe schedule — deliverable on paper, SMS,
  WhatsApp, or app [[src-consult-cvs-scriptpath-adler]] [[pharmacy-ux-patterns]]
  [[inclusive-low-bandwidth-design]].
- **Treat AI as triage + adherence support with a guaranteed human (pharmacist)
  fallback** — consistent with both BCG/Accenture's "alongside humans" stance and the
  China-style guardrails already adopted in
  [[wiki/global-benchmarks/patterns-to-borrow-and-cautions]]
  [[wiki/digital-transformation/ai-in-pharmacy]].
- **Set the experience bar at retail convenience (Bain) while keeping the clinical
  trust layer (Deloitte/IDEO)** — SA consumers benchmark pharmacy against Sixty60 and
  Capitec, not against other pharmacies [[src-consult-bain-front-line-healthcare]]
  [[wiki/consumer-context/_index]].

## Open questions
- **No firm has published an end-to-end *scripting service blueprint*** (submit →
  validate → dispense → pay → collect → deliver → adhere). Is this genuinely white
  space, or is the best prior art in non-consultancy sources (Australia eScript, NHS
  EPS, Apollo) already captured in [[wiki/global-benchmarks/patterns-to-borrow-and-cautions]]?
- **frog/Mad\*Pow adherence outcomes are vendor-reported** (~70% adherence; Accenture's
  ~50% lift). What independent evidence corroborates design-led adherence gains vs the
  modest/conditional effects in [[wiki/digital-transformation/adherence-tech-and-digital-therapeutics]]?
- **WebFetch 403** means every figure here rests on search extracts — load-bearing
  numbers need primary verification before they enter a blueprint. Logged to
  [[meta/open-questions]] convention.
- **Is there any SA/African consultancy or design POV on scripting?** All sources are
  global-North. A local-evidence gap remains [[wiki/consumer-context/_index]].
- **Could Accenture Song/Fjord, IDEO, or frog have done *confidential* pharmacy
  scripting engagements** not publicly published? Likely, but uncitable — treat
  published POV as the floor, not the ceiling.
