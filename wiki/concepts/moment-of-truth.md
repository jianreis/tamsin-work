# Moment of truth

A **moment of truth (MoT)** is a make-or-break interaction where the customer
forms (or breaks) their judgement of the whole service: the points that carry
disproportionate emotional weight and where a single failure outweighs a hundred
smooth steps. In journey-mapping terms they are the spikes on the emotion lane,
and in service-design terms they are where backstage reliability becomes visible.

The Rx journey has four MoTs that deserve disproportionate design effort:

1. **First submit**: handing over a script (paper, photo, e-token). The
   customer is trusting the channel with a health need and a deadline. If it
   feels lost or unacknowledged, trust collapses immediately.
2. **Funding result**: the moment a scheme claim adjudicates or a cash price is
   revealed. This is where "is it covered? what does it cost me?" is answered,
   and where an unexplained rejection or a surprise co-pay does the most damage.
3. **Fulfilment hand-over**: collection at counter/locker or the courier
   arriving. The physical proof the system worked, and the moment counselling
   must happen.
4. **First dose**: taking the medicine correctly. The true outcome moment;
   everything upstream exists to make this safe and correct.

These map directly onto the pain spikes in the as-is journey (the silent wait,
the cost shock, the failed handover). Designing the MoTs well, with
acknowledgement, honest status, no surprises, and a visible human, is the highest-leverage work.

## Design stance
Identify, name and over-invest in the four MoTs. Each must deliver: instant
acknowledgement, honest real-time status, no unexplained outcome, and an easy
route to a human. Score every blueprint on how it handles the *worst* version of
each MoT, not the happy path. See also [[wiki/concepts/graceful-failure|graceful failure]].
