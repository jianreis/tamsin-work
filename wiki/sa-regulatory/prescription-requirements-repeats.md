# Prescription validity, repeats, substitution & emergency supply

What makes a prescription legally valid in SA is set by **Regulation 33** of the
General Regulations (2017) under the Medicines Act; schedule-specific repeat and
supply rules come from **§22A** and the Schedules
[[src-reg-general-regulations-2017]] [[src-reg-medicines-act-101-1965]].

## A valid prescription (Reg 33)
Required content [[src-reg-general-regulations-2017]]:
- **Prescriber:** name, qualification, registration number, address (+ signature).
- **Patient:** name, identification, address.
- **Date** of issue.
- **Medicine:** name, dose, strength, dosage form, quantity, directions for use.
- **Number of repeats** (where applicable).

Two valid **formats**: (a) **paper** signed with a wet-ink signature; (b) a true
**electronic prescription** generated by an *electronic agent* and signed with an
**Advanced Electronic Signature (AES)** under ECTA s13, legally equal to paper
[[src-reg-ecta-eprescribing]]. A scanned/emailed/faxed copy of a wet-ink script is
an electronic *transmission*, not an e-prescription; the pharmacist must retain a
permanent copy and **verify authenticity** [[src-reg-pharmaciae-eprescription-transmission]].
See [[e-prescription-telehealth-popia]].

## Validity period & repeats by schedule
- **S2–S5:** a repeat prescription may **not be repeated for longer than six
  months** [[src-reg-general-regulations-2017]].
- **S4** specifically: repeats allowed **only if the prescriber states the number of
  times** it may be dispensed [[src-reg-general-regulations-2017]].
- **S6:** **no repeats** (a new prescription each time) and **max 30-day supply**
  [[src-reg-medicines-act-101-1965]].
- **S7/S8:** permit / DG special permission (out of scope for routine scripting).

(General industry practice treats an *original* prescription as valid for dispensing
within ~6 months of the date written; the statutory anchor is the six-month repeat
ceiling above. The precise "first-dispensing" validity wording is logged as a check.)

## Verbal / telephonic & transmitted prescriptions
A **verbal prescription** must be followed by a **signed prescription within 7
days**; any fax/verbal/transmitted script requires a **permanent record copy**
[[src-reg-general-regulations-2017]] [[src-reg-pharmaciae-eprescription-transmission]].
**S5/S6 by fax/email** is contentious (authenticity/diversion risk) and attracts the
strictest scrutiny [[src-reg-pharmaciae-eprescription-transmission]].

## Generic substitution (§22F)
A pharmacist **must inform** the patient about cheaper **interchangeable
multi-source (generic)** medicines and **must substitute**, **unless**
[[src-reg-medicines-act-101-1965]]:
1. the **patient expressly refuses**;
2. the **prescriber has written that substitution may not be made**; or
3. the medicine is one for which substitution is **not permitted** (e.g.
   narrow-therapeutic-index items on the prohibited list).

## Emergency supply (pharmacist)
- **S2/S3/S4:** pharmacist may sell on a **non-recurring** basis in an emergency.
- **S5/S6:** pharmacist may supply up to a **48-hour** quantity in an emergency
  [[src-reg-medicines-act-101-1965]].

## Who counsels
Patient counselling on safe/effective use is a **GPP requirement** performed by a
pharmacist [[src-reg-gpp-rules-sapc]]. See [[pharmacy-act-sapc-gpp]].

## Design implications / constraints
- **Validation is the gate before dispense.** A future scripting flow must
  programmatically check Reg-33 completeness (prescriber reg-number, patient ID,
  signature/AES, repeats) before a pharmacist authorises dispensing. Incomplete
  scripts must be returned to the prescriber, not "fixed" by the platform.
- **Repeat engine must be schedule-aware.** Allow repeats only within the 6-month
  ceiling for S2–S5, only up to the prescriber-stated count for S4, and **hard-block
  repeats for S6** with a 30-day cap. A "set up auto-refill" feature must respect
  these limits.
- **AES is the legal pivot for paperless scripts.** To remove paper entirely, the
  prescriber's system must apply an **ECTA AES**. A photo of a script is not enough.
  Mainstream provider e-prescribing with AES is still maturing in SA, so partial
  reform/adoption is needed, flag 🔮 requires-reform for a 100% AES future state.
- **Generic substitution is a built-in default with overrides.** The UI should
  default to the cheapest interchangeable generic, capture prescriber "no
  substitution" and patient refusal as first-class flags, and respect the
  non-substitutable list.
- **Emergency-supply path.** A digital experience can offer a compliant
  pharmacist-led emergency supply (48h for S5/S6; non-recurring for S2–S4) when a
  script lapses, a real adherence safety-net.
- **Counselling cannot be designed away** (see GPP).
