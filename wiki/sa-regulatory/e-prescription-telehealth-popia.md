# E-prescriptions, telehealth & POPIA

This page covers the three legal pillars of a *digital* scripting journey: whether
an **electronic prescription** is legally valid, whether a script can originate from
a **telehealth** consultation, and how **POPIA** governs the health data involved.

## E-prescriptions & electronic signatures (ECTA)
A genuine **electronic prescription is legally valid** in SA when it is generated by
an **electronic agent** (as defined in the Electronic Communications and
Transactions Act 25 of 2002, ECTA) and **signed with an Advanced Electronic
Signature (AES)** under **ECTA s13** — giving it the **same legal force as a
wet-ink paper script** [[src-reg-ecta-eprescribing]] [[src-reg-general-regulations-2017]].
An **AES** is one produced by a process accredited by the SA Accreditation Authority
and carries a **presumption of validity** [[src-reg-ecta-eprescribing]].

**Critical distinction:** a scanned / photographed / emailed / faxed copy of a
wet-ink script is an electronic **transmission**, **not** an e-prescription. For
those (and verbal scripts), the pharmacist must keep a **permanent record copy** and
**verify authenticity**; verbal scripts need a **signed prescription within 7 days**
[[src-reg-pharmaciae-eprescription-transmission]] [[src-reg-general-regulations-2017]].
S5/S6 by fax/email is the most contested case.

## Telehealth (HPCSA Booklet 10, rev. Dec 2021)
- **Pre-COVID:** telehealth allowed **only** with an **established
  practitioner-patient relationship**.
- **April 2020 (COVID) → carried into Dec 2021:** relaxed — telehealth "should
  **preferably**" occur within an existing relationship, but **where none exists a
  practitioner may still consult** if it is in the patient's **best clinical
  interest** [[src-reg-hpcsa-telehealth-2021]].
- **Telehealth prescribing** is permitted, but **informed consent and clinical
  standards must match face-to-face** prescribing; the assessment must justify the
  script [[src-reg-hpcsa-telehealth-2021]].
- Guidelines remain in force (2024+) and are revised by HPCSA periodically.

This means a **script can lawfully originate from a virtual consult** — the
foundation for an end-to-end digital journey (consult → e-script → dispense →
deliver).

## POPIA (health data)
Prescription/health data is **"special personal information"** under POPIA (s26);
processing is **prohibited unless** a s27 ground applies — chiefly **processing by/
for health professionals to provide healthcare**, or **informed, specific,
voluntary consent** [[src-reg-popia-health-data]]. The **8 processing conditions**
apply (purpose limitation, security safeguards s19, breach notification s22, etc.).
Full commencement was **1 July 2021**; **new health-data regulations are in progress
(2026)** [[src-reg-popia-health-data]]. [In flux.]

## Design implications / constraints
- **An e-script must still be linked to a registered prescriber and validated by a
  pharmacist before dispensing.** Digitisation changes the medium, not the
  accountability chain.
- **To go truly paperless, build for AES.** Integrate with prescriber systems that
  apply an ECTA AES; otherwise the artefact is a *transmission* requiring extra
  verification and record-keeping. 🔮 requires-reform / adoption: ubiquitous
  provider-side AES e-prescribing is not yet standard in SA.
- **Telehealth-originated scripts are viable today** under the relaxed HPCSA rules,
  but the platform must (a) ensure a registered practitioner makes the clinical
  decision, (b) capture **informed consent** to the same standard as in-person, and
  (c) not let an algorithm substitute for the prescriber's judgement
  (🔮 requires-reform otherwise).
- **POPIA is a design constraint, not a checkbox.** Lawful basis (healthcare
  provision and/or explicit consent), purpose limitation, encryption/security
  safeguards, breach response, minimal data sharing with couriers/schemes, and
  data-subject access must be engineered in. WhatsApp/app channels must meet s19
  security and consider cross-border processing rules.
- **Authenticity verification is mandatory** for any non-AES script — design a
  pharmacist verification step and an immutable record store.
- **Anticipate tightening:** the pending 2026 health-data regulations may add
  obligations — keep consent and data flows configurable.
