# The NHI horizon

**National Health Insurance (NHI)** is the government's plan to fund healthcare
through a single **NHI Fund** as dominant purchaser, aiming at universal coverage
across the [[cms-medical-schemes-overview|two-economy split]]. It is the biggest
*potential* disruptor to how prescriptions get paid for — but as of **mid-2026 it
is paused and contested**. We document **scenarios**, and **do not bet on one**.

## Status (as of 2026-06-13)

- **NHI Act (2023) signed into law 15 May 2024** [[src-sch-nhi-act-status]].
- Planned **phased implementation 2024–2028** ("fully functional by 2028" —
  aspirational) [[src-sch-nhi-act-status]].
- **Implementation paused**: the **High Court (Pretoria)** ordered a halt;
  government agreed not to advance key parts pending constitutional resolution
  [[src-sch-nhi-act-status]].
- **Constitutional Court heard challenges 5–7 May 2026** (BHF, Western Cape
  Premier; public-participation grounds); **judgment pending**
  [[src-sch-nhi-act-status]].

## The contested core — Section 33

Section 33: once the Minister declares NHI **"fully implemented"** (a term the
Act **leaves undefined**), **medical schemes may only offer complementary cover**
— i.e. only services the NHI Fund does not reimburse [[src-sch-nhi-act-status]].
This is the existential question for schemes (and for courier pharmacy, whose
volume rides on scheme-funded chronic benefits). Challengers — **BHF, SAMA,
HASA, Discovery, Solidarity**, Western Cape — cite constitutional defects
(public participation, provincial powers, signing process)
[[src-sch-nhi-act-status]].

## Scenarios for retail & courier pharmacy

These are **planning scenarios**, deliberately not ranked as predictions:

1. **Status quo persists (most likely near/medium term).** Litigation, fiscal
   reality and the undefined "fully implemented" trigger mean schemes, PMBs, DSPs
   and courier pharmacy continue largely unchanged for years. **Design for this
   today.**
2. **Partial / programmatic NHI.** NHI procures specific services or population
   segments (mirroring how **CCMDD** already distributes public chronic meds at
   scale [[src-sch-ccmdd-public]]). Retail pharmacy could become an **accredited
   NHI dispensing/pick-up provider** — a large new uninsured-market channel.
   🔮 requires-reform (accreditation, tariffs, e-Rx, formulary).
3. **Full Section 33 implementation (long-horizon, contested).** Schemes shrink
   to complementary cover; the **NHI Fund becomes the dominant payer** and
   formulary-setter. Real-time claiming would re-point at the Fund; courier/
   meds-by-mail logistics likely persist but funded differently.

## Why CCMDD is the best current proxy

The public sector already runs a **national chronic-meds dispensing-and-
distribution machine** (**>4.9m enrolled**) with retail pick-up points
[[src-sch-ccmdd-public]]. Whatever NHI becomes, it will likely **build on CCMDD-
style centralised dispensing + distributed collection**. A retail pharmacy that
is already a strong **CCMDD / pick-up partner** is well-positioned for any NHI
chronic-medicine role. Its documented pain points (tracking, notifications,
rural access) are the gaps a better experience would close.

## Design implications

- **Don't design *for* NHI; design *robust to* it.** Anchor the experience in
  today's scheme + cash reality (Scenario 1), but keep the architecture **payer-
  agnostic** — abstract "who funds this script" so a new payer (NHI Fund) can slot
  in without a redesign.
- **Lean into the uninsured/CCMDD bridge now.** Serving the uninsured via
  pick-up-point and primary-care-insurance models builds exactly the muscle any
  NHI scenario rewards — and grows the addressable market today.
- **Treat formulary/funding rules as configurable, not hard-coded.** PMB,
  scheme formulary, MMAP, and a future NHI formulary are the same *shape* of
  rule; build one rules engine, swap the data.
- **Flag NHI-dependent concepts `🔮 requires-reform`** in blueprints; never make
  a roadmap contingent on a contested, undated trigger.

## Open questions
- ConCourt judgment outcome and timing (heard May 2026, pending). ⚠️ → [[open-questions]].
- Definition/trigger of "fully implemented" (Section 33) — undefined in the Act.
- Whether retail pharmacy will be accreditable as an NHI dispensing/pick-up
  provider, and on what tariff.
