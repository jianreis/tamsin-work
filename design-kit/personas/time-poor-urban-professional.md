# Aisha Patel — time-poor urban professional

> **One-liner:** A 33-year-old insured city professional whose expectations are set
> by Sixty60, Takealot and Uber — she wants speed, door delivery, an upfront price
> and live tracking, and is unforgiving of any opaque status, surprise co-pay or
> failed delivery.

## Demographics & context
- **Age 33**, lives in Sandton / inner-city Cape Town; consultant working long
  hours. Single, no dependents, findable apartment address with a concierge.
- **App-fluent, English-first, fibre + uncapped mobile** — at the unconstrained
  end of the connectivity spectrum [[wiki/consumer-context/digital-access-and-whatsapp]].
- Time, not money, is her scarce resource; she optimises everything for minimal
  friction.

## Health situation
- **Mostly acute + light chronic:** occasional one-off scripts (antibiotics, an
  acute issue) plus a routine repeat (e.g. contraception or a reflux medicine).
- Health admin is **low-salience until it's urgent** — she wants it to disappear
  into the background, handled in two taps between meetings.
- Some of her needs are **privacy-sensitive** (contraception); discreet handling
  matters even for an affluent user [[wiki/consumer-context/language-literacy-and-trust]].

## Devices, channels & connectivity
- **Power user of the best apps** — she explicitly benchmarks any pharmacy
  experience against **Checkers Sixty60 (60-min delivery, flat R35 fee, live
  tracking), Takealot, and Uber/Bolt (named provider, upfront price, rating
  loop)** [[wiki/consumer-context/adjacent-digital-expectations]].
- Equally happy on **WhatsApp** for quick submit/track/reorder
  [[wiki/consumer-context/digital-access-and-whatsapp]].
- Expects **live status, an honest ETA, and a transparent fee** as table stakes —
  she does not judge the pharmacy against other pharmacies
  [[wiki/consumer-context/adjacent-digital-expectations]].

## Money & funding
- On a **medical scheme** (comprehensive option); willing to **pay for
  convenience** (delivery fees, premium speed) — but allergic to **opaque or
  surprise** costs [[wiki/medical-schemes/cms-medical-schemes-overview]].
- Wants the **scheme applied automatically** and any co-pay shown **before she
  presses pay**; a co-pay surprise reads as a broken, low-quality experience
  [[wiki/medical-schemes/pmb-cdl-chronic]].
- Pays instantly with a **stored card / instant EFT** and expects checkout to be
  frictionless [[wiki/consumer-context/adjacent-digital-expectations]].

## Goals / Jobs-To-Be-Done
See [[jtbd-library]]. Her dominant jobs:
- **Get a new (acute) script filled fast** with minimum extra effort — ideally
  delivered.
- **Know it's handled** — valid, in stock, priced, with an ETA; no guessing.
- **Pay in a way that works for me** — scheme auto-applied, cost shown up front,
  one-tap checkout.
- **Get it where I am** — fast door delivery to a findable address.
- Emotional: **feel in control**, not made to chase status or queue.

## Key pains in the current journey
- **Opaque status, no honest ETA** — the current app's failures (reversed orders
  minutes before delivery, confusing multi-step address entry) are exactly what
  Sixty60 has already solved ([[as-is-prescription-journey]] Stage 7)
  [[wiki/consumer-context/adjacent-digital-expectations]],
  [[wiki/consumer-context/last-mile-addressing-logistics]].
- **App reliability** — login/payment failures and crashes break her preferred
  channel ([[as-is-prescription-journey]] Stages 2, 5).
- **Co-payment surprise at the till** reads as an avoidable failure
  ([[as-is-prescription-journey]] Stage 4) [[wiki/medical-schemes/pmb-cdl-chronic]].
- **Having to make a store trip at all** for something a courier should bring
  ([[as-is-prescription-journey]] Stage 7).

## Behaviours & quote
She submits a photo, expects a price and ETA back immediately, tracks the courier
on a map, and rates the experience. One failed delivery or opaque wait and she
churns to a competitor or a courier pharmacy without sentiment.

> *"If Sixty60 can get groceries to my door in an hour with a live map and a flat
> fee, why is getting my prescription such a mystery? Tell me the price, track it,
> and bring it."*

## How to design for them
- **Meet the Sixty60/Uber bar explicitly:** live status, **honest ETA**, a
  **transparent flat fee**, a **named pharmacist/provider**, and a rating loop
  [[wiki/consumer-context/adjacent-digital-expectations]].
- **Two-tap submit-and-reorder** over app *or* WhatsApp, with instant,
  multi-rail, **frictionless checkout** [[wiki/consumer-context/digital-access-and-whatsapp]].
- **Hide the necessary complexity:** run scheme validation and the pharmacist
  clinical check in the background, but **surface cost (incl. any co-pay) before
  commit** so it never lands as a surprise [[wiki/medical-schemes/pmb-cdl-chronic]].
- **Fast, reliable door delivery** to a findable address, with proactive status
  and a **collection fallback** when delivery can't be guaranteed
  [[wiki/consumer-context/last-mile-addressing-logistics]].
- **Discreet by default** even for the affluent user — neutral packaging/wording
  for privacy-sensitive items [[wiki/consumer-context/language-literacy-and-trust]].
