# Mandla Zwane — public-sector patient bridging to retail

> **One-liner:** A 52-year-old uninsured chronic patient who collects ART and
> blood-pressure medicine through the public clinic via CCMDD/Pelebox, and is
> weighing whether a retail pharmacy could offer more reliable stock and more
> convenient collection than the clinic queue.

## Demographics & context
- **Age 52**, lives in a township on the edge of a metro (e.g. Mamelodi,
  Gauteng). Employed in semi-formal work with limited time off.
- **Uninsured** - part of the ~84% majority that relies on the public sector
  [[wiki/consumer-context/two-economy-affordability]],
  [[wiki/medical-schemes/cash-uninsured-pathway]].
- Home language **isiZulu/Sepedi**; functional English; reads health info in a
  second language [[wiki/consumer-context/language-literacy-and-trust]].

## Health situation
- **Stable, multi-medicine chronic** patient: on **ART** plus **hypertension**
  treatment - a common multimorbid public-sector profile
  [[wiki/consumer-context/health-seeking-behaviour]].
- Enrolled in **CCMDD**: clinically stable, collects **pre-packed parcels every
  2–3 months** at a community pick-up point, increasingly via **Pelebox** lockers
  (SMS + PIN, no app, sub-minute) [[wiki/consumer-context/health-seeking-behaviour]],
  [[wiki/consumer-context/last-mile-addressing-logistics]].
- **Stigma-sensitive:** ART disclosure fear is real; decentralised, discreet
  collection is valued precisely because it reduces visibility
  [[wiki/consumer-context/language-literacy-and-trust]].

## Devices, channels & connectivity
- **Modest Android on prepaid**, data-conscious; **WhatsApp + SMS** are his
  reliable channels, and he already trusts the **SMS + PIN** collection pattern
  [[wiki/consumer-context/digital-access-and-whatsapp]].
- Comfortable with **no-app, locker-based collection** - the CCMDD/Pelebox model
  is his current normal, and it sets his expectations for any alternative
  [[wiki/consumer-context/last-mile-addressing-logistics]].

## Money & funding
- **Cash / free public route.** CCMDD chronic meds are **free** at the clinic;
  any retail switch must compete on **affordability** as well as convenience -
  he cannot absorb a large out-of-pocket jump [[wiki/medical-schemes/cash-uninsured-pathway]].
- What pushes him toward retail is **non-price pain**: clinic **stock-outs**, long
  queues, travel cost, and inflexible collection windows - the trip and queue are
  themselves a cost [[wiki/consumer-context/health-seeking-behaviour]],
  [[wiki/consumer-context/two-economy-affordability]].

## Goals / Jobs-To-Be-Done
See [[jtbd-library]]. His dominant jobs:
- **Keep chronic meds flowing without thinking about it** - reliable supply, no
  stock-out, predictable collection.
- **Get it where I am** - discreet, near-home, convenient-hours collection that
  beats the clinic queue.
- **Pay in a way that works for me** - stay affordable; a retail option must not
  price him out of medicine he currently gets free.
- Emotional: **dignity and privacy** - collect ART without being seen or outed.

## Key pains in the current journey
- **Public-sector stock-outs and long queues** drive the very interest in retail
  [[wiki/consumer-context/health-seeking-behaviour]].
- **Affordability cliff:** moving off the free CCMDD route risks an out-of-pocket
  cost he can't sustain [[wiki/medical-schemes/cash-uninsured-pathway]].
- **No bridge between public and retail rails today** - retail pharmacies are
  already CCMDD pick-up points, but there is no smooth path to *enrol/serve* him
  end-to-end (🔮 likely needs partnership/reform)
  [[wiki/consumer-context/health-seeking-behaviour]].
- **Stigma exposure risk** if a retail flow names his condition or makes
  collection public ([[as-is-prescription-journey]] Stages 7–8)
  [[wiki/consumer-context/language-literacy-and-trust]].

## Behaviours & quote
He tolerates the clinic because it's free, but resents wasted days when stock is
out. He is open to retail **if** it is reliably stocked, **as discreet and cheap**
as Pelebox, and near home - and will not pay a premium that strains his budget.

> *"At the clinic I queue half a day and sometimes there's no stock. If the
> pharmacy can have it ready in a locker near me, private, without costing me a
> fortune — I'd switch. But it can't cost more than free."*

## How to design for them
- **Build the public→retail bridge:** lean on retail pharmacies already being
  **CCMDD collection points**; offer a path to serve stable chronic/CCMDD patients
  on the **same near-home collection rails** (flag scope/funding as
  🔮 requires-reform where needed) [[wiki/consumer-context/health-seeking-behaviour]].
- **Match the Pelebox bar:** **SMS/WhatsApp + one-time PIN**, no-app, sub-minute,
  **private** locker/pick-up collection near home
  [[wiki/consumer-context/last-mile-addressing-logistics]].
- **Compete on reliability, not just speed:** guarantee stock and a predictable
  collection window - the clinic's biggest failure is the stock-out
  [[wiki/consumer-context/health-seeking-behaviour]].
- **Protect affordability:** transparent cash price, generics, and surface any
  free/CCMDD eligibility rather than pricing him out
  [[wiki/medical-schemes/cash-uninsured-pathway]].
- **Discreet by default:** neutral parcels and notification wording, personal PIN
  - never out an ART patient [[wiki/consumer-context/language-literacy-and-trust]].
