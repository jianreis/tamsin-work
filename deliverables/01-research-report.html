The Store of the Future & the Prescription Journey

What the strategy houses, the design firms and the global exemplars say about pharmacy's next decade — and what it means for the South African scripting experience.

Deliverable 1 of 4 · Research report · generated 2026-06-13 from the Bigly ground-truth vault · for Tamsin (Bigly Labs / Dis-Chem) & Jian

1 · TL;DR — the things to internalise

Eight take-aways. Pillar ids in S?/U? badges are defined in §3 and recur throughout.

  1. Nobody has published the thing we are building. The strategy firms own the "pharmacy of the future" thesis; the design firms own the experience/adherence craft. No tier-1 firm has published an end-to-end scripting service blueprint (submit → validate → dispense → pay → collect → deliver → adhere) for a regulated, multi-funder, low-bandwidth market. That white space is the opportunity. wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research
  2. The store becomes a health front door, not a shop with a counter. Oliver Wyman, Deloitte and McKinsey converge: pharmacy is a clinical destination and the physical estate is a strategic asset. S2 wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research
  3. You can't compete on price — it's regulated — so compete on the experience around the drug. SEP + a capped dispensing fee fix the medicine price nationwide; Bain's "beyond the pill" logic and SA's own pricing law point to the same place. S3 wiki/sa-regulatory/sep-dispensing-fee
  4. Two South Africas, one spine. ~16% insured, ~84% cash/uninsured — genuinely different journeys that must share one service spine, with the cash majority never a degraded afterthought. This is the single most load-bearing SA reality. S1 wiki/consumer-context/two-economy-affordability
  5. Borrow mechanisms, reject funding architectures. Australia (token + active-script-list), India and China (teleconsult→Rx→deliver loops, AI-with-human-fallback) transfer far better than US/UK models built on a single payer or a PBM middle layer. wiki/global-benchmarks/patterns-to-borrow-and-cautions
  6. Design for the constraint stack, not around it. Expensive data, prepaid metering, load-shedding, feature phones, 11 languages, missing street addresses. WhatsApp/USSD-first, low-data, async, resumable — never app-only. U1 wiki/ux-service-design/inclusive-low-bandwidth-design
  7. Adherence is a design problem, not a compliance problem. "Effectiveness" is the gap between a drug's efficacy and real-world use, and it is lost in the experience after dispensing — frog's reframing, plus CVS ScriptPath's redesigned schedule/label. U7 wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research
  8. The chronic refill loop is the retention engine — and Dis-Chem is losing it. Courier pharmacies (Pharmacy Direct >1.2m scripts/month; Medipost ~730k) win the proactive, scheme-funded chronic loop; Dis-Chem's PackMyMeds is still opt-in/manual. Owning a managed refill flywheel is the prize. S4 wiki/dischem/competitive-landscape-sa-pharmacy

2 · The shift — "store of the future" & scripting thought leadership

Across the consultancy and design canon, six ideas recur. None of the firms join them into a regulated scripting journey; together they describe the destination this work must reach.

2.1 Pharmacy as the front door / health destination

The strongest retail-health-site thesis is Oliver Wyman's: organisations that treat pharmacy services as a strategic asset, not a downstream function, win, and physical estates become integrated health touchpoints — a "new front door to healthcare." Deloitte forecasts retail pharmacies as consolidated health destinations where automation frees pharmacists into clinical care, warning that community pharmacies that don't reorient lose share to digital disruptors. McKinsey's consumer-survey work finds convenience and personalisation dominant, with ~40% of consumers wanting broader clinical services via the pharmacy. The UX literature gives this a name: the digital front door — "a front door, not the whole building" — that routes (care-navigates) rather than menus. S2 wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research wiki/ux-service-design/healthcare-service-design-patterns

2.2 The store as a fulfilment node — omnichannel

Omnichannel lets a patient start, pay for and receive a script across any mix of channels — in-store, app/web, click-and-collect, locker, courier, on-demand — against one continuous profile. The discipline that makes it "omni" rather than "multi" is a unified patient profile (one identity, med history, scheme/eligibility, consent) so channel choice never resets context. Globally, Apollo (India) fulfils online orders from Asia's largest pharmacy network for metro delivery — the store estate is the dark-store network. The pragmatic target the vault lands on: match channel to script type — acute scripts to in-store/on-demand; chronic/repeat to central-fill → locker/courier with remote verification — so the dispensary queue shrinks while reach grows. Dis-Chem and Clicks already own dense store + clinic footprints, so the building blocks (app ordering, click-and-collect, courier) are partly live; the missing layer is the single profile that stops the customer re-entering data every time they switch channel. U6 wiki/digital-transformation/telepharmacy-and-omnichannel wiki/global-benchmarks/patterns-to-borrow-and-cautions

2.3 The top-of-licence pharmacist + automation of toil

Deloitte, McKinsey and EY share one operating-model move: use automation, central-fill and robotics to absorb dispensing mechanics so pharmacists move "to the top of their licence" — counselling and clinical work, the human moments that build trust. EY cites exemplars (~70% of Danish pharmacies use dispensing robots) and the workforce-fragility risk (81% of UK community pharmacists considered leaving in 2022). Telepharmacy multiplies pharmacist reach: remote final verification lets a branch network run more collection points than it has resident pharmacists, with rural-US evidence showing no difference in medication-use quality versus traditional pharmacies. S5 wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research wiki/digital-transformation/telepharmacy-and-omnichannel

2.4 Trust, dignity and the pharmacist relationship (the design firms)

IDEO's Walgreens work is the marquee physical-service case: it brought the pharmacist out from behind the high rear counter onto the floor, added private consultation rooms and a "health guide", and helped launch the Well Experience format. The lesson for the in-store scripting moment is relationship and dignity by design. The health-UX canon (Picker's eight principles, NHS/GOV.UK) supplies the bar: information & education, emotional support, continuity, access, respect — plus POPIA-grade consent, discreet notifications, plain-language error recovery, and a guaranteed human fallback. U5 U4 wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research wiki/ux-service-design/healthcare-service-design-patterns

2.5 Adherence-as-design and the script artefact

frog offers the sharpest single frame: experience is the delta between a drug's efficacy and its real-world effectiveness, because effectiveness depends on adherence, which is an experience problem — "pharma drops the baton in the home stretch." CVS ScriptPath (designer Deborah Adler) turns a pile of separate prescriptions into one icon-driven medication schedule plus a redesigned label — the script artefact is a design surface, high-leverage for low-literacy/multilingual users. Mad*Pow's Behavior Change Design (target behaviour → modifiable determinants → matched techniques) is the professional version of COM-B/EAST. McKinsey frames medication synchronisation + home delivery as an adherence and relationship play, not just logistics. U7 S4 wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research

2.6 E-script / token rails and AI alongside humans

The single most transferable mechanism is the QR-code e-script token (SMS/WhatsApp/email), scanned at any pharmacy — Australia's token is a key that unlocks the script, forwardable to a carer, with a fresh token issued per repeat as a built-in anti-double-dispense control. On AI, BCG and Accenture converge on "alongside humans, never instead": consumers want to be "understood, not processed," and China's rules ban AI-generated prescriptions ("prescription first, drug second") — a defensible safety stance. U1 wiki/global-benchmarks/patterns-to-borrow-and-cautions wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research

2.7 Be honest about the gaps

Three honesty notes the vault insists on: ⚠️ gap BCG has no dedicated retail-pharmacy or scripting publication — borrow its personalisation + AI-with-human stance, expect no pharmacy journey. ⚠️ gap PwC and KPMG are macro-only; the widely-cited "Pharmacy Next" survey is Wolters Kluwer, not PwC (a common misattribution). ⚠️ UNVERIFIED consultancy figures rest on search extracts (WebFetch was 403-blocked) and vendor-reported adherence outcomes (~70% / ~50% lift) need primary verification before they enter a blueprint. And the central absence — the end-to-end scripting blueprint itself is white space. wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research

4 · Common themes — how the pillars recur

The synthesis payoff: the same handful of ideas surface independently across the strategy houses, the design firms, the global exemplars and the SA evidence. Where they converge, confidence is high.

  • Convenience is now the bar, and it's set outside pharmacy. Bain finds consumers expect more convenience (~65%) and responsiveness (~70%) than three years ago, with experience "marred by long waits and billing confusion." In SA, people benchmark pharmacy against Sixty60, Takealot and Capitec — not other pharmacies. The fix is omnichannel fulfilment + transparent money + never-guessing status. S3 U2 U6 wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research wiki/consumer-context/_index
  • The relationship, not the fill, is the unit of value. Deloitte's "beyond the pill," McKinsey's med-sync, frog's effectiveness frame and IDEO's pharmacist-forward store all reframe the single transaction as the entry to an ongoing care relationship — which in SA is the chronic flywheel courier pharmacies already monetise. S2 S4 U7 wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research wiki/dischem/competitive-landscape-sa-pharmacy
  • Automation serves the human, it doesn't replace them. Top-of-licence pharmacist (Deloitte/McKinsey/EY), AI-alongside-humans (BCG/Accenture), China's "prescription first, drug second," and the SA non-negotiable that safety/legality are invisible-not-absent — all say the same: machines absorb toil; the pharmacist stays the visible trust anchor with a guaranteed fallback. S5 U4 U5 wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research wiki/digital-transformation/telepharmacy-and-omnichannel
  • Transparency builds trust; opacity destroys it. Amazon/Cost Plus price-at-commit, the NHS App's real-time tracking, POPIA-grade consent and SA's SEP all converge on: show price and status before the user commits, and never spring a cost shock or a silent wait. SA's regulated pricing makes legible money easier here than in the US. U2 U3 S3 wiki/global-benchmarks/patterns-to-borrow-and-cautions wiki/sa-regulatory/sep-dispensing-fee
  • Inclusion is the design problem, not a feature. "This is for everyone" (NHS/GOV.UK), Smart Design's inclusive heritage, ScriptPath's low-literacy schedule, and MomConnect's USSD→SMS→WhatsApp ladder reaching ~5m mothers all point to the SA channel ladder and graceful degradation. Reducing enrolment friction (EY: only ~1 in 10 UK patients manage repeats digitally) is itself a growth and adherence lever. U1 U5 S1 wiki/ux-service-design/inclusive-low-bandwidth-design
  • Borrow the mechanism, reject the plumbing. Token/QR, nominated-pharmacy/active-script-list, auto-refill, and teleconsult→Rx→deliver loops transfer; the US PBM rebate layer, a single tax-funded rail, PIN-gated redemption, and density-dependent delivery promises do not. The recurring caution is: design an offline/degraded mode as non-negotiable (Germany's central rail became a single point of failure). U1 U4 U6 wiki/global-benchmarks/patterns-to-borrow-and-cautions

5 · What it means for Dis-Chem / SA

The global canon supplies the destination and the experience craft. SA adds the layer none of the firms carry: a regulated, multi-funder, low-bandwidth, emerging-market transaction. The reality check sharpens — and constrains — the opportunity.

5.2 The strategic opportunity

Dis-Chem's structural edge over pure couriers and supermarkets is the clinics + telehealth (Kena) + insurance ecosystem attached to a dense store + clinic estate (316 retail pharmacy stores, 540+ clinics, plus 280 TLC franchise pharmacies extending reach into small-town SA) — exactly the "front door" the strategy houses describe, and an asset Pharmacy Direct, Medipost and Medirite lack. The competitive read is blunt: the distance/chronic battle is won on scheme funding + a reliable proactive refill loop (couriers excel — Pharmacy Direct dispenses >1.2m scripts/month, Medipost ~730k, both with free recurring delivery; Dis-Chem's PackMyMeds is still opt-in/manual), while the acute/convenience battle is won on speed and reliability (Dis-Chem leads on the ~60-minute DeliverD but is undermined by fulfilment-reliability complaints). The play is to own both by closing the digital↔physical reliability gap and making chronic refills genuinely managed — proactive run-out alerts and synchronised repeats rather than a customer-initiated ask. S2 S4 wiki/dischem/competitive-landscape-sa-pharmacy

Concretely, the SA future-state should fuse the two camps the firms keep separate — the destination/front-door vision and the experience/adherence craft — and add the regulated-transaction layer: a token/QR e-script with USSD/WhatsApp/printed fallback; one profile across channels; legible money via real-time claims switching plus SEP; central-fill chronic medication released by remote pharmacist verification to lockers and courier; pharmacists redeployed to acute scripts and counselling; AI for triage and adherence support with a guaranteed pharmacist fallback; and the script artefact itself (ScriptPath-style schedule) designed for low-literacy, multilingual users. Every step is defensible from a pillar and a vault page. U1 U6 U7 wiki/global-benchmarks/patterns-to-borrow-and-cautions wiki/digital-transformation/telepharmacy-and-omnichannel

Open uncertainties to carry forward ⚠️: the SA-specific identity/consent model for an e-script is unresolved; whether to build a single national e-Rx rail or a federated/outage-tolerant one is an open architectural decision; SAPC acceptance of remote verification + unattended locker release is unconfirmed (likely 🔮 requires-reform); and exact insured share (15.8% self-report vs ~17.4% other bases) and chronic-courier market shares need primary verification. wiki/digital-transformation/telepharmacy-and-omnichannel wiki/consumer-context/two-economy-affordability
