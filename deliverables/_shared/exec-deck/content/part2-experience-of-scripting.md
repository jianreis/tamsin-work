## SLIDE: theme
id: trust-dignity-relationship
kicker: Experience · Theme A
title: Trust is the product — design the pharmacist relationship, not just the queue
so_what: The scripting moment is where dignity and trust are won or lost, so the human relationship is the thing to design for, not the throughput.
body:
  - Bring the pharmacist forward — out from behind the counter, with private consultation space.
  - Honour Picker's principles: information, emotional support, continuity, access, respect for preferences.
  - Design for the caregiver as a co-user — SA's proxy and household-collection norm is the default, not the edge case.
  - Discreet by default — "your order is ready", never "your ARV is ready".
evidence: [wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research, wiki/ux-service-design/healthcare-service-design-patterns, src-consult-ideo-walgreens-pharmacy, src-ux-picker-patient-centred-care]
flags: [unverified]
notes: IDEO pharmacist-forward / Walgreens Well Experience is the exemplar; frog and Picker's patient-centred principles back the frame. FLAG unverified — IDEO's ~750-store scale is extract-only; cite the mechanism (pharmacist forward, private rooms) as proven, the scale as unverified.

## SLIDE: theme
id: efficacy-effectiveness
kicker: Experience · Theme B
title: Adherence is a design problem, and the label is the design surface
so_what: A medicine only works if it is taken, so the experience after dispensing — and the artefact in the patient's hand — is where real-world value is created or lost.
body:
  - Experience is the gap between a drug's efficacy and its real-world effectiveness — adherence is that gap.
  - Packaging is one of the few adherence levers with meta-analytic support — 71% vs 63%, blister strongest.
  - It works best when pharmacy-delivered and contact-rich — packaging alone is necessary, not sufficient.
  - Treat the script label and schedule as a design surface, not a regulatory printout.
evidence: [wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research, wiki/dispensing-practice/adherence-packaging, src-disp-conn-packaging-meta, src-consult-frog-pharma-experience]
visual: dia:efficacy-effectiveness
flags: [unverified]
notes: frog's efficacy-to-effectiveness frame is the spine; Conn et al. meta-analysis (52 reports, ~22,858 subjects; 71% vs 63%; blister effect ~0.802) is the hard evidence. FLAG unverified — frog's ~70% adherence app result and Accenture/CCS ~50% lift are vendor-reported; cite the mechanism (design lifts adherence) as proven, the numbers as unverified.

## SLIDE: pillar
id: script-artefact
kicker: Experience · Theme C
name: CVS ScriptPath — the script artefact, redesigned
what: One icon-driven medication schedule plus a redesigned bottle label that replaces a pile of separate prescriptions with a single legible picture of when to take everything.
why: The label is the highest-leverage, lowest-cost adherence surface — and an icon-led, multilingual schedule is exactly what SA's low-literacy, polypharmacy population needs across paper, SMS, WhatsApp or app.
evidence: [wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research, src-consult-cvs-scriptpath-adler]
example: Designed by Deborah Adler (creator of Target's ClearRx); consolidates timing and dosing into easy-to-understand icons for polypharmacy patients and caregivers.
visual: dia:script-artefact
flags: []
notes: Transferable lesson — the artefact is designable; deliver it on whatever channel the patient has. Scale/outcome claims are company-stated (Tier B) but the design pattern is the load-bearing point, not a number.

## SLIDE: theme
id: pillpack-dose-sachets
kicker: Experience · Theme D
title: PillPack proves the dose, not the bottle, is the unit patients can manage
so_what: Organising medicines by the moment they are taken — not by the box they came in — is the adherence innovation worth borrowing, separate from the operating model.
body:
  - PillPack pre-sorts a patient's daily medicines into per-dose sachets labelled by date and time.
  - One roll replaces six bottles — the regimen, not the package, becomes the unit of management.
  - The mechanism is lawful in SA under unit-dose rules; the subscription pricing is not.
  - Pair packaging with pharmacist contact — the evidence rewards contact-rich, not packaging alone.
evidence: [wiki/global-benchmarks/amazon-pharmacy-pillpack-deepdive, src-gbl-amazon-pharmacy, src-disp-conn-packaging-meta]
visual: img:medicine-cabinet
flags: []
notes: Kept strictly to the EXPERIENCE/adherence innovation (date/time dose sachets) — deliberately NOT covering Part 3's market/operating-model material (RxPass economics, scale, PBM rails). PillPack was prototyped with IDEO, reinforcing the design lineage.
