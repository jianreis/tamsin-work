## SLIDE: theme
id: front-door
kicker: Direction of travel · Theme A
title: The pharmacy stops being a counter and becomes the front door to health
so_what: If the store is the entry point to a care relationship, the script is the first conversation, not the transaction to optimise away.
body:
  - Treat pharmacy services as a strategic asset, not a downstream dispensing function.
  - Dense store-plus-clinic estates become integrated health touchpoints - screening, coordination, behaviour change.
  - Roughly 40% of consumers want broader clinical services delivered via the pharmacy.
  - Dis-Chem already owns the estate the global thesis assumes you must build.

## SLIDE: theme
id: fulfilment-node
kicker: Direction of travel · Theme B
title: The store becomes a fulfilment node - one regimen, many channels
so_what: Winning means meeting the patient on whichever channel they choose, with the store as the omnichannel hub behind all of them.
body:
  - Medication synchronisation plus home delivery is an adherence and relationship play, not just logistics.
  - The convenience bar is set by retail - SA benchmarks pharmacy against Sixty60, not other pharmacies.
  - Digital repeat-management adoption is the untapped lever - only ~1 in 10 UK patients manage repeats digitally.
  - Store-as-node lets collect, deliver and locker coexist off one inventory and one record.
evidence: [wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research, src-consult-mckinsey-pharmacy-home, src-consult-bain-front-line-healthcare, src-consult-ey-pharmacy-innovation]
visual: dia:fulfilment-node
flags: []
notes: Firms behind it - McKinsey (med-sync + home delivery); Bain (retail-grade convenience/responsiveness expectations); EY (~1 in 10 UK manage repeats digitally - the adoption gap). Global only - direction of travel.

## SLIDE: theme
id: time-reallocation
kicker: Direction of travel · Theme C
title: Automate the toil so the pharmacist works at top-of-licence
so_what: Robots should absorb the counting and checking, and the scarce pharmacist hour should buy the clinical and trust work machines cannot.
body:
  - Automation and AI free pharmacists from counting pills to manage chronic disease and counsel.
  - The clinical-pharmacist vision is real only if you invest in the workforce behind it.
  - Exemplars exist at scale - roughly 70% of Danish community pharmacies already run dispensing robots.
  - Workforce fragility is the live risk - 81% of UK community pharmacists considered leaving in 2022.
evidence: [wiki/ux-service-design/consultancy-pharmacy-research, src-consult-deloitte-future-of-pharmacy, src-consult-ey-pharmacy-innovation, src-consult-mckinsey-retail-pharmacy-future]
visual: dia:time-reallocation
flags: []
notes: Firms behind it - Deloitte (automation frees clinical time); McKinsey (reallocate pharmacist time from counting to clinical); EY (~70% Danish robots; 81% UK pharmacists considered leaving 2022). Global only - direction of travel.

## SLIDE: theme
id: synthesis-direction-of-travel
kicker: Direction of travel · Synthesis
title: The firms agree on the destination - none has drawn the map for our market
so_what: We can borrow the vision wholesale, but the journey, the funding plumbing and the low-bandwidth reality are ours to design.
body:
  - Front door, fulfilment node and top-of-licence are one coherent picture - the strategic consensus.
  - It is almost entirely US/UK-framed and assumes single-payer or PBM plumbing we do not have.
  - No firm has published an end-to-end scripting blueprint - submit to adhere - for any market.
  - Read globals as direction of travel; the SA-specific layer is the defensible work.
